# Taxonomy — revised working draft (v5)

**Status.** Fifth iteration (v5). Anchored in all prior scans and units through the US academic research cycle (July 2026). Substantively extended with **four new cross-cutting tags** for maturity assessment, surfacing the multi-dimensional evaluation that the field guide's corpus has been doing implicitly.

**Supersedes:** v4 (`taxonomy/v4.md`). v1, v2, v3, and v4 retained as change-history anchors.

**Why v5 exists.** The maturity-assessment framework unit (`units/ai-maturity-assessment-framework.md`) made explicit what the corpus has been doing implicitly: a deployment's "maturity" is not a single value but a multi-dimensional assessment across *scale*, *verification*, *longevity*, and *translation*. The v4 schema collapses these into the single `activity-status` tag (`deployed | piloting | research | experimental | announced | discontinued`), which obscures substantive differences (e.g. DJI Agriculture S4/V0 vs. Bayer Climate FieldView S4/V1 vs. JoinData S3/V2). v5 promotes the four dimensions to first-class cross-cutting tags, so the corpus can be filtered and queried on each dimension independently.

## 1. Three primary axes (unchanged from v3, v4)

### Axis 1 — Sector position

1. inputs (seeds, fertilizers, agrochemicals)
2. on-farm production — open field (broadacre crops, fruits, vegetables)
3. on-farm production — protected (greenhouses, vertical farms, controlled-environment agriculture)
4. animal production (livestock, dairy, poultry)
5. post-harvest handling and storage
6. processing (food and feed processing, dairy processing, meat processing)
7. distribution and retail (wholesalers, grocers, restaurants)
8. consumption (consumer-facing, end-of-chain)
9. waste and recovery (food waste, surplus diversion, composting)

### Axis 2 — AI technique class

1. predictive ML (forecasting, classification, regression)
2. computer vision (image-based detection, classification, segmentation)
3. robotics — autonomy — ground (ground robots, autonomous tractors)
4. robotics — autonomy — aerial (drone spraying, aerial imaging)
5. generative AI / LLMs (foundation models, generative tools)
6. decision-support systems (rule-based or hybrid; not ML-first)
7. sensors and IoT ML (sensor fusion, edge ML)

### Axis 3 — Purpose

1. yield optimisation (productivity, output per unit input)
2. input reduction (herbicides, pesticides, fertilizers, water)
3. climate adaptation (resilience, drought response)
4. supply chain efficiency (logistics, traceability, demand forecasting)
5. worker conditions (labour displacement, occupational safety)
6. governance (data governance, traceability for regulatory purposes)
7. financial services / risk (insurance, credit, weather-indexed products)
8. market access (connecting producers to buyers, e-commerce)
9. food security / sovereignty (domestic production capacity, food safety)

## 2. Cross-cutting tags

The schema now has **fifteen** cross-cutting tags (up from eleven in v4). Four new tags are added in v5 for maturity assessment; the existing eleven tags remain unchanged.

### 2.1 Existing tags (eleven — unchanged)

- **claim-type:** example | claim | statistic | framework
- **activity-status:** deployed | piloting | research | experimental | announced | discontinued (kept as coarse single-value summary; new tags below provide the multi-dimension breakdown)
- **critical-voice:** food-sovereignty | indigenous-data-sovereignty | right-to-repair | digital-equity | (none directly — vendor framing)
- **capital-intensity:** smallholder | industrial | mixed | research
- **language-literacy-profile:** voice-first | low-literacy | standard-smartphone | (not applicable)
- **policy-instrument:** regulatory | fiscal | strategy | (none)
- **region:** NA-Canada | NA-US | EU-Netherlands | EU-France | EU-Germany | EU-other | East-Asia | LATAM | SSA | South-Asia | Oceania | Global | (multi-region)
- **actor:** named individual or organisational entity
- **actor-type:** vendor | state-agency | multilateral-body | academic | farmer-led | Indigenous-organisation | critical-civil-society | industry-association | farmer-cooperative | standards-body
- **data-governance:** open | proprietary | dark | mixed | state-stewarded | co-operative / commons
- **data-rights-framework:** farmer-owned | vendor-owned | state-stewarded | Indigenous-sovereign | co-operative | distributed | not applicable

### 2.2 New tags in v5 (four — maturity dimensions)

The maturity-assessment framework (`units/ai-maturity-assessment-framework.md`) defines four dimensions, each as a five-level ladder (S0-S4, V0-V4, L0-L4, T0-T4). Levels may be omitted where they don't apply (e.g. analytical / framework units).

#### `maturity-scale` — quantified deployment volume

**Values:**
- **S0** — no quantified deployment. Vendor / academic / institutional description only; no named figures.
- **S1** — pilot-scale deployment. Tens to low hundreds of units / acres / users.
- **S2** — early deployed. Hundreds to low thousands of units / acres / users; multi-region or single-region multi-partner.
- **S3** — deployed at scale. Tens of thousands to millions of units / acres / users; multi-region, multi-year.
- **S4** — global deployment. Hundreds of thousands to billions of units / acres / users; multi-continent, multi-decade.

#### `maturity-verification` — epistemic posture of the claim

**Values:**
- **V0** — vendor-reported only. Figure provided by the deploying vendor / institution; no external verification cited.
- **V1** — academic / institutional peer-reviewed. Peer-reviewed publication supports the figure (Plant Phenomics, AI Magazine, Computer and Electronics in Agriculture, etc.).
- **V2** — third-party case study. Reputable external organisation (Development Gateway, INRAE, Mozilla, IPES-Food, civil-society research) has produced a case study that names the figure.
- **V3** — independently audited. Figure independently audited by an accounting firm, government inspector general, or comparable oversight body.
- **V4** — replicated by independent party. Multiple independent parties have reproduced or verified the figure across different contexts.

#### `maturity-longevity` — operational durability

**Values:**
- **L0** — announced / not yet operational. Deployment announced but not in operational use.
- **L1** — first-generation operational. Deployed 0-2 years; first iteration; no generational update cycle yet.
- **L2** — multi-generation operational. Deployed 3-7 years; second or third iteration; vendor / institution stable.
- **L3** — mature / durable. Deployed 7-15 years; multi-iteration; survived market cycles; institutional memory.
- **L4** — generational / legacy. Deployed 15+ years; multiple vendor or institutional generations; established as operational infrastructure rather than product.

#### `maturity-translation` — research-to-deployment pathway

**Values:**
- **T0** — research output only. Peer-reviewed papers and grant-funded research; no documented operational adoption.
- **T1** — pilot deployment documented. At least one operational pilot / deployment named; figures may be small.
- **T2** — multiple operational deployments. Multiple named deployments across partners or regions; translation arms in place.
- **T3** — institutional adoption pathway. Cooperative Extension, USDA-NIFA translation program, or comparable institutional structure moves research into operational deployment.
- **T4** — self-sustaining translation. Operational deployments continue without ongoing academic / research-side involvement.

### 2.3 Tag omission rules

For units that are **analytical, framework, or critical** (claim-type: claim, framework), the maturity dimensions may be omitted or marked `(n/a — analytical)`. Examples from the framework unit's maturity-grade table:

- `farm-data-ownership-critical.md` — (n/a — analytical) on S, V2 on Verification (third-party references), L2 on Longevity (corpus-stable), (n/a) on Translation
- `dark-data-agrifood.md` — (n/a — analytical) on S, V1 on Verification (peer-reviewed), L2 on Longevity, (n/a) on Translation
- `usda-fy25-26-ai-strategy.md` — (n/a — strategy) on S, V2 on Verification, L2 on Longevity, T2 on Translation

## 3. Schema extension rationale

### 3.1 Why promote maturity dimensions

The corpus had been doing implicit multi-dimensional maturity assessment for some time — gap indexes G-015 (Agrosmart / Kilimo verification), G-020 (independent verification of vendor-reported input-reduction figures), G-033 (DJI 222M tons water / 30.87M tons CO2 verification) all engage with the verification dimension; G-046 (academic-to-deployment translation gap) engages with translation; the Indigo Ag unit's boom-bust framing engages with longevity. The maturity-assessment framework makes this implicit work explicit and queryable.

### 3.2 Why four dimensions, not one

A single "maturity" value would re-create the v4 `activity-status` problem at a different level of granularity. The four dimensions are *independent*: a deployment can be high on scale and low on verification (DJI Agriculture: S4, V0), or high on translation and low on scale (Cornell Atkinson IDSov pilot: T2, S0). Collapsing these into one value would obscure the substantive differences.

### 3.3 Why these specific dimensions

- **Scale** is the most visible vendor-figure claim and the most contested.
- **Verification** addresses the vendor-figure hygiene discipline that the field guide treats as primary.
- **Longevity** addresses the durability question (Indigo Ag's boom-bust; Climate FieldView's decade-plus deployment).
- **Translation** addresses the research-to-deployment gap that the academic-side corpus has surfaced and the Extension Foundation 2026 Report substantively acknowledges.

### 3.4 Why additive rather than restructuring

Same principle as v4's promotion of data-governance / data-rights-framework. The three primary axes remain about *what the AI does in the agrifood system*. Maturity is about *how substantively mature that work is*. Different ontological categories. The schema's three primary axes survive; maturity is a cross-cutting tag set.

## 4. v4 → v5 changes summary

### 4.1 What changed

- **Four new cross-cutting tags added:** `maturity-scale` (S0-S4), `maturity-verification` (V0-V4), `maturity-longevity` (L0-L4), `maturity-translation` (T0-T4).
- **All existing units have been maturity-graded** in `units/ai-maturity-assessment-framework.md`'s table; units should be retro-tagged with the new optional fields as they are updated on their normal refresh cycle.
- **Three new gaps added:** G-050 (independent verification of vendor-reported agrifood AI scale claims), G-051 (research-to-deployment pathway documentation), G-052 (longevity / generational durability).
- **Two new contested claims added:** C-041 (academic agrifood AI research translates to operational deployment), C-042 (vendor-reported agrifood AI scale figures are reliable).

### 4.2 What did not change

- Three primary axes unchanged.
- Eleven existing cross-cutting tags unchanged.
- Existing `activity-status` tag retained as coarse single-value summary; the four new maturity tags provide the multi-dimension breakdown.
- Unit frontmatter schema extended with four new optional fields.
- Source attribution / freshness model unchanged.

## 6. What v5 enables

1. **Filter by verification level.** Talks about "vendor-reported agrifood AI" can now query `maturity-verification: V0` and surface the 9+ high-scale vendor-reported figures for honest citation. Talks about "independently verified deployment" can query `maturity-verification: V2` and surface JoinData, Mozilla, INRAE-backed units.
2. **Filter by translation level.** Academic audiences can query `maturity-translation: T2` to surface research-stage work with documented translation; `T3` to surface institutional adoption pathways (USDA-NIFA, Cooperative Extension, La Ferme Digitale).
3. **Filter by longevity.** Critical-voice audiences can query `maturity-longevity: L1` and surface Indigo Ag's boom-bust; `L4` and surface multi-decade legacy deployments.
4. **Cross-corpus comparison at maturity-grain.** JoinData (S3 / V2 / L3 / T3) vs. NAPDC (S0 / V0 / L1 / T1) vs. Bayer Climate FieldView (S4 / V1 / L4 / T4) — same conceptual space (data infrastructure), different maturity profiles. The v5 schema supports this comparison.
5. **Pilot vs scale differentiation.** Vendor pilot announcements can be flagged `maturity-scale: S1` rather than `deployed`. The two tags together (`activity-status` for single-value summary, `maturity-scale` for quantified volume) are honest about pilot-vs-scale distinctions.
6. **Vendor-figure hygiene at the corpus level.** Per memory hygiene discipline, vendor-reported figures circulate without independent verification. v5 makes this visible at the schema level, not just case-by-case.

## 7. Schema pressure and v6 considerations

Three observations emerging from the v5 work:

1. **The `maturity-scale` and `maturity-translation` dimensions may evolve into their own primary axes.** If the corpus grows to include substantially more pilot-scale and research-stage work, the maturity breakdown may become a primary structural distinction. Not in scope for v5.
2. **`maturity-longevity` is the dimension with the sparsest current data.** Most academic units are L1-L2; vendor units are L2-L4; longevity requires multi-year operational data. Future corpus growth will populate this dimension.
3. **`maturity-translation` is the dimension where the academic / practitioner gap is most visible.** 6 academic units score T1; 5 score T2; 1 (INRAE) scores T2; none score T3. This is consistent with the Extension Foundation 2026 Report's workforce-readiness-as-limit finding. Worth surfacing in any talk about academic agrifood AI.

These observations don't trigger v6 schema changes but inform what to watch.
