# Taxonomy — revised working draft (v4)

**Status.** Fourth iteration (v4). Anchored in the initial scan, two regional scans (Canada/China/Global South; Latin America / Caribbean), the NA/EU industry scan, the France cycle, the FCC deepening, the Quebec cycle, the China deepening, the data cycle, and the data cooperatives / commons deepening. Substantively extended with **two new cross-cutting tags** surfacing the data substrate.

**Supersedes:** v3 (`taxonomy/v3.md`). v1 and v2 retained as change-history anchors.

**Why v4 exists.** The data cycle (scan + four units, July 2026) and the data cooperatives / commons deepening (four units, July 2026) foregrounded the *data substrate* layer as cross-cutting to all agrifood AI deployment. The v4 schema promotes this layer to first-class status by adding two new cross-cutting tags: `data-governance` and `data-rights-framework`. The promotion is additive (does not break existing units) — every prior v3 unit can be retro-tagged without altering its primary matrix position.

## 1. Three primary axes (unchanged from v3)

### Axis 1 — Sector position

The agrifood value chain, broken into nine cells:

1. inputs (seeds, fertilizers, agrochemicals)
2. on-farm production — open field (broadacre crops, fruits, vegetables)
3. on-farm production — protected (greenhouses, vertical farms, controlled-environment agriculture)
4. animal production (livestock, dairy, poultry)
5. post-harvest handling and storage
6. processing (food and feed processing, dairy processing, meat processing)
7. distribution and retail (wholesalers, grocers, restaurants)
8. consumption (consumer-facing, end-of-chain)
9. waste and recovery (food waste, surplus diversion, composting)

A unit can occupy multiple cells (e.g., the SoraLINK × Saputo/Olymel/Agropur unit occupies primarily `processing` but also spans `on-farm` and `distribution` at a smaller scale).

### Axis 2 — AI technique class

1. predictive ML (forecasting, classification, regression)
2. computer vision (image-based detection, classification, segmentation)
3. robotics — autonomy — ground (ground robots, autonomous tractors)
4. robotics — autonomy — aerial (drone spraying, aerial imaging)
5. generative AI / LLMs (foundation models, generative tools)
6. decision-support systems (rule-based or hybrid; not ML-first)
7. sensors and IoT ML (sensor fusion, edge ML)

### Axis 3 — Purpose

1. yield optimisation (productivity, output per unit input)
2. input reduction (herbicides, pesticides, fertilizers, water)
3. climate adaptation (resilience, drought response)
4. supply chain efficiency (logistics, traceability, demand forecasting)
5. worker conditions (labour displacement, occupational safety)
6. governance (data governance, traceability for regulatory purposes)
7. financial services / risk (insurance, credit, weather-indexed products)
8. market access (connecting producers to buyers, e-commerce)
9. food security / sovereignty (domestic production capacity, food safety)

A unit can occupy multiple purposes.

## 2. Cross-cutting tags

The schema now has **eleven** cross-cutting tags (up from eight in v3). Two new tags are added in v4.

### 2.1 Existing tags (eight — unchanged)

- **claim-type:** example | claim | statistic | framework
- **activity-status:** deployed | piloting | research | experimental
- **critical-voice:** food-sovereignty | indigenous-data-sovereignty | right-to-repair | digital-equity | (none directly — vendor framing)
- **capital-intensity:** smallholder | industrial | mixed | research
- **language-literacy-profile:** voice-first | low-literacy | standard-smartphone | (not applicable)
- **policy-instrument:** regulatory | fiscal | strategy | (none)
- **region:** NA-Canada | NA-US | EU-Netherlands | EU-France | EU-Germany | EU-other | East-Asia | LATAM | SSA | South-Asia | Oceania | Global | (multi-region)
- **actor:** named individual or organisational entity
- **actor-type:** vendor | state-agency | multilateral-body | academic | farmer-led | Indigenous-organisation | critical-civil-society | industry-association | farmer-cooperative | standards-body

### 2.2 New tags in v4 (two)

#### `data-governance` — the data-flow / openness posture

**Values:**
- **open** — public, FAIR-compliant, accessible without restriction. Examples: SoilGrids, Copernicus Sentinel, USDA Ag Data Commons, CGIAR open data.
- **proprietary** — held by vendor or aggregator, controlled access. Examples: Climate FieldView, John Deere Operations Center, AGCO PTx Trimble.
- **dark** — collected but not surfaced for broader use. Examples: IoT sensor data collected but aggregated only at vendor level; machine telemetry aggregated in vendor platforms.
- **mixed** — combination of openness levels in the same deployment. Examples: HAVEN Greens (closed-loop proprietary farm + open consumer retail integration); Mila DISA (uses open satellite data + proprietary ML model); JoinData (cooperative platform with proprietary vendor integrations).
- **state-stewarded** — held by government agencies, accessible with public-interest governance. Distinct from `open` in that stewardship is governmental rather than multilateral. Examples: Statistics Canada, USDA NASS, MARA outlooks.
- **co-operative / commons** — collectively managed by farmers or member organisations. Examples: JoinData platform, NAPDC / Ag Data Coop development, OADA standards.

(Values can be combined where a deployment spans multiple data flows.)

#### `data-rights-framework` — who has rights over the data

**Values:**
- **farmer-owned** — contractual statement that farmers own their data; operationally complex. Examples: Climate FieldView (statement), John Deere Operations Center (Ag Data Transparent certified).
- **vendor-owned** — vendor controls aggregation, anonymised pools, derived model outputs. Examples: Indigo Ag, Climate FieldView aggregation, Taranis.
- **state-stewarded** — public-interest data held by government or multilateral body. Examples: USDA Ag Data Commons, Copernicus Sentinel, FAOSTAT.
- **Indigenous-sovereign** — Indigenous community data sovereignty; CARE Principles. Examples: Indigenous Navigator Peru, AGUAPAN, Mila FLAIR (Indigenous languages).
- **co-operative** — collectively owned / controlled by farmer cooperative. Examples: JoinData, FrieslandCampina dairy data, NAPDC development.
- **distributed** — open-source standards / non-commercial / farmer-controlled. Examples: OADA, Ag Data Transparent certification (industry-led but transparency-focused).
- **not applicable** — for framework units that span multiple rights frameworks.

A unit can have multiple values where rights are mixed.

## 3. Schema extension rationale

### 3.1 Why promote the data layer

The data cycle and data cooperatives / commons deepenings surfaced that **the data substrate is at least as important as the AI model layer**. The competitive dynamics and value capture are increasingly in the *data layer*, not the *model layer*. Without the two new tags:

- Content units could not be filtered by data-governance posture (open vs proprietary vs dark).
- Talks about data sovereignty could not be assembled from the field guide by querying the data-rights-framework tag.
- The dark-data problem (the inequality problem in agrifood AI) could not be surfaced as a queryable dimension.

### 3.2 Why additive rather than restructuring

Two options were considered:
- **Additive** (chosen): add the two new tags as cross-cutting dimensions. Existing units are retro-tagged without altering their primary matrix position.
- **Restructuring** (rejected): promote data-substrate to a fourth primary axis. This would require re-tagging all 48+ units in a way that conflates sector position / AI class / purpose with data posture, making the schema less navigable.

The additive approach is cleaner. Talks can filter by data governance or data rights framework without losing the primary matrix positioning.

### 3.3 Why not promote data to a primary axis

The primary axes are about *what the AI does in the agrifood system* (where, how, why). The data layer is about *the substrate the AI operates on*. Different ontological categories. The schema's three primary axes survive; data is a cross-cutting tag set.

If the data layer becomes more central in talks, v5 could promote data to a primary axis. Worth considering but not in scope for v4.

## 6. What v4 enables

1. **Filtering by data posture.** Talks about "open data infrastructure in agrifood" can now query `data-governance: open` and get the substantive cells (SoilGrids, Copernicus, Ag Data Commons).
2. **Filtering by rights framework.** Talks about "Indigenous data sovereignty" can now query `data-rights-framework: Indigenous-sovereign` and surface CARE-aligned units.
3. **Surface dark data as a queryable dimension.** Talks about the inequality problem in agrifood AI can now query `data-governance: dark`.
4. **Cooperative / commons aggregation.** Talks about alternative models can now query `data-rights-framework: co-operative` or `data-governance: co-operative / commons`.
5. **Cross-cutting analysis.** A talk could query `data-governance: proprietary AND capital-intensity: smallholder` to surface deployments that bridge smallholder agriculture with proprietary data infrastructure (a meaningfully distinct analytical category).

## 7. Schema pressure and v5 considerations

Three observations emerging from the v4 work:

1. **The `actor-type` tag now exercises 9 of 10 values.** farmer-led remains under-populated. Worth surfacing for the next iteration.

2. **`policy-instrument` is mostly empty / `not applicable`.** Most units don't engage with policy instruments as a primary dimension. The FCC / Root AI / China Smart Agriculture Action Plan / IVADO units are exceptions.

3. **The `claim-type: statistic` value is now substantively populated** by the FCC Canada AI adoption unit (1.8% / 12.2%) and the China import signal unit (26.2% decline). The earlier schema-comment that "statistic" might be under-used has been resolved by v4 era.

These observations don't trigger v5 schema changes but inform what to watch in the next iteration.
