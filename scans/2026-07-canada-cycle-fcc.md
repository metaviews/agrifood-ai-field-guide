## 1. Farm Credit Canada — the institutional anchor

Farm Credit Canada (FCC) is a federal Crown corporation, governed by the Farm Credit Canada Act, reporting to Parliament through the Minister of Agriculture and Agri-Food but operating at arm's length. As Canada's leading agricultural lender, FCC holds a unique institutional position: a Crown corp that reinvests profits, with the financial firepower to act as both lender and (through FCC Capital) investor.

**FCC's own framing of its AI relevance.** From the FCC Thought Leadership piece (May 2026): "AI in agriculture is no longer experimental; it is delivering measurable value across the sector" (quoted from Deloitte partner) and "FCC has announced a $2 billion investment to advance ag-tech innovations" and "FCC's own Root AI and AgExpert show how trusted, accessible digital solutions can support better farm management and stronger decision-making." FCC is positioning itself as both *funder* (through FCC Capital) and *operator* (through AgExpert, FCC Root AI).

**The 1.8% / 12.2% gap — the most concrete Canadian adoption figure we have.** From the FCC / Deloitte Canada report (July 2026):

> As of the second quarter of 2025, only 1.8 per cent of Canadian agricultural businesses were using AI, compared to 12.2 per cent across other industries; and only 61 per cent of agriculture, forestry, fishing and hunting enterprises have adopted advanced technologies, with the sector ranking 9th out of 12 industries.

Canada ranks 25th globally in private investment in agricultural R&D. AI adoption in Canadian agriculture "is not constrained by technology availability, but more by systemic weaknesses" — fragmented digital infrastructure with limited rural connectivity, talent shortages, capital constraints, and historically unclear governance frameworks.

**FCC's specific framing of the gap.** "Leading countries have advanced more rapidly through coordinated investment, strong public-private collaboration, and clear policy direction. Without action, adoption will remain fragmented and Canada risks falling further behind global competitors." (Darren Baccus, EVP, Agri-Food, Alliances and FCC Capital.) FCC is positioning itself as the *convener* that can address these ecosystem constraints.

This is a more sophisticated and self-aware framing than typical institutional AI advocacy. FCC explicitly names *ecosystem factors* (connectivity, talent, capital, governance) as the binding constraint, not technology. The field guide should treat this framing seriously even as it remains vendor / institutional.

## 2. FCC Capital — the investment arm

FCC Capital, launched 2024, is FCC's investment vehicle. The headline commitment: **$2 billion by 2030 to advance agtech innovation in Canada's agriculture and food industry.**

**Inaugural year (2024-2025):**
- 9 direct investment deals totaling $170 million.
- 3 new fund investments.
- 1 new business accelerator added to portfolio.

**Why this matters for talks.** FCC Capital is the largest Canadian-specific institutional commitment to agrifood AI investment as of mid-2026. The $2B by 2030 commitment dwarfs the cited 2023 Canadian agtech VC total of ~$270 million. The structural question is: where do those investments land — in input-vendor data layers (Bayer pattern), equipment-vendor lock-in (Deere pattern), independent platforms (xFarm / Agrosmart pattern), or farmer-led models (Atelier Paysan pattern)? The unit should track FCC Capital's portfolio composition over time.

**FCC Capital Investment Coalition.** FCC has also brought together an investment coalition of over 20 organizations pledging to deploy $5 billion into agricultural and food innovation by 2030. Specific member organisations not all named in our sources.

## 3. AgExpert — FCC's farm management software

AgExpert is FCC's farm management software platform, 25,000+ users, 6.5M acres under management. Two named products: AgExpert Accounting (financial management) and AgExpert Field (crop / field management).

**Recent AI capability additions.** FCC has begun rolling out AI functionality to AgExpert. The Western Producer piece mentions "FCC says it will allow farmers to: Quickly build a personalized business plan. Track weather predictions for the crop season. Analyze images to [identify issues]." A "Root AI" product was named in FCC's July 2026 release as a separate FCC product for producer-facing AI assistance.

**AIVA validation project with AgExpert.** EMILI is validating *AgExpert Field's Sustainability Regional Benchmarking software* across the AIVA validation hubs in 2026 to "understand how insights into soil and crop health can support good crop management decisions." Worth noting because it's a concrete AI-validation project on FCC's own product.

## 4. AIVA Network — the validation infrastructure

AIVA (Agriculture Innovation, Validation, and Adoption Network) is a network founded in May 2026 by FCC, EMILI, and the Wabash Heartland Innovation Network (WHIN). Launched May 12, 2026.

**AIVA's stated model.** "Farmer-centric model that rigorously vets early-stage and market-ready technologies on Canadian farms, ensuring that innovators are providing farmers with agtech that solves real-world challenges." Technologies tested in real-world conditions; those demonstrating results may be recognised as AIVA Technology Partners.

**Concrete figures (AIVA, May 2026):**
- 9 multi-site collaboration projects in 2026 growing season.
- 23 Farmer Alliance Members representing 235,000+ acres in Saskatchewan and Manitoba (initial regions; expanding nationally).
- 4 validation hubs across Canada.

**Validation hubs:**
1. EMILI Innovation Farms — Manitoba
2. Olds College — Alberta and Saskatchewan
3. Area X.O — Ottawa, Ontario
4. Innovation Farms Ontario — near Bothwell, Ontario

**AI-relevant projects validated in 2026:**
- **Geco Strategic Weed Management AI** — AI software predicting weed zones including herbicide-resistant patches
- **Corteva Agriscience spray drone trials** — fungicide application via drone, efficacy vs ground-based
- **Picketa Systems LENS™** — in-field tissue scanning for plant nutrients
- **Miraterra** — Gabe AI chatbot for field management and soil sampling decisions
- **AgExpert Field Sustainability Regional Benchmarking software** — soil and crop health insights
- **FarmerTitan** — named but not detailed in our sources

**The Health Canada engagement.** Area X.O and Corteva met with Health Canada in June 2026 to discuss spray drone regulations. Notable because it shows AIVA as not just validation infrastructure but also as a *regulatory conversation conduit* — bridging agtech innovators and federal regulators.

## 6. What the FCC cycle asks of the field guide

Three observations:

1. **The 1.8% / 12.2% figure is the load-bearing Canadian adoption data point.** Should appear in any talk about Canadian agrifood AI. Source: FCC / Deloitte Canada, July 2026.

2. **FCC's institutional framing is more nuanced than typical vendor advocacy.** The "ecosystem not technology" framing is worth engaging with, even where the field guide has critical lenses to apply. FCC is naming structural barriers; the field guide's critical voice should engage with the structural question rather than dismissing the framing.

3. **AIVA is a concrete Canadian contribution to global AI validation practice.** The validation hub model with farmer alliance members and standardised testing frameworks is meaningfully distinct from US/EU agtech validation infrastructure. Worth its own unit.
