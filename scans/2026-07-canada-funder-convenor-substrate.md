# Canada Funder/Convenor Substrate Cycle — July 2026

**Status.** First of three Canada-deepening scans (the other two are value-chain scan and regional substrate scan, both pending). Focus: the **funder + convenor substrate** — the federal + provincial + cluster + RDA infrastructure that funds and convenes Canadian agrifood AI. This is the *backbone* of the Canadian picture: every regional deployment or vendor unit that follows will cite at least one funder in this scan.

**Scope.** CAAIN (Canadian Agri-Food Automation and Intelligence Network, federal ISED + AAFC); Scale AI (federal $284M Global Innovation Cluster); Digital / DIGITAL Technology Supercluster (BC-anchored Global Innovation Cluster, now extended nationally); Protein Industries Canada (PIC) AI Programme ($30M under PCAIS, ended March 2026); Regional Artificial Intelligence Initiative (RAII, Budget 2024 $200M / 5 years, *AI for All* June 2026 scales to $500M, delivered by seven regional development agencies).

**Why this scan now.** The existing Canada scans name these actors in passing (FCC cycle §5 actor list, regional scan §6 actor list) but have **zero dedicated units** for the funder/convenor substrate. The substrate is **structurally distinct** from the EU's (which is multi-institutional and DG-distributed) and from the US private-venture pattern (which is sector-fund dominated). Canada's substrate is *federally orchestrated + regionally delivered + supercluster-channeled* — a third model worth naming in its own right.

**Method.** Primary sources for every load-bearing claim. Where the funder has a public projects database, enumerated directly from the source. Where the funder has only narrative reporting, summarized with explicit naming of the source.

**Cutoff.** Active in the last 12 months (July 2025 – July 2026). Older instruments included only when load-bearing.

## 1. The five-funder system

The Canadian agrifood AI funder substrate in mid-2026 comprises **five distinct federal channels**, layered on top of the FCC-led federal Crown corporation substrate documented in the existing Canada FCC cycle.

| Funder | Type | Federal funding | Focus | Window | Canadian agrifood-AI signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **CAAIN** (Canadian Agri-Food Automation and Intelligence Network) | Federal third-party delivery + AAFC Clean Technology programme | ~$19M+ CAAIN contribution against $52M+ total project value (visible projects only) | Automation, robotics, data-driven decision-making, validation/demonstration | Active 2020–present | 35+ projects; ~17 currently on public listing. Multiple open calls in 2026. |
| **Scale AI** (Global Innovation Cluster) | Federal Innovation Canada cluster | Up to $284M | AI for supply chain resilience | Active 2018–2028 (extended) | 13 named Agriculture & Mining projects |
| **Digital / DIGITAL Technology Supercluster** | Federal Innovation Canada cluster (BC-anchored) | Multi-hundred-million | Cross-sector digital technology | Active 2018–2028 (extended) | 20+ Natural Resources & Agriculture projects |
| **Protein Industries Canada AI Programme** | Federal Innovation Canada cluster (PIC-delivered, PCAIS-funded) | $30M committed under Pan-Canadian AI Strategy (PCAIS) delivery mandate | Plant-protein sector AI | September 2022 – March 2026 (programme closed) | 7 projects total (5 active + 1 development + 1 completed by 2025–26) |
| **Regional Artificial Intelligence Initiative (RAII)** | Federal ISED, delivered by 7 regional development agencies | $200M over 5 years (Budget 2024); *AI for All* June 2026 scales to $500M | AI productization + AI adoption across priority sectors including agriculture | Active 2024–25 onward | Named agrifood recipients across all 7 RDA regions |

This is **five federal channels** stacked on the **FCC Crown corporation substrate** (FCC Capital $2B + AIVA Network + AgExpert + Root AI — existing scan). The substrate is *federally orchestrated* and *not provincial-led* in the same way that Australian state-led or German Bundesland-led substrates are. Distinct Canadian pattern.

### 1.1 What this means structurally

The five channels have **distinct project-selection logics**:

- **CAAIN** — third-party delivery. Project Selection Committees (independent). Active calls with rolling intake in 2026. Up to $1M per project pre-approval in the Clean Agtech stream.
- **Scale AI** — co-investment. Industry-led projects. Co-investment matched to cluster programme funding.
- **Digital** — co-investment. Industry-led projects across multiple streams (Natural Resources & Agriculture is one).
- **PIC** — programme-end as of March 2026. **No new AI calls in 2025–26.** Three streams: Projects, Systems, Community.
- **RAII** — RDA-delivered. Two pillars: AI productization/commercialization + AI adoption. Dates for opening of application intake vary by RDA.

**Three of the five** are Global Innovation Clusters (Scale AI, Digital, PIC). The fourth is third-party delivery (CAAIN). The fifth is RDA-delivered (RAII). All five draw from the same federal budget envelope but operate with **different selection logics, different timeline cadences, and different size scales per project**.

The contrast with the EU: the EU's main agrifood AI funding channels (Horizon Europe Cluster 6, EIT Food, Digital Europe Programme, EU Mission Soil, CAP Strategic Plans) are **DG-distributed + co-funded**. Canada's are **federally orchestrated + single-window per cluster + cluster-co-investment**. The Canadian pattern is more concentrated; the EU pattern is more distributed.

The contrast with the US: the US private-venture pattern is sector-fund + foundation-model-vendor dominated. The federal channel (USDA NIFA, NSF) is research-oriented but small relative to private capital. Canada's federal channels are **much larger than USDA NIFA's agrifood AI spend**, which is why Canada has a federal convenor substrate that the US structurally lacks.

## 2. CAAIN — Canadian Agri-Food Automation and Intelligence Network

### 2.1 What CAAIN is (verified)

The **Canadian Agri-Food Automation and Intelligence Network (CAAIN)** was launched in 2020 with mandate "automation and robotics, data-driven decision-making, and validation and demonstration of emerging agricultural technology." Funding provided by:
- **Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada (ISED)** via the Strategic Innovation Fund.
- **Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada (AAFC)** via the **Clean Technology Program – Research and Innovation Stream–Accelerator** (most recently the 2026 Clean Agtech Validation and Integration Program).

**Active 2026 funding calls** (per CAAIN Funding Calls page, May 2026):
- **Clean Agtech Validation and Integration Program** — Application opens May 20 2026; PAF intake June 18, August 14, October 15 2026; project completion March 31 2028. **CAAIN invests $3.8M.** Successful applicants pre-approved up to **$1M CAD** per project. TRL focus: 7+ → 8 or 9 (validation/demonstration through adoption). Eligibility: ≥2 Canadian SMEs; cash contributions from at least two SMEs; Canadian operations.
- **2026 CAAIN Open Competition** — Application opens March 23 2026. **Rolling intake with 4 deadlines: April 3, June 5, August 7, October 9 2026. CAAIN commits up to $9M CAD.**
- **Prairie Agtech Validation Program** — Alberta-anchored, Prairie-based agtech companies and Alberta producers.

CAAIN has invested in **35+ projects since 2020**, with $19.1M+ CAAIN contribution against $52M+ total project value (only what's surfaced on public project pages — actual figure is higher). Project selection by independent Project Selection Committees.

### 2.3 The CAAIN portfolio's distinctive structural features

Three structural patterns worth naming:

1. **Founder / operator demographic.** Many CAAIN project leads are *second-generation Canadian agtech founders* whose families are named in the project narrative — Van de Pol (CATTLEytics), Pawluczyk (P&P Optica), Baresich (Haggerty AgRobotics), Bergen (mode40), Zeng (Super GeoAI), Ngadi (MatrixSpec, originally Nigeria via Government of Canada scholarship). The CAAIN programme thus surfaces a specific Canadian founder pattern: *immigrant or multi-generation agricultural family × technical training × federal funding as the leveller that commercial banks won't provide for "theoretical products"*.

2. **Federal funding fills the bank-financing gap.** Multiple project leads (mode40's Cameron Bergen; Haggerty AgRobotics' Chuck Baresich, who previously worked at FCC) explicitly say "financial institutions don't like lending for a theoretical product… CAAIN is just the opposite." The CAAIN funding model is structurally *counter-cyclical* to venture capital and bank lending for early-stage agtech — it sits between government-grant and Series A.

3. **Smart farm validation infrastructure.** CAAIN's mandate is "validation and demonstration of emerging agricultural technology" — it operates through the Pan-Canadian Smart Farm Network (already mentioned in existing scans; AIVA Network's four hubs are: EMILI Innovation Farms Manitoba; Olds College Smart Farm Alberta; Area X.O Ottawa; Innovation Farms Ontario). The CAAIN funding model is *physically-validated* — projects must be deployable on a smart farm — which is structurally distinct from software-only AI accelerator models.

The unit would compress the 35+ projects into a single navigable corpus cell. ### 5.2 PIC AI programme structure (verified)

Three streams:
1. **Projects** — individual SMEs incorporating AI as a core business function. Example: "Use AI to improve processing efficiency, reduce labour costs, improve food safety."
2. **Systems** — multi-partner sector competitiveness. Project selection by independent Project Selection Committee, with an additional data-feasibility study to ensure companies are prepared to work with AI technology.

## 6. Regional Artificial Intelligence Initiative (RAII)

### 6.1 What RAII is (verified)

The **Regional Artificial Intelligence Initiative (RAII)** — Budget 2024 allocated **$200M over 5 years** across Canada's **seven regional development agencies (RDAs)**. *AI for All* (June 4 2026) scales RAII to **$500M** ("a $500-million national investment to expand and enhance the Regional Artificial Intelligence Initiative, delivered by Canada's regional development agencies").

Two streams:
1. **AI productization and commercialization** — helps AI startups scale from lab prototypes to real-world applications (securing growth capital, hiring, data management, regulatory navigation).
2. **Adoption of AI applications across critical sectors** — addresses the AI adoption lag among Canadian businesses; targets SME skills gaps, integration complexities, and compliance with regulatory/industry standards.

Priority sectors include agriculture.

### 7.3 What the substrate does NOT do

4. **No FCC AgExpert data-licensing public framework.** AgExpert has 25,000 users and 6.5M acres; the data-licensing terms are private. G-316.
5. **RAII's "TBD" funding shares for non-Prairie regions.** The Open Canada Grants portal enumerates recipients but the budget allocation by region beyond PrairiesCan's $33.8M has not been publicly disclosed in primary sources. **Gap in transparency.** G-317.

## 9. Substantive contested claims and gaps

### 9.1 New contested claims

- **C-278 (new):** "CAAIN is the principal Canadian agrifood AI funder." Counter: Scale AI + Digital + PIC + RAII together fund more agrifood AI projects in dollar terms (CAAIN ~$19M+ vs Scale AI $284M cap × Agriculture share). CAAIN's distinction is *automation/robotics/data-driven decision-making focus* + *physical-validation requirement* (Pan-Canadian Smart Farm Network). Worth tracking.
- **C-279 (new):** "Federal AI strategy is sufficient substrate for Canadian agrifood AI adoption." Counter: AI for All (June 2026) is adoption-led, not regulation-led (constraint/critical scout §1.3). Adoption without regulatory guardrails is not the same as adoption with public-interest oversight. Plus the binding constraint is rural connectivity (Universal Broadband Fund non-renewal). Worth tracking.
- **C-280 (new):** "Cluster programmes are independent." Counter: at least 5 named leads (Vivid Machines, Terramera, Agi3, Verge Ag, EarthDaily) appear in *both* Scale AI and Digital. Same companies, multiple federal funding streams. Substantively, Canada's cluster architecture allows stacking.
- **C-281 (new):** "RAII is uniformly available across regions." Counter: PrairiesCan $33.8M is the only RDA funding share publicly disclosed in primary sources; the Open Canada Grants portal shows named recipients across regions but not by-region totals. Worth flagging as a transparency gap.
- **C-282 (new):** "PIC AI Programme is ongoing." Counter: PIC Corporate Plan (March 2025) confirms "there will be no new calls for projects in 2025–26" and programme ends March 2026. As of July 2026, PIC AI is *closed*. Substantively the question is what comes next — and what the federal AI for All $500M scaling means for a successor plant-protein AI stream.

### 9.2 New gaps

- **G-313 (new):** No federal or provincial programme specifically funds Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployment. CanNor REGI-AI funded an Inuit economic-development AI tool, not an agrifood AI tool. PolArctic Sanikiluaq (Inuit-led mariculture AI, parallel scout §11A.1) is one operational case; Salmon Vision (Indigenous-led BC wild salmon AI, parallel scout §11A.1) is another. Both are *operational*, not *funder-supported*. Worth tracking.
- **G-314 (new):** No federal farm-data-governance programme adopted. EMILI/CADI guidebooks are industry-led. UK Farm Data Principles certification recommended (CAPI/EMILI ISED submission) but not adopted.
- **G-315 (new):** No CSA-equivalent or equivalent safety standard for agrifood AI systems in Canada. Health Canada cellular agriculture novel-food guidance is the closest sector-specific AI-adjacent federal posture.
- **G-316 (new):** No FCC AgExpert data-licensing public framework. AgExpert has 25,000 users and 6.5M acres; the data-licensing terms are private contract terms.
- **G-317 (new):** RAII funding shares by RDA (other than PrairiesCan's $33.8M) not publicly disclosed.
- **G-318 (new):** No federal funder channel covers post-consumer food waste AI specifically across CAAIN / Scale AI / Digital / PIC / RAII. Worth a future cycle scan focused on waste-and-recovery matrix cell + funder substrate.

## 11. What this scan asks of the field guide

1. **The funder/convenor substrate is its own substantive corpus cell.** Five federal channels × 35+ CAAIN projects × 13 Scale AI Agriculture projects × 20+ Digital projects × 7 PIC AI projects × RAII × 7 RDAs. This is the most concentrated, federally-orchestrated agrifood AI substrate in any jurisdiction the corpus tracks. Distinct from the EU's DG-distributed substrate and the US private-venture substrate.

2. **Five funder units are warranted** (one per channel): CAAIN portfolio, Scale AI Agriculture, Digital Supercluster Agriculture, PIC AI Programme, RAII. Each compresses a substantive channel into a navigable corpus cell. The CAAIN portfolio unit alone is **the largest single uncovered substantive content surface** in the Canadian agrifood AI picture (27 named project leads, $19M+ CAAIN contribution, $52M+ total project value, 35+ projects).

3. **Cross-cluster funding is a Canadian structural feature.** At least 5 leads appear in both Scale AI and Digital. Worth naming as a corpus-level observation, not just a per-unit note.

4. **The RAII / Universal Broadband Fund tension is the binding constraint dynamic.** RAII is the federal AI SME funding layer that scales ($200M → $500M); UBF is the rural connectivity layer that doesn't renew (May 2026). For rural AI adoption to work, both must scale together. RAII's scale-up is good news for agrifood AI in regional Canada; UBF's non-renewal is the binding constraint.

5. **The PIC AI programme's closure (March 2026) is a substantive observation.** No new AI calls in 2025–26. The question of what comes next for plant-protein AI is open and worth tracking in any future Canadian cycle.

6. **Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployment remains framework-supported, not deployment-funded.** G-313 is a real federal funding gap. The PolArctic and Salmon Vision operational cases (parallel scout §11A.1) are the only documented Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployments in primary sources, and both are *grant-funded* through non-Indigenous-specific programmes (BCSRIF + DFO + WWF-Arctic + Qikiqtaaluk). Worth a dedicated future scan.
