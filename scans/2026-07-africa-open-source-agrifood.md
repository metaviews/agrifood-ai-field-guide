### 8.1 African-led open-source ecosystem is multilaterally-led + civil-society-led + academically-led

The African open-source ecosystem for agritech AI is structured as:

- **Multilateral / institutional layer**: CGIAR Open and FAIR Data Assets Policy + GODAN 2.0 + AUDA-NEPAD + AGRA open data
- **Civil society / NGO layer**: Ushahidi (Kenyan origin) + Code for Africa (pan-African) + Digital Green (700,000+ farmers) + Viamo + A Growing Culture + Pelum Zambia + Swaziland Rural Women's assembly
- **Academic / research layer**: Open AIR (288 citations Gwagwa et al. 2020) + Maseno University + Strathmore University + University of Cape Town + Alliance Bioversity-CIAT (100,000+ banana disease observations)

- **Open-source seed systems layer**: Open Source Seed Initiative (OSSI) + Seed Savers Network (Kenya)
- **Open-source AI infrastructure layer**: Mozilla Common Voice + CGIAR AgriLLM + Strathmore University AI tools + MBZUAI

The structural observation: **the African open-source ecosystem for agritech AI is more multi-actor than any other region in the corpus**. Compare to:
- **EU**: Mozilla + La Ferme Digitale / GAIA + JoinData + OADA (substantive but at the European institutional layer)
- **US**: Mozilla (Mozilla Foundation is US-based but global) + FarmOS + FarmVibes.AI (substantive but at US-vendor layer)
- **China**: substantive but state-led

The African open-source ecosystem is **structurally distinctive** because of the **multilateral + civil-society + academic + tooling + seed + voice** combination — substantively more multi-actor than any other region.
