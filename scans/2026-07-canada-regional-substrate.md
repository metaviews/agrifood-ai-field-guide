# Canada Regional Substrate Cycle — July 2026

**Status.** Third and final Canada-deepening scan. The first (funder/convenor substrate) is committed at `e466f3b`; the second (value-chain matrix) is committed at `ee86ba3`. Focus: the **regional substrates** — Atlantic, Prairies, British Columbia, North, and fisheries/aquaculture. Each region has a named institutional anchor + CAAIN-funded projects + named deployment actors.

**Scope.** Atlantic Institute for Digital Agriculture (AIDA) + Dalhousie Faculty of Agriculture; Prairies cluster (Saskatoon/Regina/Winnipeg/Steinbach/Vermilion); BC horticulture cluster (Surrey/Salmon Arm/Vancouver Island); Northern Canada (CanNor Feb 2026 cycle + Indigenous-led AI); Fisheries and Oceans Canada (DFO Pacific salmon AI) + Canadian aquaculture AI vendors (ReelData, OnDeck, Mowi, WellFish, Marine Thinking, Oceans North).

**Why this scan now.** The existing Canada scans name the regions in passing but have **zero dedicated regional substrate units**. The AIDA, the Prairie grain AI cluster, the BC horticulture cluster, the Northern CanNor cycle, and the DFO salmon AI + aquaculture vendor ecosystem are each substantively populated but under-coordinated. Writing regional units lets every downstream Canadian cycle cite specific regional anchors cleanly.

**Method.** Primary sources for every load-bearing claim. Each region's unit consolidates institutional anchors + named deployment actors + CAAIN projects into a single corpus cell.

**Cutoff.** Active in the last 12 months (July 2025 – July 2026). Older instruments included only when load-bearing.

## 2. Atlantic Canada — AIDA and the Maritimes substrate (verified)

### 2.1 Atlantic Institute for Digital Agriculture (AIDA)

**Atlantic Institute for Digital Agriculture (AIDA)** — launched in 2024 within Dalhousie University Faculty of Agriculture (Truro, NS). Director: **Dr. Travis Esau**, Professor, Department of Engineering. The institute is **the academic+convening substrate for digital agriculture in Atlantic Canada**, with five pillars:
1. **Precision agriculture** — soil sensors, satellite imagery, AI for resource-efficient farming.
2. **Robotics, automation & artificial intelligence** — autonomous vehicles, drones, AI-powered robots.
3. **Data-driven management** — big data and IoT for farm operations optimization.
4. **Human-computer interface** — farmer education, policy advocacy, user-friendly tools.
5. **Knowledge mobilization & capacity building** — knowledge transfer and outreach.

**Director's framing.** Travis Esau: "Traditional agricultural farming is not sustainable and therefore requires new engineering advancements to remain competitive in both local and global markets. Increased discovery and innovation are necessary to bridge the knowledge gap, enabling the development of new technologies and traceability protocols that enhance farm efficiency and promote the effective use of environmental resources."

## 5. Northern Canada — CanNor Feb 2026 cycle (verified)

### 5.1 CanNor February 6 2026 announcement

**Canadian Northern Economic Development Agency (CanNor)** announced on February 6 2026 the provision of **$2,815,999** for four projects supporting digital literacy and AI adoption in Nunavut, Northwest Territories, and Yukon. Programs used: IDEANorth, REGI – AI.

### 5.2 The four CanNor projects (verified)

1. **AI Driven Entrepreneurship and Business Support Centre** — Prosper NWT (Northwest Territories). Three years. Up to $2,316,000 from IDEANorth. Suite of AI-powered business tools + in-person/online AI training for business owners.

2. **Digital Literacy, Online Safety, and AI Training Program** — Inuvik Tech Society. Two years. Up to $200,000 from IDEANorth. Targets the Gwich'in Settlement Region and Inuvialuit Settlement Region ("a region that only recently gained reliable, high-speed connectivity"). Mobile training lab, workshops in seven communities.

3. **Empowering Inuit-led Economic Growth Through AI and Innovation** — Nunavut Economic Developers Association. Three years. Up to $200,000 from REGI – AI. Two AI/ML tools: (a) data-driven AI tool for community-level economic development plans in Nunavut municipalities; (b) AI/ML-powered grant and proposal writing tool "to enable Nunavut communities to independently draft applications aligned with their unique priorities and cultural context."

4. **AI platform for Arctic sensor** — DeltaVue Inc. (Whitehorse, Yukon). One year. Up to $99,999 from REGI – AI. Software that interprets and standardizes a wide array of sensor data for northern governments/researchers/industries.

### 5.3 PolArctic / Sanikiluaq mariculture AI (Nunavut)

**PolArctic LLC** + Nunavut Fishery Association + Qikiqtaaluk Corporation + WWF-Arctic + C-CORE + Sanikiluaq community (Inuit). **First AI model to treat Indigenous Knowledge and Western science as equals during training/validation.** Inputs: Nunavut Coastal Resource Inventory (NCRI), Indigenous community knowledge, satellite remote sensing, sea-surface temperature, bathymetry. Output: predicted habitat zones for scallops, clams, kelp near Sanikiluaq. Results in Inuktitut and English. Pilot completed 2021. Operational status unclear — primary follow-up needed.

### 5.4 Salmon Vision (BC + future Yukon)

**Salmon Vision** — SFU (Jiangchuan Liu, Jonathan Moore) + Pacific Salmon Foundation + Wild Salmon Center (William Atlas) + Gitanyow Fisheries Authority + Skeena Fisheries Commission. Supported by BC Salmon Restoration and Innovation Fund + DFO. Deep-learning computer vision at First-Nation-run weirs on **Bear River and Kitwanga River** (Northwestern BC). 12 species, 500,000+ annotated video frames, mAP 67.6%, coho >90%, sockeye >80%. Model moving to YOLO v8 + multi-object tracking. Expanding to Koeye and KwaKwa rivers. **Tier-1 source:** Atlas et al., Frontiers in Marine Science 2023.

## 6. Fisheries and aquaculture — DFO Pacific salmon AI and Canadian aquaculture vendors (verified)

### 6.1 DFO Pacific salmon AI — three active pilots (verified)

The **Fisheries and Oceans Canada (DFO) 2026–27 Departmental Plan** (June 2026) is explicit: "Explore options for policy and licensing changes that will modernize west coast commercial fisheries… Invest in digital modernization, including online licensing forms and pilot projects using artificial intelligence to digitize fishing data and reduce administrative burden."

**Three active DFO AI pilot projects in the Pacific Salmon Strategy Initiative (PSSI):**

1. **Chumputer vision AI** — Deep-learning CNNs that read salmon scale growth rings (circuli) to determine chum salmon age. Origin: manual age-reading by trained experts, **80,000+ scales/year**, demand exceeds capacity. The Chumputer is the *first step toward an all-salmon-species scale-reading AI*. Next: Chinook, sockeye, coho.

2. **Computer-vision salmon migration counter** — Automated fish counting at fish fences/weirs (Sproat and Stamp River fish ladders, Vancouver Island). Currently manual: trained staff count and identify sockeye/coho/Chinook from underwater camera footage, frame by frame. AI counters automate this in real time.

3. **Factoid Finder** — AI tool that "quickly reads, understands, and collates information from large literature collections" for the Integrated Planning for Salmon Ecosystems (IPSE) watershed planning process. Reads reports, scientific studies, policy documents. Used for the Nicola Watershed Integrated Salmon Ecosystem Strategy. Supports First Nations + Provincial/Territorial co-development.

**DFO's digital modernization counterweight.** DFO's 2026-27 spending reduction commitment: $54.47M (2026-27), $101.91M (2027-28), $193.82M (2028-29). Approximately 551 FTEs decrease by 2028-29. The AI pilots are part of the digital modernization counterweight to these cuts.

### 6.2 Digital Supercluster "AI for Aquaculture" (verified)

**Project Lead:** Excel Career College. **Partners:** BC Salmon Farmers Association, Comox Valley Chamber of Commerce, Vancouver Island First Nations and regional development organizations. **Funding stream:** Digital Talent & Connectivity pillar. **Updated:** June 5 2025. **Distinctive feature:** Programme explicitly combines traditional knowledge and traditional Indigenous knowledge with training on AI/ML/IoT and digital skills. Targets oyster farming, salmon hatcheries, fish processing. Includes scaling blueprint for Canada-wide.

### 6.3 OnDeck Fisheries AI (Vancouver, BC) (verified)

OnDeck Fisheries AI (Vancouver lead). Partners: Teem Fish Monitoring, Ha'oom Fisheries Society, Snap Information Technologies, Canadian Groundfish Research and Conservation Society, Ocean Wise, Goodfish Seafood Co., J.O. Thomas & Associates. **$1.5M from Canada's Ocean Supercluster under Pan-Canadian AI Strategy (PCAIS) Program.** Aims to make electronic monitoring (EM) of commercial fisheries 10–12× more affordable for the projected surge in vessel coverage needed.

### 6.4 ReelData AI (Halifax, NS) (verified)

ReelData AI (founded 2018, Halifax/Dartmouth HQ). VC-funded by Buoyant Ventures. Biomass-estimation camera, feed-management automation, behavioural-monitoring platform for land-based recirculating aquaculture systems (RAS). Pilots include Fifax (Finland) rainbow trout RAS. **Sources:** Buoyant VC writeup 2023; Land Based Aquaculture coverage (Fifax); Aquaculture North America.

### 6.5 Mowi Canada West — Centralized Feed Centre (verified)

Mowi Canada West (subsidiary of Mowi ASA, world's largest Atlantic salmon farmer). Centralized Feed Centre using AI to integrate oxygen/temperature/salinity sensors with feeding behaviour; staff 100% focused on feeding, removed from farm sites. **Caveat:** 2026 DFO transition plan for 79 BC salmon farms remains unresolved as of July 2026 — operational future of these AI deployments depends on policy outcomes.

### 6.6 WellFish Tech (PEI) (verified)

WellFish Tech — Scotland-origin startup, expanded to Norway and Canada; PEI presence via PEI BioAlliance. Founded 2021. Combines production data + clinical chemistry + AI tools for non-lethal blood assessment in aquaculture, wild stocks, research animals.

### 6.7 Marine Thinking (Newfoundland) (verified)

Marine Thinking. AI for catch monitoring, autonomous detection and location of ghost gear in fishing waters. Project stage not detailed publicly.

### 6.8 Ocean Supercluster fish welfare AI (verified)

Ocean Supercluster has a documented H2S-sensor + AI-camera fish welfare project with **Grieg Seafood** for real-time monitoring of fish welfare.

### 6.9 What the DFO/aquaculture unit captures

A dedicated `units/dfo-pacific-salmon-ai.md` would consolidate the DFO pilots. A dedicated `units/canadian-aquaculture-ai.md` would consolidate the aquaculture vendor ecosystem. Or, a combined unit consolidating both could be more efficient.
