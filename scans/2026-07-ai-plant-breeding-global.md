## §2 — Six substantively distinct regional cluster shapes

The substantive regional-cluster-comparison observation: there are six substantively distinct regional cluster shapes for AI plant breeding globally.

### §2.1 — Chinese state-orchestrated cluster

Per `units/longping-yuan-caas-china-seed-ai.md`:

- **State-academic:** CAAS + Xie 2026 Plant Communications + Huihui Li CAAS genomic prediction.
- **State-OE:** Syngenta Group (Swiss HQ, Chinese state-owned via Sinochem/ChemChina after USD$43B 2017 acquisition).
- **State-network:** BGI/MGI genomics + Huawei + China Mobile 5G + unmanned-farm robotics.
- **Policy frame:** 2021 amended Seed Law (IP-strengthening); 2024-28 Smart Agriculture Action Plan; 2024 National Food Security Law; March 2025 Sanya Seed Congress smart-breeding national frame.
- **Distinct feature:** Substantively largest state-orchestrated cluster globally; BGI/MGI genomics substrate; Sinochem-ChemChina-owned Syngenta Group integration.
- **Critical voice:** GRAIN 2023 "Capturing the seed."

### §2.2 — CGIAR public-platform for the Global South

Per `units/cgiar-eib-global-south-plant-breeding.md`:

- **Multilateral-system governance:** ITPGRFA Multilateral System + 700K+ germplasm samples.
- **12 priority crops:** wheat, maize, rice, sorghum, millet, groundnut, common bean, cowpea, cassava, sweetpotato, yam, banana.
- **15-30% genetic-gain uplift commitment** through AI-augmented pipelines.
- **Substantive operational:** ICRISAT × CIMMYT AI-driven predictive breeding initiative.
- **Funding substrate:** Gates Foundation + USAID Feed the Future + FFAR + GIZ + World Bank + FCDO + ACIAR + AAFC + EU + CGIAR Trust Fund.
- **Distinct feature:** Substantively only actor combining (a) substantive AI-augmented breeding pipelines, (b) multilateral-system governance, and (c) 700K+ germplasm samples in ITPGRFA trust.

### §2.3 — EU private-breeding cluster (cooperative + family-controlled + public-academic)

Per `units/limagrain-kws-ragt-eu-private-plant-breeding.md`:

- **Cooperative:** Limagrain (FR cooperative; InVivo federation).
- **Family-controlled mid-cap corporates:** KWS (DE, founded 1856); Enza Zaden (NL); Rijk Zwaan (NL).
- **Public-academic:** Wageningen University & Research (NL) + INRAE (FR) + Garcia-Oliveira 2026 (cited 7) + Thomsen 2026 (cited 1) + Liang 2026 (cited 1).
- **Mid-cap corporate:** RAGT (FR) + KWS + Limagrain + Enza Zaden + Rijk Zwaan.
- **Policy frame:** EU New Genomic Techniques (NGT) Regulation (final approval per KWS 2025); EU Horizon Cluster 6; Euroseeds industry convening.
- **Distinct feature:** Substantively most diverse ownership forms globally + substantive public-academic AI plant-breeding-research tier.

### §2.4 — Multinational-corporate-pipelined cluster

Per `units/bayer-syngenta-corteva-multinational-pipelines.md`:

- **Bayer Crop Science (DE):** ~€6-11bn R&D pipeline 2024-2030+; DEKALB Disease Shield® + 10 blockbusters in 10 years; Seed Production Innovation Center at Iowa State (2024).
- **Syngenta Group (CH; Chinese state-owned):** 5 key AI trends in agriculture (2025); TraitSeq + Syngenta Crop Protection collaboration; planned 2026+ IPO.
- **Corteva (US):** Granular on-farm AI + AI-augmented crop protection pipeline + Pioneer brand.
- **BASF (DE):** xarvio Digital Farming + Nunhems vegetable seeds.
- **Concentration observation:** ETC Group/GRAIN 2025: "Four firms control 56% of global seed market."
- **Distinct feature:** Substantively largest commercial deployment + substantive concentration concern.

### §2.5 — US land-grant cluster

Per `units/usda-ars-iowa-state-aiira-us-land-grant.md`:

- **USDA-NIFA AI Research Institutes:** AIIRA at Iowa State (one of 11 NSF AI Research Institutes, 2021 cycle, $220M NSF investment).
- **USDA-NIFA 2026 cycle:** AFRI FY2026 $140M pool; AI for K-12 Food and Agricultural Sciences ($1-2M grants).
- **USDA-ARS federal-research:** Plant Genetic Resources Unit (Geneva NY); Genomics and Bioinformatics Research Unit (Stoneville MS); Grape Genetics Research Unit (Geneva NY); National Plant Germplasm System (NPGS) public-domain germplasm.
- **Land-grant universities:** Texas A&M (wheat-breeding pipeline genomic prediction); University of Tennessee; NAPB community of practice.
- **Corporate-startup layer:** Heritable Agriculture (Google X spinoff, 2024, digital twin); Biographica (US/UK, $9.5M funding + BASF partnership, 2026).
- **Distinct feature:** Substantively unique land-grant × USDA-ARS × extension × corporate-startup architecture.

### §2.6 — Canadian provincial-commodity-pipelined cluster

Per existing corpus unit `units/uog-bean-gpt-najafabadi.md`:

- **Academic-research-led:** Najafabadi lab (U Guelph OAC); BeanGPT (RAG over 314K+ articles + Ontario trial data since 2006).
- **Provincial-commodity-pipelined:** Ontario Bean Growers + ARIO + OMAFA + Hensall Co-Op + Sprague Foods + Agilent Technology.
- **Federal-tri-council:** NSERC.
- **University tech-transfer:** U Guelph RIO.

- **Distinct feature:** Substantively academic-research-led + provincial-commodity-pipelined + farmer-co-op-partnered cluster shape — substantively different from multinational-corporate-pipelined or state-orchestrated clusters.

### §2.7 — Substantive cross-cluster observations

The substantive cross-cluster observations:

1. **Syngenta Group bridges multinational-corporate cluster AND Chinese state-orchestrated cluster** — substantively Swiss-HQ + Chinese state-owned via Sinochem/ChemChina. Substantively the global-bridging entity.
