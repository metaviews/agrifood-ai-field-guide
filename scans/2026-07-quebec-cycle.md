# Quebec Cycle — July 2026

**Status.** New regional cycle. Substantial Canadian deepening following the FCC cycle, Neethirajan ecosystem work, and Haven Greens / food-waste framework.

**Scope.** Quebec specifically: Montreal AI cluster (Mila, IVADO, the agtech ecosystem), Quebec's export-oriented food processing industry (Saputo, Olymel, Agropur, SoraLINK deployment), greenhouse AI (Montérégie cluster, Sollum, ChrysaLabs, Zone Agtech), and the Quebec positioning in the *AI for All* national strategy.

**Why this scan now.** Quebec has been substantively absent from the field guide despite being the most distinctive Canadian jurisdiction for AI. Montreal is the largest academic AI cluster in Canada (Mila), Quebec is the largest Canadian dairy producer, and Quebec's food processing industry is the most export-oriented in Canada. Worth surfacing as a distinct cycle.

**Method.** Substantive reading of primary sources from named Quebec institutions, vendors, and federal partners. Honest about gaps where they exist.

**Cutoff.** Active in the last year.

## 1. Quebec's distinctive currents in agrifood AI

### 1.1 Montreal academic AI cluster — Mila and IVADO

The Montreal academic AI cluster is the largest in Canada and one of the largest in the world. Two institutions anchor it for agrifood purposes:

**Mila** (Quebec AI Institute) — founded 2017 by Yoshua Bengio, headquartered Montreal. ~1,100 researchers, ~1,000 students. Quebec provincial funding of $36M (announced 2026). Direct agrifood work via the **DISA (Data-driven Insights for Sustainable Agriculture)** project — uses satellite imagery and algorithms to assess existing practices and promote regenerative agriculture, which sequesters carbon while reducing soil erosion and water pollution. Other Mila applied projects (Antenna, FLAIR, AIMS) include agrifood-adjacent work even when not strictly agricultural.

**IVADO** (Institute for Data Valorization) — 150+ partners, 1,400+ scientists, anchored at Université de Montréal. The **R3AI project** (Robust, Reasoning, Responsible AI) is IVADO's flagship substantive initiative. June 8, 2026 statement: "Canada's National AI Strategy: Rising to the Ambitions of Canada, and of IVADO" — IVADO positioning itself as the Quebec-side implementation partner for the federal *AI for All* strategy. Workforce training, technology transfer, and francophone / Indigenous community engagement are explicit workstreams.

Both institutions are positioned to anchor Canada's *AI for All* national strategy from the Quebec side. The federal strategy was developed through 11,000+ Canadian submissions; Mila and IVADO are the Quebec-side structural implementation partners.

### 1.2 Greater Montréal agtech cluster — Zone Agtech and 220+ organisations

Québec is one of Canada's main agtech hubs with over 220 organisations. Greater Montréal is the epicentre. The **Zone Agtech** in L'Assomption has 250+ members and partners including 13 universities and research centres, with C$400M+ in funding. The Zone Agtech was established by transforming a vacant manufacturing plant into an agtech innovation campus.

**Concrete operational data (Investment Monitor / Montréal International):**
- 130+ agtech businesses in Quebec integrating AI
- Quebec produces C$200M (~$148.53M) of fruit, vegetables, leafy greens annually
- 50% of Canada's organic agriculture production
- Quebec target: 37.5% GHG reduction by 2030, carbon neutral by 2050

**Named Montreal-based agtech vendors:**
- **Sollum** — "sun-as-a-service" AI-powered greenhouse light fixtures. Reduces electricity consumption by up to 40%. See `sollum-sun-as-a-service.md`.
- **ChrysaLabs** — photonics + AI for real-time soil nutrient measurement. Laboratory-grade data on soil health and fertility.
- **SoraLINK** — AI predictive maintenance deployed across Quebec's largest food processors (Saputo, Olymel, Agropur). See `soralink-export-food-processing.md`.
- **Savoura** — large-scale tomato producer (Montérégie-based).
- **Lufa Farms** — rooftop farming pioneer. Montréal-based.
- **GoodLeaf Farms** — 100,000ft² indoor microgreen farm in Greater Montréal (vertical farming).
- **Hoogendoorn** (Dutch) — Quebec deployment of IIVO software and LetsGrow.com for greenhouse automation.

The cluster is *vertically integrated* across precision agriculture, vertical farming, smart farming, agrobiotechnology, automation, and greenhouse technology. AI is positioned as a *force multiplier* across the cluster rather than a single deployment pattern.

### 1.3 Quebec's export-oriented food processing industry

This is the distinctively Quebec angle. Quebec's food processing industry is *the most export-oriented in Canada*. The major players — Saputo (dairy), Olymel (pork and poultry), Agropur (dairy cooperative), Lassonde (beverages), Bonduelle (vegetables) — operate internationally.

**The SoraLINK deployment is the most concrete AI-deployment evidence we have for the export-oriented food industry.** SoraLINK's predictive maintenance AI is trained on food and bev production cycles, handles seasonal variability, and is *deployed across Saputo, Olymel, Agropur, and more* (per SoraLINK's industry page). Claim: $250,000 saved in a single intervention.

**Why this matters structurally:** The food processing industry is a *cell* of the matrix that has been largely under-populated in the field guide. Most of our units are on-farm (production) or distribution/retail. The processing cell is where a large fraction of value capture happens, and where the export-oriented food industry AI is most likely to be deployed. Saputo operates in the US, Australia, Argentina, and the UK. Olymel is the largest pork and poultry processor in Canada. Agropur is the largest dairy cooperative in Canada. AI deployment at this scale touches the *processing cell* of the matrix substantively.

**Loblaw × Blue Yonder** (`loblaw-blue-yonder-forecasting.md`) is the closest existing analog — supply-chain forecasting for the largest Canadian grocer. The SoraLINK × Saputo/Olymel/Agropur pattern is the *processing-side* analog. Together they cover the major Canadian food-system AI deployment outside on-farm.

### 1.4 The Quebec positioning in *AI for All*

The federal *AI for All* strategy (June 4, 2026) is positioned to be implemented through Quebec-side institutional anchors: Mila, IVADO, and the R3AI project. The strategy's *supply chains* priority sector aligns with IVADO's research clusters. The strategy's *training and adoption* priorities align with IVADO's SME-focused technology transfer work.

The Conversation (June 2026) framing: "To achieve 'AI for all' in agriculture, Canada's farmers need regional, systems-level change." This is the *regional* implementation question — and Quebec is positioned as the testbed for Quebec-style institutional implementation (Mila + IVADO + Zone Agtech + food processors).

The U of Guelph (June 2026) response: "Agri-Food Sector Requires Coordinated Approach to AI." Worth knowing that Ontario academic voices are pushing the same coordination point.

## 3. Quebec-specific contested claims and gaps

- **C-020 (new):** "Mila is principally a research institute; agrifood is a small subset of its work." Counter: the DISA project plus the Applied Projects portfolio shows Mila positions agrifood as a *core applied project area*, not peripheral. Worth tracking.
- **C-021 (new):** "Quebec's food processing AI is competitive with US deployments." Counter: SoraLINK's $250,000 saved in a single intervention is vendor-reported, single-deployment; not at the scale of US food processing AI. Worth verifying.
- **G-026 (new):** Quebec consumer-facing agrifood AI (Loblaw, Metro, Sobeys operations in Quebec). Distinct from Ontario patterns. Largely under-surfaced.
- **G-027 (new):** Quebec field-crop (open-field) AI deployment. Real but under-surfaced in our scan.
- **G-028 (new):** IVADO R3AI substantive deployment (vs. positioning). The June 8, 2026 statement is forward-looking; substantive deployment outcomes not yet visible.
- **G-029 (new):** Mila DISA partner farms and concrete deployed acreage. The project is real but operational scale not surfaced.
- **G-030 (new):** Sollum / Zone Agtech actual deployed acreage and number of grower deployments. The 40% electricity reduction figure is per-deployment; aggregate deployment is the gap.

## 5. What the Quebec cycle asks of the field guide

1. **The processing cell of the matrix is now substantively populated for the first time.** SoraLINK × Saputo/Olymel/Agropur is the *first export-oriented food processing AI unit* in the field guide. Worth knowing because the field guide was previously heavy on production and distribution; processing is where much of the value capture happens.

2. **Montreal as a distinct academic AI cluster.** Mila + IVADO + Zone Agtech + Montréal International = a *regional academic cluster* that complements the Ontario / Prairies / West Coast patterns. Distinct because the Quebec cluster is more *agricultural processing* focused than the Ontario / Prairies which are more on-farm.

3. **The Quebec angle is more export-oriented than other Canadian jurisdictions.** Saputo, Olymel, Agropur operate internationally. The AI deployment in their operations has implications for food system AI globally. Worth tracking.

4. **IVADO is positioned as the Quebec-side implementation partner for *AI for All*.** The federal strategy's success depends on regional implementation. Quebec is the testbed. Worth tracking whether the R3AI project delivers substantively.

5. **The processing-cell population is the most important new content for the field guide.** Most of our units have been about production or distribution. Processing is a third major cell and the Quebec cycle gives it real content.
