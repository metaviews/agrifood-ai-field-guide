# Canada Academic-Research Substrate Cycle — July 2026

**Status.** Fourth Canada-deepening cycle, parallel to the **funder/convenor substrate scan** (federal cluster programmes — CAAIN / Scale AI / Digital / PIC / RAII), the **value-chain matrix scan**, and the **regional substrate scan**. This cycle is the **academic-research substrate layer** that sits *upstream of* the cluster deployment tier.

**Scope.** The Canadian university + college + research-institute substrate for agrifood AI research. Anchors:
1. AIDA at Dalhousie Faculty of Agriculture (Truro NS) — Atlantic academic+convening substrate.
2. AI4Food at University of Guelph — Ontario academic anchor.
3. Agri-Food Analytics Lab (AAL) at Dalhousie — critical-voice academic-policy lab.
4. Mila + IVADO + Université de Montréal + McGill — Quebec academic AI substrate (PCAIS-funded).
5. Olds College Smart Farm — Prairie applied-research college.
6. National academic-research funding stack (NSERC + Mitacs + CFI + CFREF + FRQ + AAFC).
7. Per-individual analytical voices (Neethirajan, Dara, Charlebois, plus the federal-AIDA academic / federal-AIDA regulatory acronym collision).

**Why this scan now.** The existing Canada scans name these actors in passing (constraint/critical scout §7 critical-academic voices; FCC cycle §5 actor list for Olds College + AIVA Network; deepening-scout §4 for AIDA; Quebec cycle §99 for Mila DISA) but have **zero dedicated units for the university/college research substrate**. The corpus previously had:
- **3 critical academic voices** named in constraint/critical scout (Neethirajan, Dara, Charlebois).
- **AIDA referenced** in deepening-scout + LEADS.md L208 + canada-deepening-cycle-talk L225.
- **AI4Food, AAL, Mila-agrifood funding** referenced but lacking dedicated units.
- **The NSERC + Mitacs + CFI + CFREF + FRQ + AAFC academic-research funding stack** documented in CHANNEL NAMES only; not surfaced as an agrifood-AI funding-stack unit.

This cycle fills that gap with five dedicated units (4 academic-research-institute / lab units + 1 funding-stack unit) and addresses specific substantive corpus corrections (G-029 reframing — DISA is Rwandan, not Canadian-deployment; AIDA's 13 SAC count).

**Method.** Primary sources for every load-bearing claim. Where the research institute has a public projects database or named-team page, enumerated directly from the source. Where the institute has only narrative positioning, summarized with explicit naming of the source.

**Cutoff.** Active in the last 12 months (July 2025 – July 2026). Older work included where load-bearing (CCSC 2021 SSHRC; CFREF awards 2015–2017; etc.).

## 1. The five-anchor academic-research substrate

The Canadian agrifood AI academic-research substrate in mid-2026 has **five distinct anchors**, layered on top of the federal-cluster deployment substrate (existing scan) and the AIVA validation network.

| Anchor | Region | Lead | Type | Coverage |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **AIDA** | Atlantic (Truro NS) | Travis Esau | Cross-faculty academic institute | Atlantic region; 5 pillars; 13 SAC members + Neethirajan; Canadian Soil Data Portal |
| **AI4Food** | Ontario (Guelph) | Rozita Dara | AI-research-and-governance institute | Ontario; 4 focus areas (Application / Responsible AI / AI Tech / Data); AI4Food-augmented by Food from Thought CFREF |
| **AAL** | Atlantic (Dalhousie) | Sylvain Charlebois | Critical-voice academic-policy lab | Pan-Canadian media voice; Canada's Food Price Report; "data-deficit" critical frame |
| **Mila + IVADO** | Quebec (Montreal) | Mila / IVADO directors | PCAIS-funded AI hub | Quebec + international (DISA Rwanda; Innovasea aquaculture) |
| **Olds College Smart Farm** | Prairie (Olds AB) | Olds College | Academic applied-research college | 3,600 acres; Bachelor of Digital Agriculture; AIVA hub |

Distinct from the federal-cluster substrate (CAAIN / Scale AI / Digital / PIC / RAII) and from the AIVA validation network (which includes EMILI / Innovation Farms Ontario / Area X.O). The academic-research substrate is the **prototype-research + critical-voice layer**; the cluster substrate is the **deployment tier**.

### 1.1 What this means structurally

The substrate has **distinct academic-voice logics**:

- **AIDA** — cross-faculty regional academic+convening substrate. Engineers + soil scientists + animal scientists + CS faculty + business + social science. Convening mandate (knowledge mobilization pillar).
- **AI4Food** — AI-research-and-governance institute. Application + Responsible AI + AI Tech + Data. Governance-and-policy research substrate.
- **AAL** — critical-voice academic-policy lab. Media-facing; Canada's Food Price Report; Food Sentiment Index; "data-deficit" frame.
- **Mila + IVADO** — national-AI-hub-tier research capacity (Mila is PCAIS Tier 1). Portfolio of projects (DISA + Innovasea + R3AI) rather than standing institute. International reach (DISA in Rwanda).
- **Olds College** — applied-research + applied-degree (Bachelor of Digital Agriculture). AIVA hub for Prairie.

**Three of the five** (AIDA, AI4Food, Olds College) have **explicit AI-agrifood mandates**. **One** (AAL) is positioned as the data-deficit critical voice. **One** (Mila + IVADO) is positioned as the national-AI-hub capacity pipeline.

This is structurally distinct from US land-grant agronomy departments (which often have *AI as a tool within an agronomy department*) and from EU Wageningen-style mono-faculty models (which concentrate AI in one research school). Canadian agrifood AI is **dispersed across multiple institute architectures**, each with a different mission statement.

## 7. National academic-research funding stack

`units/canada-academic-research-funding-stack.md` is the unit-level detail.

**Seven-channel stack:**
1. **NSERC Alliance** (incl. NSERC Alliance-Mitacs Accelerate joint) — industry-partnered academic research.
2. **Mitacs Accelerate** — graduate-student/postdoc internships.
3. **CFI + JELF** (Canada Foundation for Innovation + John R. Evans Leaders Fund) — university-lab infrastructure.
4. **CFREF** (Canada First Research Excellence Fund) — institutional excellence; Food from Thought (U Guelph) + P2IRC (U Saskatchewan, now wrapped into GIFS).
5. **SSHRC** — data-governance / social-dimensions research (Fulton CCSC 2021).
6. **FRQ** (Fonds de recherche du Québec) — Quebec counterpart.
7. **AAFC AgriScience** — applied agrifood research; mode40 Lacombe path.

The academic-research funding stack is **parallel to** the federal-cluster deployment substrate (CAAIN / Scale AI / Digital / PIC / RAII). Cluster funding (TRL 7+) takes prototype-to-deployment; academic-research funding (TRL 3–6) takes prototype-grade projects. **They are complementary, not duplicative.**

**G-326 (new):** There is no federal channel that explicitly funds agrifood-AI research as a sectoral priority at the academic-research tier. Canadian agrifood AI research is funded as part of general computer-science / engineering / agriculture research (NSERC, Mitacs, CFI, CFREF) — not through a dedicated federal agrifood-AI channel. The cluster tier (CAAIN) has an agrifood-AI-specific channel; the research tier does not.

## 8. The federal-AIDA / academic-AIDA acronym collision

A substantive corpus-clarity note: **two distinct AIDAs** exist in the Canadian agrifood AI picture:

1. **Federal AIDA** (Bill C-27, *Artificial Intelligence and Data Act*) — the regulatory bill that **died on the order paper at prorogation on 6 January 2025**. Substantively a regulatory substrate (now dead).
2. **Academic AIDA** (Atlantic Institute for Digital Agriculture, launched November 25, 2025) — the academic+convening substrate at Dalhousie Faculty of Agriculture.

Same acronym; different entities; one dead and one launched. **Worth carrying in talks for clarity.** AFN October 2023 INDU submission critiqued the *federal* AIDA as "overly individualistic, excluding any protection from community harms" — a critique of the *regulatory bill*, not the *academic institute*.

## 11. Substantive contested claims and gaps (this cycle)

### 11.1 New contested claims

- **C-283 (new):** "Canadian agrifood AI is academically led." Counter: AIDA + AI4Food + AAL + Mila + IVADO + Olds College are academic anchors, but **federal-cluster deployment** (CAAIN / Scale AI / Digital / PIC / RAII) is the primary *funding* tier; academic anchors support deployment but are not the *funding* model. The Brazilian-seed-AI cluster-with-three-structures pattern (see `units/brazilian-seed-ai-academic-research-led.md`) is *not* the dominant Canadian pattern — Canadian agrifood AI is *cluster-led + academic-research-anchored*, not academic-research-led + cluster-pipelined.
- **C-284 (new):** "AIDA, AI4Food, AAL are university-based so they have similar governance." Counter: AIDA is a Dalhousie cross-faculty institute with CFREF/NSERC/CFI funding; AI4Food is a U Guelph School-of-CS-based institute with Food from Thought CFREF + Mitacs funding; AAL is a Dalhousie Faculty-of-Agriculture consumer-policy lab with media-facing outputs. Three different governance structures; three different funding models.
- **C-285 (new):** "Mila's DISA project deploys in Canada." Counter: Mila's DISA primary source explicitly says "Our scope is initially focused on the Rwandan context." Substantively DISA is Rwandan-deployment + Canadian-research-pipelined; not Canadian-farm-partner deployment. Worth tracking and correcting G-029.
- **C-286 (new):** "Canada has a federal academic-research agrifood AI funding channel." Counter: None. NSERC, Mitacs, CFI, CFREF, SSHRC, FRQ, AAFC fund agrifood AI as part of general programs; no dedicated sectoral channel. Cluster tier (CAAIN) has one; research tier does not.

### 11.2 New gaps

- **G-322 (existing):** AIDA's specific industry partnerships and deployment outcomes not publicly detailed.
- **G-329 (new):** AI4Food's specific industry partnerships, deployed acreage, and named partner farms / companies not publicly detailed.
- **G-330 (new):** AAL's specific AI-technique deployment projects not surfaced in primary sources; AAL's substantive contribution is the data-deficit critical voice, not AI technique deployment.
- **G-331 (new, supersedes G-029):** DISA is Rwandan-deployment + Canadian-research-pipelined, not Canadian-partner-farm deployment. Mila + IVADO agrifood profile is "Canadian ML capacity reaching international food systems" rather than "Canadian agrifood deployment." G-029's original framing should be retired or reframed.
- **G-332 (new):** No dedicated federal academic-research funding channel for agrifood AI as a sectoral priority at the research tier; Canadian agrifood AI research is funded as part of general NSERC + Mitacs + CFI + CFREF programs.
- **G-333 (new):** Provincial RDC (Research and Development Centre) academic-research inventory not surfaced for AAFC Sherbrooke RDC, AAFC Lacombe RDC, AAFC Saint-Hyacinthe RDC, etc. AAFC runs multiple RDCs that are relevant academic-tier partners; specific RDC × agrifood-AI projects not enumerated.
