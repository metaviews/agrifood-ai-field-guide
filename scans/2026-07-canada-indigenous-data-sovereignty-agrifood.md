# Canada — Indigenous Data Sovereignty + AI in Agrifood

## 1. Scope and substantive finding

This scan consolidates the **Indigenous Data Sovereignty (IDSov) + AI in Canadian agrifood** substantive cell. It does so across four layers per the project's ecosystem-mapping pattern:

1. **Framework layer** — international + Canadian Indigenous data governance frameworks and standards.
2. **Canadian institutional layer** — Indigenous-led organisations + Canadian academic + federal-provincial funding instruments.
3. **Operational deployment layer** — Indigenous-led AI deployments with agrifood-adjacent relevance.
4. **Critical-analytical-voice layer** — Canadian researchers writing substantively about IDSov + AI + agrifood.

**Substantive finding:** The Canadian agrifood AI ecosystem has substantive operational deployments from Indigenous-led and Indigenous-partnered organisations (PolArctic, Salmon Vision, CanNor-funded projects) that are *ahead of* the dedicated federal funding instrument for Indigenous-led agrifood AI. The framework side is rich (CARE, IEEE 2890-2025, OCAP, FNDGS, NCIAF); the deployments operationalise the framework's principles but are funded through non-Indigenous-specific instruments (DFO, CanNor, BCSFA, BCSRIF). **This is the framework-deployment funding-instrument gap — G-313.**

The **critical-analytical voice** (Ruder + Wittman + collaborators) is substantively important Canadian work that the existing corpus has not yet anchored as a dedicated unit. The Big Data & Society paper (2025, cited 22 times), the Journal of Peasant Studies paper (2025, cited 14 times), the Journal of Rural Studies paper (2026, cited 6 times), and the BCACARN Ethical Data Governance Toolkit (2024) collectively constitute the substantive Canadian agrifood data-governance analytical voice.

## 2. Framework layer (international + Canadian Indigenous data governance)

### 2.1 CARE Principles for Indigenous Data Governance (international; Canadian institutional anchor: GIDA + FNIGC)

**Published:** 2019 (Gaborone); formally in *Data Science Journal* 2020 (Carroll, Garba, Figueroa-Rodríguez, et al.). **Cited:** 1,808 (Carroll et al. 2020).

**Co-leads:** Stephanie Russo Carroll (University of Arizona) + Maui Hudson (University of Waikato, Aotearoa New Zealand).

**Institutional anchor:** GIDA (Global Indigenous Data Alliance). GIDA members from Aotearoa (NZ), Australia, Canada, Finland, Norway, Mexico, Sweden, Tonga and Samoa (diaspora Aotearoa), and the United States.

**Four principles:** Collective benefit; Authority to control; Responsibility; Ethics.

**FAIR + CARE framing:** CARE Principles explicitly complement FAIR Principles. The synthesis is "leading with CARE" — both people and purpose are foregrounded in data stewardship, access, and use.

**Translations:** Spanish, Vietnamese, Māori, German, Khmer.

**Operationalising paper:** Carroll, S.R. et al. (2021). *Operationalizing the CARE and FAIR Principles for Indigenous data futures*. *Scientific Data* 8, 108.

**Substantive agrifood engagement:** CARE Principles are engaged substantively in *Big Data & Society* (Ruder & Wittman 2025) and in the *HLPE-FSN 2026 FAO background note on AI for food security and nutrition* — the latter being the most authoritative international agrifood AI policy document.

### 2.2 IEEE 2890-2025 — Provenance of Indigenous Peoples' Data (international industry standard)

**Approved:** September 10, 2025 (IEEE SA Standards Board). **Published:** November 14, 2025. **PAR approval:** June 3, 2020 (5-year development process).

**Working Group:** ID-WG — Indigenous Data Working Group. **Standard Committee:** SSIT/SC — Social Implications of Technology Standards Committee. **Working Group Chair:** Stephanie Russo Carroll (University of Arizona). **IEEE Program Manager:** Malia Zaman.

**Format:** IEEE Recommended Practice (industry specification, not binding regulation).

**What it does:** Establishes a common set of parameters for the provenance of Indigenous Peoples' data. Supports appropriate disclosure of Indigenous Peoples' relationships and/or links to all data. **Critically: explicitly applies to AI/ML industries.** The Udall Center event description (Feb 2026) states: *"Within data-generated industries, such as artificial intelligence and machine learning, as well as biodiversity research and innovations in genomic science, this standard enhances the integrity of that data."*

**Recognition:** World's first global standard for the provenance of Indigenous data. GIDA recognised IEEE 2890-2025 as including the active participation of Indigenous members in the development process, with Indigenous Caucus, consultation with Indigenous Peoples, and ensuring open access.

### 2.3 OCAP® (Canadian First Nations data governance)

**Full form:** Ownership, Control, Access, Possession. **Institutional anchor:** FNIGC (First Nations Information Governance Centre, Ottawa). **Established:** 1998 as OCA; "P" affixed later (per Sattigeri 2025).

**Why this matters for Canadian agrifood:** OCAP predates and informs the CARE Principles. **"OCAP® is a registered trademark of the First Nations Information Governance Centre (FNIGC)"** (per NRCAN 2024 *Canadian Geospatial Data Infrastructure Stocktaking Desk Study*). For Canadian federal science agencies (NRCAN, AAFC, DFO), OCAP is the operative Indigenous data governance framework — not CARE.

### 2.4 First Nations Data Governance Strategy (FNDGS, Canadian national strategy)

**Submitted:** 2025 (Assembly of First Nations Resolution 30-2025). **Lead:** FNIGC + regional partners.

**What it does:** Community-driven, Nation-based national strategy. **Plans regional information governance centres (one per region).** Submitted to Indigenous Services Canada (ISC).

**Why this matters for agrifood:** The FNDGS is the most authoritative Canadian operational framework for Indigenous data governance at scale. For AAFC, ISC, regional RDA partners, the FNDGS is the reference framework — not CARE or IEEE 2890 directly.

**Operational vehicle:** AFN Resolution 30-2025 directs ISC to "direct adequate resources to the First Nations Information Governance Centre (FNIGC) to work with its regional partners" (per AFN 2025 SCA Draft On-time Resolutions).

### 2.5 Inuit Qaujimajatuqangit (IQ, Government of Nunavut)

**What it is:** Inuit traditional knowledge framework. Eight principles (per Government of Nunavut). Inuit Tapiriit Kanatami (ITK) National Inuit Strategy on Research extends IQ into research governance.

**Why this matters for Canadian agrifood:** IQ + National Inuit Strategy on Research is the operative Inuit data governance framework — analogous to OCAP for First Nations.

**Agrifood engagement:** Carleton Anako Core and Guiding Principles cites IQ as Inuit teachings providing "a culturally grounded sense of purpose and inherent responsibilities." SFU Library Indigenous Data Sovereignty page references IQ alongside the National Inuit Strategy on Research. Cunningham-Reimann 2025 (*Global Public Health*) cites IQ alongside OCAP and OCAS as foundational Indigenous research principles.

**Operational gap:** PolArctic Sanikiluaq is the only operational agrifood-adjacent AI deployment that explicitly operationalises Inuit Qaujimajatuqangit; the framework is operational in research and policy contexts but the agrifood AI deployment is rare.

### 2.6 FAIR + CARE complementarity (international bridge)

**Substantive observation:** CARE was developed explicitly to complement FAIR. The bridge is articulated by:

- Carroll et al. 2020 (foundational).
- Carroll et al. 2021 *Operationalizing the CARE and FAIR Principles for Indigenous data futures*.
- Taitingfong et al. 2024 *Aligning policy and practice to implement CARE with FAIR through Indigenous Peoples' protocols* (*Acta Borealia* 41:2, 80-90).
- GIDA 2024 "CARE Directs Us Home" communiqué (Riley Taitingfong, Stephanie Russo Carroll, Jacinta Fa'alili-Fidow, Oscar Figueroa, Aaron Franks, Ibrahim Garba, Lydia Jennings, Andrew Martinez, Jacob Prehn, Cassandra Sedran-Price, Susanna Siri, Desi Small-Rodriguez, El-Shadan Tautolo, Jennifer D. Walker, Tahu Kukutai). DOI: 10.6084/m9.figshare.29294120.v1.
- Ruder & Wittman 2025 (Big Data & Society).
- Gacenga et al. 2024 (Australian Drought Monitor dataset).

**For Canadian agrifood:** The FAIR + CARE bridge is the substantive synthesis that allows non-Indigenous federal science agencies (NRCAN, AAFC) to engage Indigenous data governance through their existing data infrastructure practices — and is the bridge Ruder-Wittman's Canadian agrifood work sits on.

## 3. Canadian institutional layer

### 3.1 FNIGC (First Nations Information Governance Centre, Ottawa)

**Founded:** 1990s. **Mandate:** First Nations information governance; OCAP® principles. **Anchor instruments:** OCAP® + FNDGS + Regional Information Governance Centres (planned under FNDGS).

**Canadian institutional context:** FNIGC is the primary Canadian Indigenous data governance institutional anchor for First Nations. AFN Resolution 30-2025 + 2025 SCA Draft On-time Resolutions call on ISC to direct resources to FNIGC. **AFN is the political arm; FNIGC is the operational data-governance arm.**

### 3.2 NCIAF (National Circle for Indigenous Agriculture and Food, Canadian institutional vehicle)

**Launched:** 13 June 2024 with FCC support. **Mandate:** Support Indigenous agriculture and food sovereignty; advance reconciliation in agriculture.

**As of 2025-2026:**
- MOU with Canadian Federation of Agriculture (CFA) signed.
- Senior director: Gabrielle Fallu (Nortera).
- President and CEO: Kallie Wood.
- Web Hub: organised into business / funding / programmes categories.

**Why this matters:** NCIAF is the **only named Indigenous-led agrifood organisation with FCC backing and national scope.** It is the Canadian institutional vehicle the framework side has been missing — FNIGC handles data governance; NCIAF handles Indigenous agrifood practice.

### 3.3 Mila Indigenous AI institutional infrastructure (Canadian academic anchor)

**Three distinct vehicles:**

- **Mila + CIFAR Indigenous AI Advisory Circle** — Six distinguished Indigenous leaders; expertise spans data sovereignty, technology. Launched 2025. *Convening function.*
- **Indigenous Pathfinders in AI** — Mila summer program. **2nd cohort (2025): 21 participants presented 8 AI prototypes at Mila on July 15, 2025.** Applications for 2026 cohort closed. *Capacity-building function.*
- **Pathways to AI: An Introduction for Indigenous** — Course launched in collaboration with the First Nations Technology Council (FNTC). *Education function.*

**Why this matters:** Mila is the substantive Canadian academic Indigenous AI institutional anchor — but the work is institutional infrastructure (convening + education + capacity-building), not standards development. **The "Mila-CSA Standard" framing in the constraint/critical scout §5 was incorrect.**

### 3.4 First Nations Technology Council (FNTC, BC-anchored)

**Partnership:** Mila + FNTC launch Pathways to AI course (October 2025). **Geographic scope:** BC First Nations; expanding national.

**Why this matters:** FNTC is the operational Indigenous technology training partner for Mila's education work.

### 3.5 AAFC Indigenous Agriculture and Food Systems Initiative (CLOSED)

**Period:** 2018-2023. **Budget:** $8.5M. **Status:** Closed. **Federal funding vehicle.**

**Why this matters:** The AAFC Indigenous Agriculture and Food Systems Initiative was the most directly relevant federal programme for Indigenous-led agrifood — and it **closed in 2023 with no successor programme named in AI for All (June 2026)**. **G-332.**

### 3.6 Indigenous Services Canada Pathway Initiative (federal funding vehicle)

**Period:** 2024-25 Call for Proposals. **Allocation:** 87 small-scale projects received $5.6M in food security funding.

**Why this matters:** ISC is the operational federal anchor for Indigenous food security funding — not AAFC. The Pathway Initiative is the federal vehicle but does not have AI-specific carve-out. **G-332.**

### 3.7 BC Indigenous Food Sovereignty funding (provincial)

**Allocation:** $8M Indigenous food security and sovereignty funding. **Announced:** 2024 (BC Ministry of Agriculture).

**Why this matters:** BC is the only province with a named Indigenous food sovereignty funding vehicle. Substantively different from AAFC Indigenous Agriculture Initiative (now closed).

### 3.8 Stats Canada Indigenous agricultural operators (demographic anchor)

**Number:** **5,160 Indigenous agricultural operators (1.9%) of 270,720 total operators. Growth: +53.7% over the comparison period** (per AAFC What We Heard Report).

**Why this matters:** The demographic base for Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployment is real and growing. The substantive deployment gap is not "no Indigenous agrifood operators" — it is "no federal Indigenous-led agrifood AI programme."

## 4. Operational deployment layer (Indigenous-led and Indigenous-partnered AI deployments)

### 4.1 PolArctic / Sanikiluaq Nunavut mariculture AI

**Lead:** PolArctic team + Inuit community of Sanikiluaq (Qikiqtaaluk Region, Nunavut, Belcher Islands).

**Approach:** Two-Eyed Seeing (Etuaptmumk). First AI to treat Indigenous Knowledge and Western science as equals. Inuktitut + English output.

**Application:** Fisheries management + climate adaptation + shoreline protection.

**Peer-reviewed:** Iravani, R., Biagi, M., Laforest, S., Lee, K., Isaacman, L., Chen, Z., An, C. (2025). *Protecting shorelines in Canadian Indigenous communities: Environmental challenges, policy interventions, and mitigation technologies*. **Marine Pollution Bulletin** 219:118310. doi:10.1016/j.marpolbul.2025.118310.

**Operational status:** Peer-reviewed in 2025; pilot completed; production mariculture status unclear. **G-335.**

### 4.2 Salmon Vision BC (Indigenous-led wild salmon AI)

**Lead:** SFU + Gitanyow Fisheries Authority + Skeena Fisheries Commission.

**Approach:** AI-integrated fish weir monitoring. Combining AI with age-old fishing weir technology.

**Peer-reviewed:** Frontiers in Marine Science 2023.

**Performance:** ~90% coho detection accuracy; ~80% sockeye detection accuracy. 12 species; 500,000+ individual video frames.

**Operational locations:** Bear River + Kitwanga River (Gitanyow) fish counting weirs, BC Central Coast.

**Forward-looking:** Stated expansion to Koeye and KwaKwa rivers (per Indigenous Innovation Initiative 2024 reporting). Operational status unclear. **G-336.**

**Public coverage:** CBC News (2024). EurekaAlert. AZoRobotics. Innovate BC. Multiple primary sources.

### 4.3 Nunavut Economic Developers Association (NEDA) AI tool

**Lead:** NEDA. **Funding:** CanNor REGI-AI. **Allocation:** $200K / 3 years.

**Operational status:** First documented Indigenous-led AI deployment with federal funding.

**Why this matters:** Despite being a small-scale deployment ($200K / 3 years), NEDA is structurally distinctive — it is **Inuit-led and federally funded through a general rural-economic-development instrument (REGI), not through an Indigenous-agrifood-specific programme.** This is the framework-deployment funding-instrument gap pattern.

### 4.4 Inuvik Tech Society digital literacy + AI training

**Lead:** Inuvik Tech Society. **Partners:** Gwich'in Settlement Region + Inuvialuit Settlement Region.

**Funding:** CanNor IDEANorth. **Allocation:** $200K / 2 years.

**Operational status:** Digital literacy + AI training. Not a deployment per se, but a capacity-building programme.

**Why this matters:** CanNor IDEANorth is the federal funding vehicle for digital literacy + AI training in Indigenous communities north of 60. **The Inuvik Tech Society project is the only documented CanNor-funded Indigenous digital literacy + AI training programme.**

### 4.5 Indigenous Pathfinders in AI 2025 cohort (Mila)

**Lead:** Mila. **Partners:** CIFAR + Indigenous mentors.

**Operational status:** **2nd cohort 2025: 21 participants presented 8 AI prototypes at Mila on July 15, 2025.**

**Specific agrifood applications:** Not publicly documented. **G-334.**

### 4.6 Why these deployments cluster: the funding-stack pattern

All four operational deployments (PolArctic, Salmon Vision, NEDA, Inuvik Tech) are funded through **non-Indigenous-specific federal instruments**:

| Deployment | Funding source | Federal programme |
|---|---|---|
| PolArctic | Mixed academic + Indigenous + International | (no single federal programme) |
| Salmon Vision BC | BCSFA + Ocean AID + DFO | (no single federal programme) |
| NEDA AI tool | CanNor REGI-AI | CanNor (general rural economic dev) |
| Inuvik Tech Society | CanNor IDEANorth | CanNor (digital literacy) |

**The deployment pattern is clear:** Indigenous-led and Indigenous-partnered AI deployments with agrifood-adjacent relevance are funded through general rural / fisheries / digital-literacy federal instruments, not through Indigenous-led-agrifood-AI-specific programmes. **G-313 (real gap).**

## 5. Critical-analytical-voice layer

### 5.1 Sarah-Louise Ruder (University of Ottawa, SSHRC PDF)

**Current position:** SSHRC Postdoctoral Fellow, University of Ottawa, School of Sociological and Anthropological Studies. PhD UBC (with Hannah Wittman).

**Substantive papers:**

- Ruder, S.-L. & Wittman, H. (2025). *Agricultural data governance from the ground up: Exploring data justice with agri-food movements*. **Big Data & Society**, July-December 2025. **Cited 22 times.** 40 workshops + 26 conferences, 2020-2023, with farmers + farming organizations + government policy + civil society + academic researchers across Western Canada, US, Latin America.
- Ruder, S.-L. (2025). *Theorizing agricultural data policy and governance: The political economy of (not) regulating datafication in Canadian agriculture*. **Journal of Peasant Studies**. **Cited 14 times.** Sole author.
- Duncan, E., Ruder, S.-L., et al. (2026). *The political economy of agricultural data governance*. **Journal of Rural Studies**. **Cited 6 times.**

**Toolkit:** BCACARN Ethical Data Governance Toolkit (2024). *First documented Canadian agrifood ethical data governance toolkit.*

**Substantive contribution:** Data-justice framing of Canadian agrifood data governance. Engages CARE principles explicitly. The 40-workshop research design is the most comprehensive Canadian-context empirical study of agrifood data governance.

### 5.2 Hannah Wittman (UBC, Faculty of Land and Food Systems + IRES)

**Current position:** Professor, Faculty of Land and Food Systems, UBC. Member, Institute for Resources, Environment and Sustainability (IRES).

**Substantive papers (agrifood-adjacent):**

- Wittman, H. et al. (2022). *Land governance for agroecology*. **Elementa: Science of the Anthropocene** 10:1, 00100.
- Wittman, H. et al. (2023). *Food sovereignty: An inclusive model for feeding the world and cooling the planet*. **One Earth**.

**Substantive contribution:** Food sovereignty framing; rights-of-nature integration; agroecology. Distinct from the data-governance framing Ruder brings.

### 5.3 Why this analytical voice matters

**Substantive observation:** Ruder (U Ottawa postdoc, PhD UBC) and Wittman (UBC IRES) are the substantive Canadian agrifood data-governance analytical voice. The existing corpus's existing critical voices are:

- **Neethirajan** (Dalhousie) — AI-deployed-on-the-farm voice (Policy Options January 2026 "Canada's AI strategy stops at city limits"; Frontiers Sustainable Food Systems April 2026 "Green AI for climate-smart dairy and poultry").
- **Dara** (U Guelph AI4Food director) — greenhouse AI risk.
- **Charlebois** (Dalhousie Agri-Food Analytics Lab) — pragmatic-critical ("the problem isn't AI, it's data").

**These are all AI-deployment critical voices. Ruder and Wittman are the *data-governance critical voices* — substantively different.** Their data-justice framing and CARE principles engagement is the analytical voice the field guide's critical-lens Indigenous sovereignty archetype needs.

## 6. International comparative anchors

### 6.1 Aotearoa New Zealand — Te Mana Raraunga (Māori Data Sovereignty Network)

**Formed:** 2015. **Position:** Māori Data Sovereignty / Māori Data Governance.

**Substantive observation:** "Data that are collected about Indigenous people should be subjected to the laws of the nation from which it is collected, including tribal nations" (Lovett et al. 2019).

### 6.2 Peru — ONAMIAP + Indigenous Navigator

**Lead organisations:** ONAMIAP (Organización Nacional de Mujeres Indígenas Andinas y Amazónicas del Perú) + IWGIA (International Work Group for Indigenous Affairs).

**Period:** 2023-2025. **Funding:** European Union.

**Concrete outcomes:** Documented collective rights challenges using Indigenous Navigator framework; identified priorities linked to water scarcity, territorial integrity, food sovereignty, local governance; municipal ordinance advocacy; specific community initiatives supported (water conservation, protection of natural springs, reforestation, fish farming, seed recovery, territorial mapping).

**Why this matters:** ONAMIAP-IWGIA Indigenous Navigator Peru is the operational anchor for IDSov-in-agrifood in Latin America; the Canadian cycle should reference as comparative anchor.

### 6.3 US land-grant academic — Cornell Atkinson IDSov cluster (Manaʻoakamai Johnson + Michael Charles)

See `units/cornell-atkinson-idsov-cluster.md` (existing). US land-grant academic IDSov anchor; distinct from the Canadian institutional layer (FNIGC + NCIAF + Mila).

### 6.4 Sámí — SODA Principles

Sámi Ownership and Data Access (SODA) Principles — the operational Sámí data governance framework, hosted at the Collaboratory for Indigenous Data Governance (indigenousdatalab.org/care-data-maturity-model).

## 7. The framework-deployment gap (substantive finding)

| Layer | Substantive presence in Canada | Substantive gap |
|---|---|---|
| Framework (international) | Strong (CARE, IEEE 2890, OCAP, FNDGS, IQ) | None substantive |
| Canadian institutional layer | Moderate (FNIGC, NCIAF, Mila Indigenous AI infrastructure, FNTC) | No Indigenous-led-agrifood-AI-specific federal programme (G-313) |
| Operational deployment | Moderate (PolArctic, Salmon Vision, NEDA, Inuvik Tech, Indigenous Pathfinders) | All deployments funded through non-Indigenous-specific instruments (G-313) |
| Critical-analytical voice | Strong (Ruder, Wittman, BCACARN toolkit) | None substantive — but Ruder's SSHRC PDF is the most substantive single voice |

**The gap is structural and funding-instrument-specific, not framework-substantive or analytical-voice-substantive.**
