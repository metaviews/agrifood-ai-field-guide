# Canada Value-Chain Matrix Cycle — July 2026

**Status.** Second of three Canada-deepening scans. The first (funder/convenor substrate) is committed; the third (regional substrates) is pending. Focus: the **value-chain matrix cells** that are substantively populated by the CAAIN portfolio but have **zero corpus coverage** — animal production (dairy, meat, poultry, bees), specialty (mushroom), horticulture (orchard), and post-harvest (grain grading).

**Scope.** This scan consolidates the CAAIN portfolio projects by matrix cell and writes one unit per cell. The matrix-cell focus matches the corpus's existing scan pattern (FCC cycle, Quebec cycle, regional scan) where each scan populates multiple matrix cells with substantive evidence.

**Why this scan now.** The CAAIN portfolio (committed in Scan 1's `units/caain-portfolio-canada.md`) is the largest single uncovered substantive content surface in the Canadian agrifood AI picture. The portfolio populates matrix cells that the existing corpus has been *thin on* (animal production) or *empty on* (bees, mushroom, orchard, grain grading). Writing matrix-cell units lets every downstream Canadian cycle cite these specific value-chain deployments cleanly.

**Method.** Primary sources for every load-bearing claim. Each cell's unit compresses 1–3 CAAIN projects into a corpus cell with matrix-cell evidence. Where the cell is anchored by a non-CAAIN actor (e.g., Geco Predictive Weed Control for prairie grain AI; Vineland robotic harvester for Ontario horticulture), the unit cites that actor as the primary anchor.

**Cutoff.** Active in the last 12 months (July 2025 – July 2026). Older instruments included only when load-bearing.

The four CAAIN dairy projects together total **$5.33M** in project value with **$1.39M CAAIN contribution**. Spans ON, NS, AB.

### 2.3 CATTLEytics detail (verified)

CATTLEytics Inc. (Hamilton ON) is a 2014 spin-off of Feedlot Health Management Services (FHMS), which was acquired by TELUS Agriculture. CEO Dr. Shari Van de Pol (DVM, McMaster Computer Engineering + Fine Arts minor, IBM, Ontario Veterinary College). Equipment-agnostic dairy platform with digital twinning, predictive modelling, AI chat interface, dosing bridge gun. John Clark (co-developer) died Sept 2024. The system supports mastitis/metritis treatment recommendations with photographs and dosage details. **The system ingests and derives insights from data collected by virtually any other dairy-sector technology** — distinctive *equipment-agnostic* posture. CATTLEytics has not sought outside investors. Sources: CAAIN project page + Dr. Van de Pol's profile.

### 2.4 SomaDetect detail (verified)

SomaDetect (Tillsonburg ON) uses light-scattering + ML for cow-specific real-time milk analysis. Targets TRL 8+. Detects subclinical ketosis + Energy-Corrected Milk (ECM). Partner: Walker Dairy. Competing systems (robotic-farm in-line analyzers) consume costly reagents. SomaDetect's solution is "scalable, AI-powered" and "agnostic to milking system." Source: CAAIN project page.

### 2.5 Milk Moovement detail (verified)

Milk Moovement (Halifax NS) is a cloud-based dairy supply-chain software company. $20M USD Series A round (2022) led by VMG Catalyst. AI tool "Moove" uses ML and optimization algorithms to autonomously generate optimal routing schedules that match real-time milk supply with processor demand. Targets processor-throughput improvement and hauling-cost reduction for dairy cooperatives. **The price processors pay for dairy products depends not only on volume but on components** (protein, butterfat, bacteria). Currently the standard practice is copying supply amounts from the previous week — Moove replaces that. Sources: CAAIN project page + LinkedIn post + Crunchbase news.

### 2.6 Lakeland College Precision Ranching detail (verified)

Lakeland College (Vermilion AB) — Precision Ranching for Improved Reproductive and Grazing Efficiencies. CAAIN $143K / total $1.30M. Spans AB + MB + BC ranches. Uses rumen bolus (temperature + movement → estrus prediction), GPS ear tags (BC forest grazing — drone monitoring of "harsher terrain"), ultrasound for heifer reproductive-tract maturity, and **LlamaZOO** (BC-based software company) for digital twin dashboard. Industry partners pay for the technology being tested on their animals but recover 40% of costs through CAAIN funding. Lakeland's Agricultural Technology degree programme is the workforce pipeline.

### 2.7 What the dairy unit captures

A dedicated `units/canadian-dairy-ai.md` would consolidate these four projects + SoraLINK (existing Quebec cycle scan) into a single corpus cell. The cell would cover the *full dairy value chain*: production (CATTLEytics, SomaDetect, Lakeland), processing (SoraLINK), and logistics (Milk Moovement). **Field-guide gap:** Canadian dairy is the *first* value chain where CAAIN projects + existing units cover the full chain. This is worth tracking.

### 3.3 P&P Optica detail (verified)

P&P Optica (Waterloo ON). Hyperspectral imaging + ML for meat quality assessment (fat, water, protein, freshness) + foreign-material detection. ~**20 systems installed across pork and poultry operations in North America**. Founders: Romek Pawluczyk (Polish photonics/spectroscopy; one of PPO's spectrometers is on the International Space Station) and Olga Pawluczyk (CEO since 2005). Initial work: recycling industry + oil & gas (bitumen analysis). Pivoted to meat in 2015 after meat processors asked if PPO equipment could differentiate meat from fat. PPO's systems are deployed on the way out in primary processing facilities and on the way in to secondary processors. The CAAIN-funded Phase 3 work focuses on the *primary meat producer* side — telling farmers what their on-farm practices are yielding. Sources: CAAIN project page + Olga Pawluczyk interview.

### 3.4 mode40 detail (verified)

mode40 (Steinbach MB). Meat Quality Management (MQM) system — AI + mobile + smart sensors for carcass cooling real-time adjustment. Born out of an Innovation Solutions Canada Challenge. After MVP, mode40 moves to **AAFC's Lacombe Research and Development Centre** for further development, then 1-year validation in 10 commercial meat processing plants. Cameron Bergen (Co-Founder and CEO): "I would advise the client to opt for a six-month solution that can elevate it to an 8 rather than waiting for a two-year process to achieve a slightly higher score of 9." mode40 team grew from 2 to 5 to 8 staff + planned 14. Sources: CAAIN project page.

### 3.5 Circulus Agtech detail (verified)

Circulus Agtech Solutions Inc. (Montréal QC). Modular on-farm manure treatment system for Quebec pork. Biological stabilization + solid-liquid separation + membrane filtration → nitrate-rich biofertilizers + stabilized solids + reusable clean water. Closed-loop nutrient recovery. Active 2026. Source: CAAIN project page.

### 3.6 What the meat processing unit captures

A dedicated `units/canadian-meat-processing-ai.md` would consolidate these three projects into the meat processing cell. The cell would cover **meat quality + carcass cooling + closed-loop nutrient recovery** as distinct processing patterns. P&P Optica's ~20 systems across North America make it a substantive deployment-scale anchor; mode40's transition from MVP to AAFC Lacombe to 10 commercial plants is the *full development path*; Circulus Agtech's closed-loop nutrient recovery is a *circular-economy* AI pattern.

### 4.3 Chick Pick Solutions detail (verified)

Chick Pick Solutions Inc. (Moncton NB). Automates sorting day-old chicks by sex. Two production-grade systems at a commercial layer hatchery in Manitoba + commercial broiler hatchery in Ontario. Earlier prototypes supported by Mitacs, IRAP, NB Department of Agriculture. Test bench system at Fredericton NB facility. Ryan Whitney (Co-Founder). Source: CAAIN project page.

Farm Health Guardian (Guelph ON). HPAI + Newcastle + aMPV + ILT disease mapping and prediction. Real-time transport biosecurity breach alerts + GPS. Partner: Feather Board Command Centre Hamilton ON (Ontario poultry four-commodity database for "eggs, broilers, turkeys, hatching egg farms"). Rob Hannam (Founder and CEO). Source: CAAIN project page.

### 4.5 MatrixSpec Hyper-Eye detail (verified)

MatrixSpec Solutions Inc. (Baie-D'Urfé QC). Hyper-Eye hyperspectral imaging AI determines egg fertility + chick gender *pre-incubation*, eliminating culling of male chicks. Scale: from 6,000 eggs/day to 10,000. Dr. Michael Ngadi (McGill James McGill Professor, 25 years; born in Nigeria, Government of Canada scholarship 1991). **Critical claim:** *"Around the world the number climbs to roughly seven billion a year [male chicks culled annually]. The process, while necessary, is emotionally demanding of the workers involved, not to mention costly, wasteful, and bad for the environment. If hyperspectral imaging can be used to determine which eggs should be kept and which should be disposed of before they hatch, the impact will be extraordinary."* Partners: Egg Farmers of Ontario Mississauga ON, McKinley Hatchery St Marys ON. Source: CAAIN project page.

### 4.6 Cross-cutting non-CAAIN poultry AI

**Targan WingScan** — US vendor deployed at Quebec hatcheries. WingScan processes **30M+ chicks/week worldwide** with 97–99% accuracy (early 2026 figure). Distinct from CAAIN projects but worth knowing for the Canadian context. Sources: Wattagnet, Canadian Poultry Magazine, Farmtario, TARGAN IPPE 2026.

### 4.7 What the poultry unit captures

## 9. Cross-cutting substantive observations

### 9.1 The matrix-cell population pattern

Reading across §2–§8, the CAAIN portfolio populates **6 previously-thin or empty matrix cells** with substantive Canadian evidence. The pattern is distinctive:

- **Animal production** was the thinnest cell pre-cycle. Post-cycle: dairy (4 projects), meat (3 projects), poultry (3 projects + Targan), bees (1 project). **Animal production is now substantively populated for the first time.**
- **Post-harvest grain grading** was empty pre-cycle. Post-cycle: Super GeoAI + Grain Discovery. **The cell is now substantively populated.**
- **Mushroom and orchard** were thin pre-cycle. Post-cycle: 4AG Robotics + CropVue + Vivid Machines. **The cells are now substantively populated.**

### 9.2 The processing-cell cross-protein observation

The Canadian processing cell is now substantively populated *across animal proteins, not just dairy*:

- **Dairy processing:** SoraLINK × Saputo/Olymel/Agropur (existing Quebec cycle scan).
- **Meat processing:** P&P Optica + mode40 (this scan).
- **Poultry processing:** Chick Pick + Farm Health Guardian + MatrixSpec Hyper-Eye + Targan WingScan (this scan + parallel scout).
- **Bees / pollination:** Nectar BeeTrack (this scan).
- **Closed-loop nutrient recovery:** Circulus Agtech (this scan).

The corpus can now cite the **processing cell across 5 protein categories** with named Canadian deployments. This is the substantive cross-cycle finding: **Canadian processing AI is now multi-protein, not single-protein.**

### 9.3 The founder-pattern observation (carry-over from CAAIN unit)

Many CAAIN project leads are second-generation Canadian agtech founders whose families are named in the project narrative: Van de Pol (CATTLEytics), Pawluczyk (P&P Optica), Baresich (Haggerty AgRobotics), Bergen (mode40), Zeng (Super GeoAI), Ngadi (MatrixSpec), Roberge (Nectar). The CAAIN programme surfaces a specific Canadian founder pattern: *immigrant or multi-generation agricultural family × technical training × federal funding as the leveller*.

### 9.4 The "ag" before "tech" framing (Nectar Roberge)

Marc-André Roberge (Nectar Technologies): "It's called 'agtech' rather than 'tech-ag.' The 'ag' part comes first. You have to take into account the needs of the producers." This framing is worth carrying into talks — *Canadian agtech is producer-led, not technology-led*, structurally distinct from US private-venture agtech which can be technology-led.

### 9.5 The animal-welfare observation (MatrixSpec 7-billion-chick culling claim)

MatrixSpec's claim that "7 billion day-old male chicks are culled annually worldwide" — and that pre-hatch hyperspectral imaging AI could eliminate this — is **the most consequential animal-welfare AI claim available in the corpus**. Worth tracking as a structural observation: Canadian AI in the poultry value chain has substantive animal-welfare implications.

### 9.6 The closed-loop circular-economy observation (Circulus Agtech)

Circulus Agtech's modular on-farm manure treatment system for Quebec pork — biological stabilization + solid-liquid separation + membrane filtration → nitrate-rich biofertilizers + stabilized solids + reusable clean water — is a *closed-loop nutrient recovery* AI pattern. Distinct from generic processing AI. The cell's circular-economy framing is structurally distinct from typical Canadian processing AI.
