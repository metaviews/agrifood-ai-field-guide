# Canada Indigenous Data Sovereignty + AI in Agrifood — Consolidation Scout

## 1. The substantive finding (load-bearing)

**The Canadian agrifood AI ecosystem has a substantive operational deployment pattern from Indigenous-led and Indigenous-partnered organisations that is now *ahead of* the Indigenous-led funding instrument.** The constraint/critical scout §4.3 states "No documented Indigenous-led AI agrifood deployment in Canada as of July 2026" — but the regional substrate cycle (July 2026) surfaced:

- **PolArctic / Sanikiluaq Nunavut mariculture AI** — Inuit-led, Inuktitut + English output, "two-eyed seeing" integrating Indigenous Knowledge and Western science as equals.
- **Salmon Vision BC** — Gitanyow Fisheries Authority + Skeena Fisheries Commission + SFU, Frontiers in Marine Science 2023 peer-reviewed, 12 species, 500,000+ frames, ~90% coho accuracy, ~80% sockeye accuracy (CBC reporting).
- **Nunavut Economic Developers Association AI tool** — first documented Indigenous-led AI deployment with federal funding (CanNor REGI-AI, $200K / 3 years).
- **Inuvik Tech Society digital literacy + AI training** — CanNor IDEANorth, $200K / 2 years, Gwich'in Settlement Region and Inuvialuit Settlement Region.
- **Iravani et al. 2025 (Marine Pollution Bulletin) review** explicitly cites PolArctic's Inuit Knowledge + AI integration as an exemplary case for Indigenous shoreline protection.

These are **peer-reviewed, federally-recognised, or community-anchored operational deployments.** The framework side is rich (CARE, IEEE 2890-2025, OCAP, FNDGS, NCIAF) — but **none of the operational deployments are funded through an Indigenous-led-agrifood-AI-specific federal programme.** They're funded through marine/fisheries/sustainable-infrastructure/rural-connectivity instruments (DFO, BCSFA + Ocean AID, CanNor).

**G-313 (no federal Indigenous-led agrifood AI funder) remains the structural gap. The deployments exist; the funder does not.**

## 2. The framework side — what the corpus now has

The existing `units/indigenous-data-sovereignty.md` already covers:

- CARE Principles (Carroll et al. 2020, Gaborone 2018, 1,808 citations; translations: Spanish, Vietnamese, Māori, German, Khmer).
- IEEE 2890-2025 (Approved September 2025; published November 2025; world's first global standard for provenance of Indigenous data; explicitly applies to AI/ML industries).
- OCAP® (FNIGC, since 2010).
- GIDA 2025 "CARE Directs Us Home" communiqué (caution against treating CARE as a static compliance checklist).
- Operational anchors in Peru (ONAMIAP-IWGIA Indigenous Navigator), Aotearoa NZ (Te Mana Raraunga), Mexico (network anchored), US land-grant (Cornell Atkinson IDSov cluster with Manaʻoakamai Johnson and Michael Charles).

**What's substantively new since that unit was last updated (mid-July 2026):**

### 2.1 Mila's Indigenous AI institutional infrastructure (Canadian substantive find)

The assumption "Mila-CSA Standard" was a single joint standard is **incorrect**. Mila's substantive Indigenous AI institutional infrastructure comprises three distinct vehicles, all 2024-2026:

- **Mila + CIFAR Indigenous AI Advisory Circle** — Six distinguished Indigenous leaders; expertise spans data sovereignty, technology. Launched 2025. *Convening function* (not a standard).
- **Indigenous Pathfinders in AI** — Mila summer program. **2025 cohort (2nd cohort): 21 participants presented 8 AI prototypes at Mila on July 15, 2025**. *Capacity-building function.* Applications for 2026 cohort closed.
- **Pathways to AI: An Introduction for Indigenous** — Course launched in collaboration with the First Nations Technology Council (FNTC). *Education function.* Partnership announced via LinkedIn October 2025.

This is **operationally distinct from a CSA standard**. Mila's Indigenous AI work is institutional infrastructure (convening + education + capacity-building), not standards development. **The cycle should treat Mila as the substantive Canadian academic Indigenous AI institutional anchor, not as a CSA joint-committee.**

### 2.2 UNESCO Indigenous Data Sovereignty in AI Report (international framework)

UNESCO has published new report and guidelines for Indigenous data sovereignty in artificial intelligence developments. This is the **multilateral-institutional anchor for Indigenous data sovereignty + AI** at international scale. Anchors CARE + IEEE 2890-2025 in the UN system alongside UNDRIP Article 3.

### 2.3 First Nations Data Governance Strategy (FNDGS, 2025)

FNIGC + regional partners submitted the FNDGS to Indigenous Services Canada in 2025 (AFN Resolution 30-2025). Plans regional information governance centres (one per region). The strategy is **community-driven, Nation-based, and is the most authoritative Canadian operational framework for Indigenous data governance at scale**. Distinct from OCAP (the principles) and IEEE 2890 (the industry specification).

### 2.4 NCIAF — National Circle for Indigenous Agriculture and Food (Canadian institutional vehicle)

Launched 13 June 2024 with FCC support. **The only named Indigenous-led agrifood organisation with FCC backing and national scope**. As of 2025-2026: **MOU with Canadian Federation of Agriculture (CFA)** signed; senior director Gabrielle Fallu; institutional presence on LinkedIn / Instagram / NCIAF Web Hub. Mission: support Indigenous agriculture and food sovereignty; advance reconciliation in agriculture.

NCIAF is **structurally distinct from framework organisations** — it's the operational arm for Indigenous-led agrifood that the framework side has been missing.

### 2.5 Indigenous Agriculture Data definition (Jennings et al. 2025)

From "Safeguarding Traditions, Enhancing Agriculture" (Jennings, Lameman, Martinez, Cummins, Wiipongwii, Arcand, Moore, Lee, Nuvamsa, Plenty Grass-She Kills, Gazing Wolf, Carroll. 2025. Tucson: Collaboratory for Indigenous Data Governance. DOI: 10.6084/m9.figshare.30432850):

> **Indigenous Agriculture Data**: knowledge and information generated by Indigenous Peoples' relationship to land and their food systems through observations, storytelling, oral histories, community protocols, seasonal and ecological changes, and other culturally grounded knowledge, reflecting their values and care of the land, community, and all living beings (Jennings et al., 2025).

This is the first explicit definition of Indigenous Agriculture Data. The brief is **US-focused** (USDA, NSF, Native American Agriculture Fund, Intertribal Agriculture Council, Tribal Agriculture Fellowship) but the definition is structurally applicable to the Canadian context.

### 2.6 Indigenous Food Sovereignty framework (consolidated definitions)

The framework vocabulary is now well-articulated across GIDA + Jennings et al. 2025 + Maudrie et al. 2021 + Rowe et al. 2024 + Whyte 2016:

- **Indigenous Data Sovereignty (IDSov)**: rights of Indigenous Peoples to govern the collection, ownership, and application of their own data.
- **Indigenous Data Governance (IDGov)**: policies and practices that support Indigenous Peoples in applying IDSov.
- **Indigenous Food Sovereignty (IFS)**: right of Indigenous Peoples to define and control their own food systems, based on land stewardship, self-determination, Indigenous values and practices.
- **Indigenous Food Systems**: complex and dynamic interactions between social, economic, and environmental actors involved in Indigenous food production, distribution, consumption, cultivation, harvest, and disposal (Kuhnlein & Chotinboriboon 2022).
- **Indigenous Agriculture Practitioners**: a variety of actors including seed keepers, commercial producers, ranchers, agriculture enumerators, those working in Indigenous food sovereignty, and those working in data optimization of agriculture production (Jennings et al., 2025; Bureau of Labor Statistics, 2025).

### 2.7 The "two-eyed seeing" operational pattern

PolArctic's Inuktitut + English output with Indigenous Knowledge and Western science treated as equals is the operational pattern the framework side is only beginning to codify. **Albert Marshall's "Two-Eyed Seeing" (Etuaptmumk / Tla'qoqme'wa'kl jij'ue'j)** is the conceptual framework; PolArctic is its operational AI implementation. The brief and the framework documents reference it but the deployments operationalise it without naming it as such.

### 2.8 Ruder-Wittman analytical voice (Canadian agrifood data governance)

**Sarah-Louise Ruder** (SSHRC Postdoctoral Fellow, University of Ottawa School of Sociological and Anthropological Studies) and **Hannah Wittman** (UBC Faculty of Land and Food Systems, IRES) are the substantive Canadian analytical voice on agrifood data governance. Their work explicitly engages CARE principles, Indigenous data sovereignty, and food sovereignty movements.

Key papers:
- **Ruder, S.-L. & Wittman, H. (2025). Agricultural data governance from the ground up: Exploring data justice with agri-food movements. *Big Data & Society*, July-December 2025.** 40 workshops + 26 conferences, 2020-2023, with farmers + farming organizations + government policy + civil society + academic researchers across Western Canada, US, Latin America. *Cited 22 times*. **Substantively the most important Canadian-context agrifood data governance paper in the cycle.**
- Ruder, S.-L. (2025). Theorizing agricultural data policy and governance. *Journal of Peasant Studies*. Cited 14 times. Ruder is sole author (her PhD work).
- Duncan, E. et al. (2026, including Ruder). The political economy of agricultural data governance. *Journal of Rural Studies*. Cited 6 times.
- **BCACARN Ethical Data Governance Toolkit (2024)** — Dr. Ruder + Dr. Wittman research instrument. **First documented Canadian agrifood ethical data governance toolkit.**

**Substantive observation:** Ruder is now at University of Ottawa (post-PhD, post-UBC); Wittman is at UBC. **The analytical voice has moved from UBC (where Wittman's IRES group operates) to U Ottawa where Ruder is SSHRC PDF.** This is the kind of pattern that warrants ecosystem mapping per the project memory ("when work surfaces a substantive analytical voice, user wants the ecosystem around them mapped at four layers").

## 3. The deployment side — what the corpus now has

The Canada deepening cycle populated this substantively:

- **PolArctic Sanikiluaq Nunavut mariculture AI** — peer-reviewed in Iravani et al. 2025 (Marine Pollution Bulletin, "Protecting shorelines in Canadian Indigenous communities"). Indigenous Knowledge + AI + scientific data integration for fisheries management and climate adaptation. Inuktitut + English output. Sanikiluaq is a community in the Qikiqtaaluk Region of Nunavut on the Belcher Islands.
- **Salmon Vision BC** — SFU + Gitanyow Fisheries Authority + Skeena Fisheries Commission. *Frontiers in Marine Science* 2023 (peer-reviewed). 500,000+ individual video frames. ~90% coho accuracy / ~80% sockeye accuracy (CBC reporting, 2024). Two Indigenous-run fish counting weirs on BC's Central Coast (Bear River + Kitwanga River Gitanyow). Currently expanding to Koeye and KwaKwa rivers.
- **Nunavut Economic Developers Association AI tool** — Inuit-led; CanNor REGI-AI; $200K / 3 years. First documented Indigenous-led AI deployment with federal funding.
- **Inuvik Tech Society** — CanNor IDEANorth; $200K / 2 years; digital literacy + AI training for Gwich'in Settlement Region and Inuvialuit Settlement Region.

## 4. The structural finding — the framework-deployment gap

**The Canadian agrifood AI ecosystem has:**
- **Framework density (international + national + Indigenous-nation):** CARE (international, 1,808 citations), IEEE 2890-2025 (international industry standard), OCAP (Canadian First Nations), FNDGS (Canadian First Nations national strategy), NCIAF (Canadian institutional vehicle), National Inuit Strategy on Research (Canadian Inuit), Inuit Qaujimajangit (Nunavut government), Collaboratory for Indigenous Data Governance (international research infrastructure), Mila Indigenous AI Advisory Circle + Indigenous Pathfinders in AI + Pathways to AI with FNTC (Canadian academic infrastructure).
- **Operational deployment density:** PolArctic + Salmon Vision + CanNor-funded projects + Inuvik Tech. **All operational deployments are funded through non-Indigenous-specific federal instruments (DFO, CanNor, BCSFA + Ocean AID, BCSRIF, WWF-Arctic, Qikiqtaaluk).**
- **Substantive analytical voice:** Ruder (U Ottawa) + Wittman (UBC). Data justice framing; CARE principles engagement; 40 workshops across Western Canada, US, Latin America.

**What's missing is a federal or provincial programme specifically funding Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployment.** The AAFC Indigenous Agriculture and Food Systems Initiative ($8.5M, 2018-2023) closed; the AI for All strategy names agriculture as a priority sector but does not carve out Indigenous-led agrifood AI. **This is G-313 from the constraint/critical scout.**

## 5. Why this matters for talks

For archetype 05 (critical-lens Indigenous sovereignty), the substantive upgrade is:

**Old claim:** "Indigenous data sovereignty frameworks exist (CARE, IEEE 2890, OCAP); operational deployments are sparse."

**New claim:** "Canadian Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployments *do* exist — PolArctic, Salmon Vision, CanNor-funded projects — but **they are funded through non-Indigenous-specific instruments** (DFO, CanNor, BCSFA, BCSRIF). The framework is rich; the dedicated funder does not exist. The deployments are doing what FNDGS / CARE call for, but the funding mechanism is incidental, not designed."

For archetype 02 (data-sovereignty Canadian), the substantive upgrade is:

**New claim:** "Salmon Vision and PolArctic are data-sovereignty-by-design deployments — community-stewarded, community-anchored, peer-reviewed. They sit at the same level of data-sovereignty-architecture as JoinData (Netherlands) or NCIA (Australia), but unlike JoinData/NCIA they don't have a federally-supported institutional vehicle behind them."

For archetype 06 (regional-cluster-comparison), the substantive upgrade is:

**New claim:** "Canada's Indigenous-led AI cluster is structurally distinct from the EU's cooperative-cluster model and the US's land-grant-extension model. It is *deployment-driven and infrastructure-anchored in Indigenous organisations* (FNIGC, NCIAF, PolArctic, Salmon Vision, Nunavut Economic Developers Association) rather than funder-anchored. This is a fourth operational model worth naming."

## 9. Substantive gaps surfaced (carry through to talks as honesty)

- **G-313:** No federal or provincial programme specifically funds Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployment. *Real gap, structural.*

- **G-330:** No Canadian agrifood-data-governance framework explicitly engaging Indigenous Data Sovereignty and Indigenous Food Sovereignty as integrated principles. (Ruder-Wittman work engages both separately; integrated framework absent.)
- **G-331:** No Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployment in Quebec (Mila's Indigenous AI infrastructure is Quebec-anchored but deployments are not documented in agrifood specifically).
- **G-332:** AAFC Indigenous Agriculture and Food Systems Initiative ($8.5M, 2018-2023) closed; no successor programme named in AI for All (June 2026).
- **G-333:** Indigenous-led agrifood AI readiness assessment for First Nations, Inuit, or Métis agrifood operators not published.
