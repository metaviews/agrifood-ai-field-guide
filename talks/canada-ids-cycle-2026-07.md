# Canada Indigenous Data Sovereignty + AI in Agrifood cycle — talk-ready findings (July 2026)

**Status.** Cycle-level consolidation of substantive findings for talk-building. The 6 new units each have their own "Why this matters for talks" section (load-bearing); the consolidation scout §1 carries the headline finding; this document collapses them into a single navigable talk-ready index.

**What changed in this cycle.** The Canadian corpus previously had:
- A substantive global IDSov unit (`units/indigenous-data-sovereignty.md`) with CARE / IEEE 2890-2025 / OCAP framework + Peru/Aotearoa/Mexico operational anchors.
- A US land-grant academic IDSov unit (`units/cornell-atkinson-idsov-cluster.md`).
- A constraint/critical scout §4 that **asserted "No documented Indigenous-led AI agrifood deployment in Canada as of July 2026"** — the deepening cycle's regional substrate scan had surfaced PolArctic + Salmon Vision + CanNor projects, but these were not integrated into the IDSov cell.

**This cycle populates the Canadian-specific agrifood IDSov cell substantively.** Six new units + 1 unit update + 2 new quote files + 1 quote date correction. The substantive finding:

**The Canadian agrifood AI ecosystem has substantive operational deployments from Indigenous-led and Indigenous-partnered organisations that are *ahead of* the dedicated federal funding instrument for Indigenous-led agrifood AI.** PolArctic (peer-reviewed 2025) + Salmon Vision (peer-reviewed 2023) + NEDA AI tool + Inuvik Tech Society + Indigenous Pathfinders in AI (Mila 2025 cohort) are operational. They are funded through non-Indigenous-specific federal instruments (DFO, CanNor, BCSFA, BCSRIF). The framework side is rich (CARE, IEEE 2890-2025, OCAP, FNDGS, NCIAF, IQ); the dedicated Indigenous-led-agrifood-AI funder does not exist. **G-313.**

---

## 1. The framework-deployment funding-instrument gap (headline for critical-lens)

**The Canadian Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployments are funded through general rural / fisheries / digital-literacy federal instruments — not through an Indigenous-led-agrifood-AI-specific federal programme.**

| Deployment | Funding source | Federal programme |
|---|---|---|
| PolArctic Sanikiluaq | Mixed academic + Indigenous + International | (no single federal programme) |
| Salmon Vision BC | BCSFA + Ocean AID + DFO | (no single federal programme) |
| NEDA AI tool | CanNor REGI-AI ($200K / 3 years) | CanNor (general rural economic dev) |
| Inuvik Tech Society | CanNor IDEANorth ($200K / 2 years) | CanNor (digital literacy) |

**Talk-ready claims.**
- "Canadian Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployments are operational — PolArctic, Salmon Vision, NEDA, Inuvik Tech — but funded through general rural / fisheries / digital-literacy federal instruments, not through Indigenous-led-agrifood-AI-specific programmes."
- "The framework is rich (CARE, IEEE 2890-2025, OCAP, FNDGS, IQ); the dedicated funder does not exist. The deployments are doing what FNDGS / CARE call for, but the funding mechanism is incidental, not designed."
- "AAFC Indigenous Agriculture and Food Systems Initiative ($8.5M, 2018-2023) closed with no successor programme named in AI for All (June 2026)."

**Anchor units.** `units/canadian-indigenous-data-sovereignty-agrifood.md`, `units/polarctic-sanikiluaq-mariculture-ai.md`, `units/salmon-vision-bc-wild-salmon-ai.md`, `units/fnigc-first-nations-data-governance-strategy.md`.

## 2. PolArctic Sanikiluaq — Two-Eyed Seeing operational pattern (headline for critical-lens)

**PolArctic is the first AI to operationalise Two-Eyed Seeing (Etuaptmumk) for a Canadian Inuit community.** Inuktitut + English output; Indigenous Knowledge and Western science integrated as equals. Peer-reviewed in *Marine Pollution Bulletin* 2025.

**Substantive observation:** The framework side articulates CARE Principles (Authority to Control, Collective Benefit, Ethics, Responsibility); PolArctic operationalises each principle through community ownership, economic development, multilingual output, and community accountability. **The deployment does what the framework articulates but does not name the framework as implementation reference.** This is the framework-deployment operationalisation pattern — frameworks articulate; deployments operationalise; alignment is implicit, not explicit.

**Talk-ready claims.**
- "PolArctic is the first AI to operationalise Two-Eyed Seeing for a Canadian Inuit community — Inuktitut + English output; Indigenous Knowledge and Western science integrated as equals."
- "PolArctic is peer-reviewed in Marine Pollution Bulletin 2025 — substantive empirical case, not aspirational framing."
- "PolArctic operationalises the CARE Principles' 'Authority to Control' through Inuit community ownership — without naming the framework."

**Anchor unit.** `units/polarctic-sanikiluaq-mariculture-ai.md`.

**Archetypes.** 05 — primary; 02 — secondary.

## 3. Salmon Vision BC — Indigenous-led wild salmon AI (headline for data-sovereignty + regional-cluster-comparison)

**Salmon Vision integrates AI with centuries-old Gitanyow fishing weir technology.** SFU + Gitanyow Fisheries Authority + Skeena Fisheries Commission. Peer-reviewed in *Frontiers in Marine Science* 2023. ~90% coho detection accuracy; ~80% sockeye accuracy. 500,000+ frames annotated.

**Talk-ready claims.**
- "Salmon Vision is data-sovereignty by design — Indigenous-led methodology (Gitanyow fish weir + Indigenous Knowledge) integrated with cutting-edge AI (computer vision, 500,000+ frames)."
- "Frontiers in Marine Science 2023 peer review + 90% coho accuracy puts Indigenous-led wild salmon AI on par with vendor-deployed aquaculture AI — and the Indigenous-led deployment outperforms on community accountability."
- "Salmon Vision is funded through BCSFA + Ocean AID + DFO — no single Indigenous-led federal programme."

**Anchor unit.** `units/salmon-vision-bc-wild-salmon-ai.md`.

**Archetypes.** 05 — primary; 02 + 06 — secondary.

## 4. FNDGS as the operative Canadian framework (headline for data-sovereignty + cooperative-alternative)

**The First Nations Data Governance Strategy (FNDGS), submitted to Indigenous Services Canada in 2025 (AFN Resolution 30-2025), is the most authoritative Canadian operational framework for Indigenous data governance at scale.** Community-driven, Nation-based national strategy. **Plans regional information governance centres (one per region).**

**Substantive observation:** FNDGS — not CARE Principles or IEEE 2890 — is the operative Canadian framework. For AAFC, ISC, regional RDA partners, FNDGS is the reference. CARE is international; IEEE 2890 is international industry standard; FNDGS is Canadian operational.

**Talk-ready claims.**
- "FNDGS is the operative Canadian Indigenous data governance framework — community-driven, Nation-based, regional implementation."
- "Regional information governance centres (one per region) are the operational model — this is what the framework-deployment gap looks like in the Canadian institutional layer."
- "For AAFC to operationalise an Indigenous-led agrifood AI programme, FNDGS is the framework reference — not CARE, not IEEE 2890."

**Anchor unit.** `units/fnigc-first-nations-data-governance-strategy.md`; quote `fnigc-fndgs-mission`.

**Archetypes.** 02 — primary; 05 — secondary.

## 5. NCIAF as the institutional vehicle (headline for cooperative-alternative + critical-lens)

**NCIAF (National Circle for Indigenous Agriculture and Food), launched 13 June 2024 with FCC support, is the only named Indigenous-led agrifood organisation with FCC backing and national scope.** MOU with CFA signed 2025. President and CEO Kallie Wood; Senior Director Gabrielle Fallu.

**Talk-ready claims.**
- "NCIAF, launched June 2024 with FCC support, is the institutional anchor for Indigenous-led agrifood in Canada — distinct from US Intertribal Agriculture Council (1987) and Aotearoa NZ Te Mana Raraunga (2015)."
- "NCIAF-CFA MOU 2025 is the substantive institutional recognition of Indigenous-led agrifood in Canada."
- "NCIAF is positioned to operationalise an Indigenous-led agrifood AI programme if AAFC and ISC were to create one — but no such programme exists (G-313)."

**Anchor unit.** `units/nciaf-indigenous-agrifood-canada.md`.

**Archetypes.** 04 (cooperative-alternative) — primary; 05 — secondary.

## 6. Mila's Indigenous AI institutional infrastructure (headline for academic / regional-cluster-comparison)

**Mila's substantive Indigenous AI work comprises three distinct vehicles — convening (Indigenous AI Advisory Circle), education (Pathways to AI with FNTC), capacity-building (Indigenous Pathfinders in AI summer program).** The constraint/critical scout §5 framing of "Mila-CSA Indigenous Data Sovereignty Standard" is **incorrect** — Mila's Indigenous AI work is institutional infrastructure, not a CSA joint-committee standard.

**Substantive observation:** The 2nd cohort of Indigenous Pathfinders in AI (2025) presented 8 AI prototypes at Mila on July 15, 2025. **Specific agrifood applications are emerging but not yet publicly documented.** G-334.

- "21 participants / 8 AI prototypes in the 2025 Indigenous Pathfinders in AI cohort — substantive Canadian academic capacity-building."
- "Quebec-anchored Indigenous AI is institutional infrastructure (convening + education + capacity-building), not standards development."

**Anchor unit.** `units/mila-indigenous-ai-infrastructure.md`.

## 7. The Ruder-Wittman analytical voice (headline for critical-lens + cooperative-alternative)

**Sarah-Louise Ruder (SSHRC PDF, University of Ottawa) + Hannah Wittman (UBC IRES) are the substantive Canadian agrifood data-governance analytical voice.** Data-justice framing; CARE principles engagement; 40 workshops + 26 conferences across Western Canada, US, Latin America (2020-2023).

Substantive papers:
- Ruder & Wittman (2025). *Agricultural data governance from the ground up*. **Big Data & Society**. **Cited 22 times.**
- Ruder (2025). *Theorizing agricultural data policy and governance*. **Journal of Peasant Studies**. Cited 14 times.
- Duncan, E. et al. (incl. Ruder). (2026). *The political economy of agricultural data governance*. **Journal of Rural Studies**. Cited 6 times.
- **BCACARN Ethical Data Governance Toolkit (2024)** — first documented Canadian agrifood ethical data governance toolkit.

**Substantive observation:** Ruder-Wittman are the **data-governance critical voices**, distinct from Neethirajan (Dalhousie; AI-deployed-on-the-farm), Dara (U Guelph; greenhouse AI risk), Charlebois (Dalhousie; pragmatic-critical). These are AI-deployment critical voices; Ruder-Wittman are the data-governance critical voices.

**Talk-ready claims.**
- "Ruder-Wittman are the substantive Canadian agrifood data-governance analytical voice — distinct from AI-deployment critical voices (Neethirajan, Dara, Charlebois)."
- "Big Data & Society 2025 (cited 22 times) — 40 workshops + 26 conferences across Western Canada, US, Latin America — is the most comprehensive Canadian-context empirical study of agrifood data governance."
- "Ruder-Wittman data-justice framing provides the substantive Canadian alternative voice — distinct from EU JoinData cooperative framing or US NAPDC cooperative framing."

**Anchor quote.** `quotes/institutional-mission-statements/ruder-wittman-data-justice-mission.md`.

## 8. IEEE 2890-2025 — world's first Indigenous data standard explicitly for AI/ML (headline for critical-lens + international comparison)

**IEEE 2890-2025 was approved by the IEEE SA Standards Board on September 10, 2025 and published November 14, 2025.** Working Group Chair: Stephanie Russo Carroll (University of Arizona). **Critically: explicitly applies to AI/ML industries** — per the IEEE Standards Association, the standard "enhances the integrity of that data, and the likelihood of engaged collaborations with data producers in ways that were previously unthinkable" within "data-generated industries, such as artificial intelligence and machine learning" (Udall Center event description, Feb 18, 2026 webinar).

**Substantive observation:** This is the **first formal international standard explicitly for Indigenous data + AI/ML provenance.** The standard does not bind Canadian federal practice directly, but it provides the technical specification that vendor-deployed AI systems would need to meet to be CARE-compliant.

**Talk-ready claims.**
- "IEEE 2890-2025 is the world's first global standard explicitly for Indigenous data + AI/ML provenance — published November 14, 2025."
- "Working Group Chair Stephanie Russo Carroll (U Arizona); standard operationalises CARE Principles into industry specifications."
- "The standard does not bind Canadian federal practice directly — but it provides the technical specification that vendor-deployed AI would need to meet to be CARE-compliant."

**Anchor unit.** Existing `units/indigenous-data-sovereignty.md` (updated). Quote: `quotes/institutional-mission-statements/ieee-2890-mission.md` (date corrected).

**Archetypes.** 05 — primary; 02 — secondary.

## 9. Substantive contested claims surfaced (carry through to talks)

- **C-291:** "Indigenous-led AI agrifood deployment in Canada does not exist." Counter: PolArctic (peer-reviewed 2025) + Salmon Vision (peer-reviewed 2023) + NEDA + Inuvik Tech + Indigenous Pathfinders in AI 2025 cohort.
- **C-292:** "CARE Principles are sufficient for Indigenous AI governance." Counter: GIDA's August 2025 "CARE Directs Us Home" communiqué explicitly cautions against treating CARE as a static compliance checklist.

- **C-294:** "Indigenous data sovereignty is a Canada-only framework." Counter: CARE Principles are international (GIDA members from Aotearoa NZ, Australia, Canada, Finland, Norway, Mexico, Sweden, Tonga and Samoa, US); IEEE 2890-2025 is international.
- **C-295:** "Two-Eyed Seeing is a metaphor without operational implementation." Counter: PolArctic is the operational implementation (peer-reviewed 2025).
- **C-296:** "Indigenous agrifood in Canada has no institutional anchor." Counter: NCIAF (June 2024) + MOU with CFA (2025).
- **C-297:** "CARE Principles are the operative Canadian Indigenous data governance framework." Counter: FNDGS (2025) is the operative Canadian framework; CARE is the international anchor.

## 10. Substantive gaps surfaced (carry through to talks as honesty)

- **G-313:** No federal or provincial programme specifically funds Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployment.
- **G-329:** No Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployment with CARE Principles *explicit* operational framework alignment (deployments do *practise* the principles but do not name them as implementation frameworks).
- **G-330:** No Canadian agrifood-data-governance framework explicitly engaging IDSov and Indigenous Food Sovereignty as integrated principles.
- **G-331:** No Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployment in Quebec (Mila's Indigenous AI infrastructure is Quebec-anchored but deployments are not documented in agrifood specifically).
- **G-332:** AAFC Indigenous Agriculture and Food Systems Initiative ($8.5M, 2018-2023) closed; no successor programme in AI for All (June 2026).
- **G-333:** Indigenous-led agrifood AI readiness assessment for First Nations, Inuit, or Métis agrifood operators not published.
- **G-334:** Mila's Indigenous Pathfinders in AI 8 AI prototypes (2025 cohort) — specific agrifood applications not publicly documented.
- **G-335:** PolArctic operational status (production mariculture vs. pilot-completed) unclear post-2025 publication.
