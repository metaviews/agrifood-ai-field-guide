## Content

La Ferme Digitale, French agtech association founded 2016, is positioned as "the leading agri-tech network in Europe" — 200+ partners and start-ups. Co-founders include Hervé Pillaud (farmer-livestock producer in Vendée, 40 years, author of *Agronumericus* 2015), Justine Lipuma (CEO Mycophyto), and David Joulin.

**GAIA project — Generative Artificial Intelligence for Agriculture.** La Ferme Digitale's flagship open-source AI initiative, in partnership with INRAE, ACTA, OSFARM, and other public and private stakeholders. From Pillaud's INRAE interview:

> "This collaborative platform, which we are leading as part of La Ferme Digitale in partnership with OSFARM, INRAE, ACTA and other public and private stakeholders, aims to democratise the use of AI in agriculture. It should be seen as a collaborative tool for generating AI agents specialised in agriculture, based on an open-source core of agricultural data made available to farmers."

**Concrete figures:**
- 90 participants in the second GAIA hackathon (February 2025, Paris International Agricultural Show).
- 8 multidisciplinary teams contributed AI-enabled agricultural solutions.
- INRAE / La Ferme Digitale 4-year partnership agreement signed April 2025.

**Hervé Pillaud's distinctive framing — French-internal critical voice.** Three substantive positions from the INRAE interview that distinguish La Ferme Digitale's framing from typical institutional AI advocacy:

1. **Funding gap recognition.** "Unicorns do not exist in the agricultural sector. The French agri-tech industry is not doing well, and funding from private investors remains insufficient."

2. **Open-source / digital commons as response.** "Using open source and developing digital commons are the obvious solution — and something farmers, who invented the mutual model and cooperation, know how to do."

3. **Margin of error framing.** "To understand how AI works, we need to move from a culture of proof to a culture of probability, while reducing the margin of error to almost zero. Farmers tolerate very little margin of error, simply because they cannot afford to absorb it."

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

Anchors the *generative AI × extension-and-advisory* cell with a *cooperative / open-source commons* deployment model. Distinct from:
- **FCC Root AI** (vendor / Crown corporation; free but Crown-corp-controlled; English/French only).
- **CGIAR AgriLLM** (multilateral; designed for low-resource smallholder contexts).
- **La Ferme Digitale / GAIA** is *industry-association-coordinated* with *open-source / digital commons* design philosophy. The structural pattern is cooperative / mutual-model agricultural tradition applied to AI infrastructure.

## Why it matters for talks

- The cooperative / digital-commons pattern is the European-internal structural alternative to vendor-led AI deployment. Worth naming alongside US lock-in patterns (Deere, Bayer, Croptimistic) and Chinese state-affiliated patterns (Alibaba, JD).
- Pillaud's "unicorns do not exist in agriculture" framing is the most credible French-internal critical voice in the field guide. Distinct from IPES-Food framing, distinct from Monsalve/Seufert framing — European cooperative tradition rather than food sovereignty or Big Tech critique.
- The "culture of proof to culture of probability" framing names an epistemic shift AI in agriculture requires. Worth understanding because it bears on the verification-discipline pattern the field guide maintains.
- The 90-participant / 8-team hackathon is real deployment-style activity, not just announcement.

## Critical context

- GAIA is piloting-stage, not deployed-scale. The 90-participant hackathon is one event.
- "Open-source core of agricultural data" raises the *what data is in the commons* question — open-source code doesn't mean open-source data. Worth distinguishing in talks.
- La Ferme Digitale's cooperative / mutual framing is genuinely French-internal. The food-sovereignty critical voice is distinct (IPES-Food framing) but the cooperative / commons framing has overlap. Worth naming the distinction without flattening.
- Pillaud's position that "unicorns do not exist in agriculture" is a *structural* critique of agtech funding, not an indictment of any specific company. Applies to the whole sector.
- The 4-year partnership with INRAE is recent (April 2025) — outcomes not yet visible.
