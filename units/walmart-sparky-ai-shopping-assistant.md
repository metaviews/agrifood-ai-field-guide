## Content

In June 2025, Walmart launched *Sparky*, a generative AI-powered shopping assistant, as a top-level "Ask Sparky" surface inside the Walmart app, available to all US customers. Sparky interprets open-ended prompts, synthesises product reviews, plans meals, and assembles shopping lists (and ultimately carts). It sits alongside Walmart's earlier AI features — search-within-nav, review summaries, product descriptions, comparisons.

On 14 October 2025, Walmart announced a partnership with OpenAI to allow Walmart and Sam's Club customers to shop directly through ChatGPT via *Instant Checkout* — extending Sparky's surface into the OpenAI app. Walmart also reports deploying ChatGPT Enterprise to internal teams and is among the first partners for OpenAI Certifications. Walmart reports it cut customer-care resolution times by up to 40% using AI, and reduced fashion product-catalog timelines by up to 18 weeks (vendor numbers, not independently audited).

The deployment reaches the largest grocery-adjacent customer base of any US retailer: Walmart reported 270 million weekly customers across 10,750+ stores in 19 countries (FY25), of which the great majority are US grocery shoppers.

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

This is the largest *deployed* consumer-facing generative AI surface in US grocery by reach. It directly parallels the Loblaw × ChatGPT unit (Canada), but at an order-of-magnitude larger customer footprint. Anchors the US end of any cross-border grocery-AI comparison.

## Why it matters for talks

- Concrete *deployed* example (not announced) at consumer scale — useful as the load-bearing case in the consumption × generative-AI cell.
- The October 2025 OpenAI / Instant Checkout extension is the clearest publicly documented case of a major US grocer embedding inside a third-party LLM surface — Loblaw × ChatGPT was first, Walmart × ChatGPT is largest.
- Vendor-reported operational metrics (40% resolution-time cut, 18-week fashion-timeline cut) are *vendor numbers*; no independent verification visible in our sources.

## Critical context the unit does not sanitise

- Sparky is a *proprietary black box from the consumer's perspective*. Walmart does not publish the model architecture, training data lineage, or evaluation methodology for the assistant. Consumer data flows are governed by Walmart's Privacy Policy, not a public standard.
- Walmart's pricing scrutiny context (parallels Loblaw) makes the deployment politically charged — the technology is inseparable from the affordability debate for public-education framing.
- Sparky's "agentic" roadmap (auto-reorder, party planning, repair-advice-to-cart) implies a long-term shift from on-demand search to predictive purchasing that raises questions about consent, basket-inflation, and consumer autonomy that have no public incident-reporting mechanism.
