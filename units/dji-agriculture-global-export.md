## Content

DJI Agriculture is the largest agricultural drone deployment globally. The **fourth annual Agricultural Drone Industry Insight Report**, presented at Brazil's Agrishow 2025 (May 2025), provides concrete deployment figures.

**Concrete global deployment figures (DJI 2024/2025 Annual Report):**
- **400,000 DJI Agriculture drones in use globally at end of 2024** (+33% YoY; +90% vs 2020)
- **980+ million acres** worldwide treated by DJI Agriculture drones (cumulative, across 100+ countries)
- **222 million tons of water saved** (vendor-reported, cumulative)
- **30.87 million tons of CO2 emissions reduced** (vendor-reported, cumulative)
- Recent regulatory liberalisation: Argentina reduced restrictions for drone deployment in agricultural areas; Spain simplified the approval process; Brazil standardised pilot training.

**Specific case studies (DJI 2024/2025 Report):**
- **Brazilian coffee (DJI Agras T40 / T50)**: Operational costs reduced by **70%** vs manual spraying; **50%** vs tractor spraying. Use: pesticide, fungicide, foliar fertilizer application.
- **Romanian vineyard (DJI Agras T50)**: Chemical use reduced from 241.64 L to 111.94 L (more than half); time reduced from 3-4 days to 2.5 hours; effective on sloped terrain and after rain.
- **Rice spreading (multi-country)**: Best-practice documentation for drone-based rice spreading.
- **Corn, coffee, canola, sunflowers, rice, bananas, vineyards**: case studies documented.

**Hardware specification (Agras T50, April 2024 international launch):**
- Coaxial dual-rotor design, 54-inch propellers
- 40 kg spraying or 50 kg spreading payloads
- Up to 50 acres (21 hectares) per hour spraying
- 16 L/minute dual atomisation spraying system; up to 24 L/minute with four-sprinkler kit
- 9-minute fast charging (30% to 95%)
- 1,500 charge cycle battery warranty

**Data privacy posture (DJI explicit statement):**
- "No flight logs, photos, or videos are synced with DJI by default."
- "DJI Agras drones are designed to store data locally in the remote control and the drone."
- Operators must opt-in to share data on DJI servers.
- Non-China-mainland user data housed in US, Japan, Europe servers (TLS-protected).
- AES-256 encryption for personal data.
- Third-party security audits: Booz Allen Hamilton, FTI Consulting, Kivu Consulting (since 2017).

## Critical context

- 400,000 / 980M acres / 100+ countries figures are vendor-reported (DJI Annual Report). Independent verification limited.
- 222M tons water saved and 30.87M tons CO2 reduced are *cumulative* vendor calculations; methodology not detailed in our source.
- DJI is a Chinese-origin company. Geopolitical posture matters: US restrictions on DJI drones have varied; DJI has explicitly built out non-China-mainland server infrastructure to address concerns. Worth knowing for talks.
- The case study figures are vendor-reported from named partners; worth verifying for high-stakes citation but concrete enough to anchor discussion.
- The DJI SmartFarm app and agritech ecosystem are bundled with the hardware; the *value capture* model is hardware-first with software complementary. Distinct from Bayer FieldView (data layer on top of inputs) or xFarm (independent platform).
