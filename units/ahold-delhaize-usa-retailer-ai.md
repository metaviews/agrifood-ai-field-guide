## Content

Ahold Delhaize USA operates a documented AI stack across its East-Coast US grocery banners (Stop & Shop, Food Lion, Giant, Hannaford). Four distinct AI deployments surface with primary-source evidence:

**RELEX Solutions — end-to-end forecasting & replenishment for fresh produce:**
- Deployed via ADUSA Supply Chain & Delhaize America
- ~1,200+ Delhaize-America stores covered
- Expanded agreement from 2019 onward for fresh-produce forecasting

**Badger Technologies "Marty the Robot" — autonomous shelf-scanning:**
- Deployed to 300+ Stop & Shop stores
- Identifies mis-priced, misplaced, out-of-stock items + hazards
- Computer-vision-driven navigation in store aisles

**"Lunchbox" cashierless / "just walk out" store pilot:**
- Ahold Delhaize USA × Cloudpick
- Computer-vision checkout-free format

**FoodStorm catering order management:**
- Acquired by Instacart
- Now operating across Ahold Delhaize USA banners

**Why Ahold Delhaize USA is the load-bearing East-Coast US grocery AI unit.** Albertsons / Afresh covers West + parts of central; Kroger covers Midwest; Walmart covers national. Ahold Delhaize USA completes the US regional-coverage picture for in-store fresh AI + shelf-scanning robotics.

## Why it matters for talks

- *Marty the Robot at Stop & Shop (300+ stores) is one of the largest *deployed* autonomous shelf-scanning robotics footprints in NA grocery.* Worth surfacing alongside SpartanNash + Simbe Tally as the canonical "shelf-vision robotics at US grocery scale" pattern.
- *The RELEX × Badger × FoodStorm stack is operationally layered* — forecasting (RELEX) at the supply-chain level, shelf robot (Badger) at the in-store level, catering OMS (FoodStorm) at the consumer-ordering level. Talks on NA grocery AI architecture can use this unit as a contrast to Walmart's single-vendor stack (Walmart Element).
- *The Cloudpick / Lunchbox cashierless pilot is a small-but-noteworthy experiment in US "just-walk-out" grocery.* A small number of locations, but a structurally distinct test of consumer-side AI in NA grocery beyond Amazon's Fresh format.
- *FoodStorm being acquired by Instacart and then serving Ahold Delhaize USA is a load-bearing example of cross-actor consolidation in grocery AI*. The platform-aggregator (Instacart) now owns the catering-OMS that Ahold Delhaize USA depends on — cross-platform data flow is implicit.

## Critical context

- The 300+ Stop & Shop deployment number for Badger is from the vendor's own communication; Stop & Shop has not enumerated the active fleet publicly.
- RELEX implementation scale (1,200+ stores) is from RELEX / ADUSA joint communication; specific store-by-store deployment not enumerated.
- "Lunchbox" cashierless pilot — number of stores, locations, outcomes not publicly disclosed in detail.
- All four deployments span banners; vendor (Badger, RELEX, FoodStorm) relationships were independently struck before the Instacart acquisition of FoodStorm — the platform mix is not unified under one Ahold Delhaize USA contract.
