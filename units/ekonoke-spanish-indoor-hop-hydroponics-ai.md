## Content

Ekonoke is the corpus's first primary-source-documented Spanish-origin agritech AI deployment. Spanish startup Ekonoke cultivates hops hydroponically indoors, using nearly **95% less water than open-field hops** (per Reuters April 2023 + Sifted 2025). Indoor cultivation is a *climate-adaptation* substrate for hops, which are otherwise climate-threatened in traditional growing regions due to drought, heat stress, and shifting seasonality.

**Documented scope (per Reuters April 2023 + Sifted 2025):**

- **Spanish origin** (northwestern Iberian Peninsula location per multiple sources)
- **Almost 10 metres high** — indoor vertical cultivation scale (per Sifted 2025)
- **Renewable-powered** hydroponic system (per Reuters 2023)
- **Multi-colour LED** lighting
- **Multi-source climate data**: AI-driven climate control for indoor conditions
- **95% less water** than open-field cultivation (per Reuters 2023)
- Strategic motivation: hops supply for beer brewing under climate-stress scenarios; "save climate-threatened beer" framing

**Why this matters for the corpus:**

This is *the* corpus's first named Spanish-origin agritech AI deployment at primary-source tier. Spanish agritech corporation / startup scope has been a corpus gap (memory explicitly names Spain / North Africa as worth a future cycle). The unit fills that gap with a substantive primary-source anchor.

The cycle's substantive analytical claim is twofold:

1. **Spanish agritech operates at the EU-cluster-pattern node with corporate-vendor-deployment shape distinct from the NA venture-funded substrate.** The Spanish state has institutional infrastructure (Eatable Adventures accelerator; ICEX trade promotion; ENIA International Chair CIIAA) but operates in a *corporate-vendor-deployment rather than cooperative-governance* shape (the EU-cluster-pattern's existing structural feature, see `units/joindata-netherlands.md`). The Spanish agritech pattern is *corporate-vendor-deployment within EU-cluster-pattern context*.

2. **The water-savings figure (95% vs. open-field) is corpus's most extreme water-savings figure to date.** The Lebanese Ground Vertical Farming unit showed 90% water savings (per GSMA 2020). Ground Vertical Farming operates via recirculation water in vertical panels; Ekonoke operates via hydroponic indoor cultivation. The two are structurally different substrates (recirculation vs. hydroponic) but produce similar water-savings magnitudes.
