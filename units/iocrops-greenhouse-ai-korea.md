# ioCrops — FTVC™ full-tech greenhouse-automation stack

## Content

**ioCrops Inc.** (Daejeon, South Korea; founded 2017) operates the corpus's cleanest example of a **greenhouse-automation vendor from an East-Asia secondary anchor**. Their product surface is the **FTVC™ ("Full Tech Value Chain")** stack — a five-product plus control-center greenhouse automation layer:

| Product | Korean (where applicable) | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **HERMAI** | 온실용 로봇 | AI-based greenhouse robot with vision-system automation |
| **ioCrops Sensor** | 스마트팜 IoT 센서 및 원격 모니터링 솔루션 | Smart-farm IoT sensors + remote monitoring |
| **ioFarm** | 데이터 기반 재배 솔루션 | Data-driven cultivation solution |
| **Ation** | 데이터 기반 인력 관리 솔루션 | Data-driven labour management solution (recent public launch) |
| **Sprayer** | 반자동 방제기 | Semi-automatic pest-control sprayer |
| **Control Center** | 관제 센터 운영 솔루션 | Operations / control centre |

The OS layer is **Grotecture™**, described by the company as "the next-generation OS for greenhouse productivity" — applying targeted solutions for environmental control, crop cultivation, IPM, labour, and management into a structured system.

**Origin and co-founders.** Jinhyung (CEO) — won the **Autonomous Greenhouse Challenge** (a Wageningen University & Research-led international autonomous-greenhouse competition). Sohee (Co-Founder & Tech Lead). Minseok (Co-Founder & Ag Lead). The HortiDaily / independent-trade-press interviews are the corpus's primary third-party source for the founders' product / strategy.

**Multi-country deployment.** Per the official ioCrops website (English): "Developed in Korea, ioCrops solutions are now expanding to **Canada, Japan, Turkey, Australia, Poland, Saudi Arabia**, and beyond." Each international expansion is a multi-continent deployment surface:

- **Canada** — Greenhouse Canada sponsored coverage confirms "Already deployed in Korea, Ation is..." (October 2024) and the North American free demo program.
- **Japan** — explicitly named in the international-expansion list (the corpus's first Japan-side indoor-CEA vendor anchor that is *not* Spread Co.).
- **Turkey, Australia, Poland, Saudi Arabia** — additional regional expansion surfaces.

**Peer-reviewed citation.** Ceccarelli 2022 (FAO / ageconsearch, cited 26) names ioCrops in the precision-agriculture literature: "ioCrops also rents out automated greenhouses and controls the complete greenhouse plant operations, ranging from climate and crop management to labour and [more]." This is the corpus's anchor for V2-grade academic-corroboration.

## Why it matters for talks

ioCrops is the corpus's anchor for the **"Korean-origin AgTech with East-Asia regional + multi-continent deployment"** pattern. It pairs:

- **Spread Co.** (`units/spread-techno-farm-vertical-lettuce-japan.md`) — Japan-side industrial CEA anchor
- **ioCrops** — Korea-origin multi-continent CEA vendor

Two presentations:

1. **Korea as an AgTech origin + export country.** ioCrops is one of the corpus's only East-Asia-export examples with multi-country deployment (Canada, Japan, Turkey, Australia, Poland, Saudi Arabia). Surface in any talk that asks "where does East-asia AgTech export come from?" — Korea is the secondary answer; Japan is more inward-focused (Spread's Series A talks about global expansion but the multi-country deployment at scale is ioCrops).

2. **The Autonomous Greenhouse Challenge as institutional layer.** Wageningen University & Research's Autonomous Greenhouse Challenge is the corpus's clearest international-research-to-deployment pathway for CEA AI. ioCrops' Jinhyung won this; the corpus has the corresponding unit on Wageningen-side institutional layer (`units/joindata-netherlands.md` — adjacent, but IoCrops is the deployment recipient). Worth surfacing in any talk about AI-and-indoor-CEA pathways.

Three secondary presentations:

- **HERMAI as a robotics-market entry.** AI greenhouse robots are an emerging category. ioCrops names HERMAI as their solution; the corpus should track whether HERMAI sells at the scale of, say, Aigen's field solar robot (`units/...` reference future) or stays niche. V0 on HERMAI deployment volume.
- **Ation as a labour-market data product.** Ation is the labour-management product at the heart of the recent ioCrops announcements. The "labour optimisation" framing is conceptually adjacent to the NA-side foodservice-deployment pattern (e.g. `units/sysco-ai360-foodservice.md`) but inside a CEA context. Cross-reference for talks about AI's effect on agricultural labour markets.
- **The Korea-Japan cross-deployment observation.** ioCrops deploying in *Japan* is the corpus's first explicit deployment of a Korean agrifood AI product into Japan (counterpart to the user-noticed gap). Surface this observation in the meta-pattern unit (`units/japan-korea-agrifood-ai-pattern.md`).

## Critical context

- **Ation's Korea deployment scale is V0.** Greenhouse Canada says "Already deployed in Korea, Ation is..." — the deployment is confirmed but the size (number of greenhouses, hectares covered) is not in primary sources.
- **HERMAI's commercial deployment numbers are V0.** ioCrops product descriptions are detailed; deployment figures are absent. Encode as a verification gap (analogous to G-073 / G-075 in adjacent units).
- **Multi-country expansion lists "expanding" — not "fully deployed."** Per the official site language: "solutions are now expanding to..." — the *expansion* is the state of the operation; the *mature multi-country deployment at scale* is the next-step claim. Tag the unit's maturity accordingly (S2-S3 not S3-S4).
- **Wageningen Autonomous Greenhouse Challenge is competition-grade, not deployment-grade.** Winning the competition is not the same as commercial deployment at scale. Cite as institutional-research-to-deployment pathway (T2-T3), not as deployment proof.
- **Co-founder interview sources (Hortidaily)** are trade-press; the language is promotional in tone. Cite as trade-press, not as third-party independent assessment.
