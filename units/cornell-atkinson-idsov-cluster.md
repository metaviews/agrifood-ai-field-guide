## Content

The **Cornell Atkinson Indigenous Data Sovereignty cluster** is the **US land-grant academic anchor** for Indigenous Data Sovereignty (IDSov) research and Indigenous-led data infrastructure work. It extends the global IDSov framework (CARE Principles, IEEE 2890-2025, UNDRIP Article 3) into a US institutional academic context, anchored at Cornell University.

### Lead researchers

**Steven Manaʻoakamai Johnson, Ph.D.**

- **Title:** Assistant Professor, Ashley School of Global Development and the Environment, Cornell University (Ithaca NY)
- **Faculty Fellow:** Cornell Atkinson Center for Sustainability
- **Affiliate Faculty:** American Indian and Indigenous Studies Program, Cornell
- **Background:** Kanaka ʻŌiwi (Native Hawaiian) scholar; Ph.D. Geography, Oregon State University (2021); M.S. Biology, University of Guam (2017); B.S. Biology, Barry University (2010)
- **Affiliations:** Vice President, Tåno, Tåsi, yan Todu 501(c)(3) (Saipan, MP); AAAS Council Early Career At-Large-Member (2023-2025)
- **Substantive research focus:** Indigenous-led data infrastructure for ocean and coastal conservation; community-led monitoring; climate cooperatives; decolonial methodology; r Kupuna / Kānaka ʻŌiwi frameworks
- **Connection to agriculture:** Substantively limited — Johnson's primary research is marine / ocean conservation, climate cooperatives, Indigenous knowledge systems. The Cornell Atkinson IDSov cluster extends his work into agrifood-adjacent questions (food systems, food sovereignty) but his primary research is marine.

**Michael Charles**

- **Title:** Citizen of the Tohono O'odham Nation; Cornell CALS ('16)
- **Substantive research focus:** Computational modeling for Indigenous data sovereignty; community-defined research atlases; statistical summaries of Indigenous data
- **Connection to agriculture:** Direct — the Tohono O'odham Nation is in the Sonoran Desert (Arizona / Mexico border region); agricultural and food-system dimensions of Indigenous data sovereignty are part of Charles's substantive work.

- **Cornell Atkinson Center for Sustainability** — Cornell's sustainability research hub. The IDSov cluster is positioned here as a Faculty Fellowship research area.
- **American Indian and Indigenous Studies Program** — Cornell's interdisciplinary Indigenous studies program.
- **Ashley School of Global Development and the Environment** — Johnson's home unit. Cornell CALS.

### Why this unit matters

The existing `units/indigenous-data-sovereignty.md` covers the IDSov framework (CARE Principles, IEEE 2890-2025) and operational anchors in Peru, Aotearoa, Mexico. The Cornell Atkinson cluster is the *US land-grant academic anchor* for IDSov work — a substantively different cell:

- **Existing IDSov unit:** covers global framework + international operational anchors (Peru, Aotearoa, Mexico)
- **This unit:** covers US land-grant academic IDSov work — connecting the global IDSov framework to US institutional / land-grant / extension context

The land-grant institutional anchor is structurally important because:
- The US Land-grant system is the largest single deployment infrastructure for US agricultural research and extension (see `units/extension-foundation-2026-national-ai-report.md`)
- IDSov work *within* this infrastructure is structurally distinct from IDSov work in international Indigenous-led organisations (GIDA, ONAMIAP, Te Mana Raraunga)
- The cluster provides an institutional pathway for IDSov methodology to engage with USDA AI Strategy and Cooperative Extension without being absorbed into either

### Substantive research contributions

Johnson's publication record (per February 2026 CV) demonstrates substantive IDSov work:

- Kassam KA, **Charles MT**, and **Johnson SM**. 2023. *"Significance of different ways of knowing in responding to the climate crisis: the necessity of Indigenous knowledge."* PLoS Climate 2(7), e0000237. — substantive IDSov-and-climate work.
- Spalding AK et al. (incl. **Johnson SM**). 2023. *"Engaging the Tropical Majority to Make Ocean Governance and Science more Equitable and Effective."* npj Ocean Sustainability 2(8). — IDSov methodology for ocean governance (parallel to agrifood).
- **Johnson SM** and Villagomez AO. 2026. *"Representation and Power in Ocean Conservation Documentaries: A Decolonial Analysis."* Oceans and Society. — decolonial methodology.
- **Johnson SM** and Watson JR. *"Climate cooperatives to support sustainability in the face of mid- and late-century ocean climate analogs."* In review at Frontiers of Ocean Sustainability. — climate cooperatives framing (parallel to JoinData / NAPDC cooperative alternative; see `units/joindata-netherlands.md`, `units/napdc-national-ag-producer-data-cooperative.md`).

The "climate cooperatives" in-review paper is substantively parallel to JoinData / NAPDC cooperative framing for agrifood data — Johnson's work extends the cooperative / commons logic to climate-adaptation contexts.

### Why it matters for talks

- The Cornell Atkinson cluster is the canonical US land-grant academic anchor for IDSov work. Talks about US institutional engagement with Indigenous data sovereignty should reference it.
- The cluster's substantive work on "climate cooperatives" parallels the cooperative / commons alternative for agrifood (JoinData, NAPDC). The parallel is worth surfacing.
- The Cornell institutional position means the cluster's work can engage with USDA AI Strategy and Cooperative Extension without losing IDSov framing.

### Critical context

- **Johnson's primary research is marine / ocean conservation**, not agrifood per se. The agricultural / food-system application of his IDSov framework is structural — agrifood is one cell where his methodology applies, not the primary cell of his work. The cluster's relevance to agrifood AI is *framework-extension*, not direct agrifood-AI deployment.
- **Charles's agricultural connection is direct** (Tohono O'odham Nation context) but his work is not primarily AI deployment — it is data sovereignty methodology applied to Indigenous community contexts.
- The cluster is **a small team within Cornell**, not a large institutional programme. Substantive IDSov operational deployment at US scale remains a gap (per G-043, G-048).
- The cluster's work is positioned within the broader Cornell / Land-grant institutional context. Whether IDSov methodology can scale to operational AI deployment within the Land-grant system is an open question — Cornell Atkinson is the *anchor*, not the *deployment infrastructure*.
- The cluster does *not* duplicate the GIDA / CARE Principles operational anchors in Peru (`units/indigenous-data-sovereignty.md`). Cornell Atkinson is the US academic-anchored counterpart; GIDA / ONAMIAP / Te Mana Raraunga are the international Indigenous-led operational anchors. Both are substantive; they are distinct cells.
