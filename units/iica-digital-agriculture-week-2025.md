## Content

The **AI Day at Digital Agriculture Week 2025** (DAW 2025), convened by the **Inter-American Institute for Cooperation on Agriculture (IICA)** together with the **Inter-American Development Bank (IDB)**, **CAF — Development Bank of Latin America and the Caribbean**, **Bayer**, **PROCISUR** (Cooperative Program for the Technological Development of Agrifood and Agro-Industry in the Southern Cone), the **University of Córdoba, Spain**, and **AWS** — held at IICA HQ in San José, Costa Rica, October 2025 — is the most important institutional convening for LAC agrifood AI in the corpus's 2025-2026 window.

Documented scope (per IICA primary press release, October 2 2025):

- **200+ participants** in hybrid format (in-person + virtual)
- Event name: *"Innovating Agriculture with AI: From Vision to Action"*
- **Two-day format** with nine high-level presentations
- **Topics covered**: precision farming, automation, data use, connectivity, productivity, sustainability, organisational culture

**Named AI deployments surfaced at AI Day (cross-units in this corpus):**

1. **PineSORT** — Drone-based pineapple plant counting (presented by Fabián Fallas Moya, UCR + TEC). Substrate unit: `pinesort-ainnovatech-costa-rica-pineapple-ai.md`.
2. **AinnovaTech** — Computer vision for pineapple flowering counting (presented by Rodrigo Herrera Garro, co-founder + CTO). Substrate unit: `pinesort-ainnovatech-costa-rica-pineapple-ai.md`.
3. **Autoplants** (Argentine) — AI-driven rainfall/frost/irrigation prediction (presented by Julián Mauro, co-founder + Director). Substrate unit: not yet drafted; tracked as candidate for next cycle.
4. **Tomás de Camino Beck**, Director, School of Intelligent Systems, Cenfotec University Costa Rica — opening keynote on success stories and impact.
5. **Fulvio Lizano Madriz** (UNA + UNED Costa Rica) — Prototype *smart hive for meliponiculture* measuring temperature, humidity, light and air quality. Not yet agrifood-AI unit; tracked as prototype.
6. **Saúl Calderón Ramírez**, School of Computer Engineering, TEC — on public R&D investment as prerequisite to AI-driven inclusion.
7. **Jesús Bejarano Quesada**, UCR — "Data is the gold of artificial intelligence. Without reliable information, any model loses value."
8. **Adrián Miranda Cordero**, Senior Solutions Engineer, Cloudera — "Technology is not the enemy; it is an ally for making better decisions."

**Co-conveners' roles (per IICA release):**
- **IICA** — institutional host and convening
- **IDB** — finance + programming for the LAC region (independent of US)
- **CAF** — finance + programming for the LAC region
- **Bayer** — corporate-vendor seed-and-crop-protection player
- **PROCISUR** — technical cooperation across southern cone
- **University of Córdoba (Spain)** — academic-research collaboration
- **AWS** — cloud-infrastructure and AI-services vendor

The seven-co-convener pattern (multilateral institution + two regional banks + corporate vendor + technical-cooperation body + academic partner + cloud-vendor) is a *distinct institutional pattern* the corpus should surface. The IICA DAW 2025 convening is *not* a single-vendor-led event, nor a single-government-led event. It is a multi-stakeholder convening with the multilateral institution (IICA) as the institutional host and orchestrator.

**Why a unit, not just a section in the regional scan:**

1. **Concrete scope that the corpus can cite directly.** IICA member-state geography; named co-conveners with specific roles; documented numbers (200+ participants, nine high-level presentations); specific deployment namesurfacing from the event.
2. **Multi-vendor and multi-institutional surfacing role.** The corpus's prior exposure to LAC AI deployments has been *vendor-led surfaces* (Agrosmart, Kilimo via Microsoft, Falabella via Google Cloud). IICA DAW 2025 demonstrates *institutional-event-led surfacing of the same deployments*. This is a structurally-distinct pattern.
3. **Surface for cluster pattern candidates.** The cluster of deployments surfacing at AI Day 2025 — PineSORT, AinnovaTech, Autoplants, plus the Autoplants-style Argentine agtech — begins to converge on a *Latin American AI-for-agriculture cluster pattern*, distinct from NA / EU / China / India / Japan / Korea / Oceania patterns.
4. **Cluster-pattern observation value.** IICA's convening role positions Latin American agricultural AI as emerging from a *regionally-coordinated institutional substrate*, not a vendor-led or state-led single-deposit pattern. This is structurally distinct from NA (vendor-led) and from India (state-DPI-led) and from China (state-vendor-hybrid + provincial). The "LAC cluster pattern" candidate is one of the corpus's working hypotheses worth tracking.

**Strategic significance:**

1. **The IICA institutional layer is the corpus's first multi-stakeholder multilateral-convening anchor for agrifood AI.** IICA's 34 member states across the Americas makes this institution the continental-scale institutional substrate that the corpus previously had no representation for. The unit anchors the *institutional substrate* layer of the LAC cluster pattern.
2. **The pro-AI-with-conditions framing** (IICA: "AI should empower rather than exclude. The region must take ownership of these technologies, developing them in an open, accessible way and adapted to its reality") is a *prospective-institutional frame*, distinct from academic or civil-society critical voices. Worth surfacing in any talk about how the cluster-pattern taxonomy maps to AI governance.
3. **CAF + IDB + IICA** represents the *multilateral-finance-and-institutional layer* of LAC. Distinct from the OECD-anchored multilateral development banks that anchor EU-style deployment, and distinct from China's bilateral-and-tied-aid pattern, and distinct from US USAID-style aid. Worth a cluster-pattern layer of its own.

## Critical context

- **This is an institutional anchor unit, not a deployment unit.** Activity status: institutional-anchor; this is a third category alongside deployed and piloting. Worth surfacing as a deliberate schema extension.
- **The "AI Day" framing emphasises adoption and acceleration.** Critical voices (like IT for Change / FIAN's Monsalve-Suárez and Seufert 2022 "Big Tech Takeover of Food Systems in Latin America") were *not* surfaced as featured speakers at this event. The cluster-pattern-as-emerging-from-institutional-convening observation should be tempered with awareness that the convening itself is pro-AI-adoption in framing.
- **The 200+ participants figure is institutional participation, not deployment scale.** Deployment-scale figures live in the substrate units (PineSORT, AinnovaTech, Kilimo, Agrosmart, Falabella TARS).
- **Latin American institutional-AI events reach a wider audience via IICA than individual vendor events.** Trade-press coverage is sparser than for NA equivalents; institutional-event primary sources (IICA press releases, partner sites) are the documentation substrate.
- **The cluster-pattern observation about LAC (multilateral-institutional substrate)** is a working hypothesis, not a closed typology. Future cycles should test it against other LAC AI events (e.g. World Agri-Tech South America Summit 2024; bilateral-context IDB-specific programmes).
- **Convening vs deployment framing caveat.** The IICA DAW 2025 event was explicitly positioned as "From Vision to Action" — meaning the cycle of convening → action is *forward-looking*. The corpus should not over-claim deployment scale from the convening.
