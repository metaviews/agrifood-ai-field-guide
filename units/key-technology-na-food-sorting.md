## Content

Key Technology, a Duravant subsidiary headquartered in Walla Walla, Washington, builds AI-driven optical sorting and digital inspection equipment for the food-processing industry. Their VERYX belt- and chute-fed sorters, Optyx, Cayman, BioPrint hyperspectral, VitiSort, COMPASS, Taurys brands all sit under one platform.

**VERYX 2.0 — the AI-load-bearing product.** Key Technology explicitly markets VERYX 2.0 as "powered by artificial intelligence (AI)" — using AI in:
- FM Alert (image classification of foreign material)
- Sort-to-Grade (clump separation for size grading)
- Discovery IIoT analytics platform

**What the AI does:**
- In-air inspection with Pixel Fusion across multiple sensor types
- AI-assisted foreign-material classification
- AI-driven clump separation for size grading
- Hyperspectral defect detection (BioPrint)

**Why this is structurally distinct from TOMRA.** Both are optical-sorting vendors with NA presence; Key Technology is US-headquartered and US-engineered (Duravant), while TOMRA is Norway-parented with Sacramento CA US HQ. Talk-stage worth distinguishing — the data-sovereignty considerations are different.

## Why it matters for talks

- *15+ named NA customers across multiple protein / produce / nut sectors is the strongest NA-deployer footprint in the optical-sorting AI category.* Worth surfacing because it makes the point that NA food processing is not under-AI'd; it is well-AI'd in this product class, just rarely named publicly.
- *The cross-sector breadth (berries, nuts, leafy greens, pasta, wine) is unusual.* Most NA food-processing AI vendors cluster in a single sub-sector; Key Technology's breadth indicates a vendor with category-spanning platform architecture.
- *Chateau Ste. Michelle + Jackson Family Wines + Chappellet — three NA wineries using AI sorters.* Wine grape sorting is small in volume but high in value; AI sorters in NA wine are a deployable-yet-underexamined cell.
- *The Que/Quebec Wild Blueberries deployment makes this unit cross-border NA-relevant*, not just US.
- *Hardware-IO + AI building block pattern.* Key Technology's products run on-premise at the line; the AI classification happens at machine speed. Cloud-mediated AI is not the architecture here.

## Critical context

- All deployment evidence is from Key Technology's own customer-stories index — strongest when confirmed by trade press but vendor-reported for the deployment count itself.
- Customer list is not exhaustive; Key Technology has more customers than the 15+ named.
- "AI-powered" framing comes from Key Technology's own product marketing. The ML model architecture is not publicly documented.
- Foreign-material detection on-tree-nut and berry lines is a high-stakes application (allergens, contaminants). Independent food-safety audit of Key Technology equipment is not surfaced in our sources.
