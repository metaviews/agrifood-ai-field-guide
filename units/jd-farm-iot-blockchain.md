## Content

JD.com's *JD Farm* initiative uses IoT, AI, and blockchain in vertically integrated deployments across Chinese agriculture. As of November 2019, 17 smart agriculture projects had been set up in partnership with farms across China, including a peach farm in Shandong and a pear farm in Hebei that reportedly increased average prices by 30–50% over prior offline sales.

The *JD Digits* fintech arm extends the same architecture into livestock with machine vision, AI-enabled analytics, robots, and IoT — including facial recognition for individualised pig feeding plans and the broader *Stockbreeding intelligent solution*. NYT (2019) reported China-wide deployment of pig facial recognition across multiple vendors including JD and Alibaba affiliates.

The deployment model is *vertically integrated* — JD provides the platform, the IoT infrastructure, the AI, the marketplace, and the logistics network. Farmers gain access to JD's customer base and traceability features; consumers gain supply-chain provenance. This is structurally distinct from Alibaba's cloud-mediated ET Agricultural Brain model.

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

Anchors the *vertically integrated platform* pattern, distinct from *cloud-mediated* (Alibaba) and *cooperative-mediated* (CGIAR / IDSov). Together these three units (`alibaba-et-agricultural-brain.md`, `jd-farm-iot-blockchain.md`, plus the CGIAR and IDSov work in the regional scan) cover the *deployment model* spectrum.

## Critical context

- The 30–50% price increase figure for JD Farm pear and peach operations is a *vendor-reported* outcome. It does not establish whether the increase came from AI-driven quality improvement, from marketplace access, from JD's brand premium, or from displacement of competing supply chains. Worth flagging in talks — the figure circulates widely and is often presented without the disaggregation.
- JD Farm's smallholder interface runs through smartphones and JD's app; the deployment does not address low-literacy or feature-phone contexts. This means the smallholder *interface* is present, but the design assumptions are not smallholder-context design assumptions. The capital-intensity tag captures this — smallholder-interface, industrial-back-end.
- JD's pig facial recognition was a 2018–2019 announcement; no recent operational data found. Re-verify annually.

## Why it matters for talks

The unit supports a comparative slide on *deployment models*: cloud-mediated vs vertically integrated vs cooperative-mediated vs government-mediated. It's also a concrete example of *retailer-led AI in agrifood*, parallel to but distinct from Loblaw's consumer-facing deployment in Canada.
