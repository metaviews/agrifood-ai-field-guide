## Content

Alibaba Cloud's *ET Agricultural Brain* was launched at the Shanghai Computing Conference in June 2018. The product uses AI and big data to help farmers optimise planting, irrigation, and fertilisation, with reported use cases in pig farming and fruit / vegetable production.

The deployment pattern is *cloud-mediated*: Alibaba provides the AI infrastructure, agronomic enterprises integrate it, individual farmers interact via smartphone. Reported productivity and resource-efficiency claims exist in vendor and state-aligned press, but independent verification at sector scale is thin.

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

Anchors the East-Asia entry in the matrix at the on-farm / animal-production intersection. Distinct from JD Farm (different actor, different deployment model — cloud-mediated rather than vertically integrated).

## Critical context

- The ET Agricultural Brain launched as part of Alibaba's broader "ET Brain" series covering city, medical, environmental, and aviation use cases — i.e. it is one application in a *general-purpose AI platform strategy*, not a purpose-built agrifood system. Talks can use this to frame the question: is agrifood AI best developed by specialised actors, or by generalist platforms adapting to the sector?
- Underlying source dates from 2018; the deployment may have evolved significantly. Annual re-verification is required by the freshness model. As of mid-2026, no comprehensive updated operational data was found in our sources; this should be re-searched.
- The deployment operates within China's data regulation environment, which restricts cross-border data flow. This shapes what the platform can and cannot do globally — relevant to G-008 (cross-border data governance interoperability).

## Why it matters for talks

The unit supports the *generalist platform vs specialised agrifood actor* framing. It also gives a concrete Chinese anchor for any slide contrasting cloud-mediated deployment with farmer-led / cooperative-mediated alternatives.
