## Content

Domino's *Dom* is a voice-activated ordering assistant integrated into the Domino's mobile app, social channels, and other surfaces. Originally built on Nuance technology, Dom has been incrementally updated with newer AI capabilities over time.

Dom crossed the *half-million orders* threshold via mobile dictation (Retail Dive / Mobile Commerce Daily reporting, mid-2010s). The product has been continuously deployed since then, with Domino's continuing to invest in voice and conversational AI on top of it.

This unit is the *longest-running* consumer-facing AI voice ordering surface in the US restaurant sector — pre-dating the current LLM wave by nearly a decade.

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

The *durability* anchor for consumer-facing AI in US restaurant ordering. Useful for any talk making the point that the *current* LLM wave is layered on top of pre-existing voice-AI infrastructure.

## Why it matters for talks

- Demonstrates that consumer voice-AI in food ordering has a longer deployment history than the 2023–2026 LLM wave would suggest.
- The Nuance-to-LLM transition is a teachable pattern on *technology-stack evolution* in deployed AI.
- Dom has crossed cultural-saturation thresholds (referenced in popular culture, news, social media).

## Critical context the unit does not sanitise

- The Nuance-era voice recognition had known accuracy limitations; the transition to LLM-era models is recent and not fully public.
- Voice data captured for ordering is *biometric-adjacent* (voice patterns can identify individuals). Domino's privacy policy governs this data, not public standards.
