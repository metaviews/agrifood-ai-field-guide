## Content

IVADO (Institute for Data Valorization) is a Montréal-based AI consortium, anchored at Université de Montréal. 150+ partners, 1,400+ scientists, partner with HEC Montréal and Polytechnique Montréal. IVADO is positioning itself as the **Quebec-side implementation partner for Canada's *AI for All* national strategy** (June 4, 2026).

**The R3AI project — IVADO's flagship substantive initiative.** R3AI = Robust, Reasoning, Responsible AI. The project is positioned as IVADO's contribution to *AI for All*'s vision for trustworthy AI development. The project frames scientific rigour and social relevance as "mutually reinforcing, not in tension."

**June 8, 2026 statement** (verbatim, IVADO):
> "The publication of this national strategy is an important milestone for Canada's AI ecosystem. It builds on collective work to which IVADO has been contributing for nearly a decade, and opens the door to an even more structured collaboration with the federal government, national institutes, and our academic and industry partners, in service of an AI that benefits all of Canadian society."

**IVADO's substantive workstreams:**
- **Workforce training** — *Professional Practice in the Artificial Intelligence (AI) Era* program with Tech3Lab; public library support; Indigenous youth training via Printemps Numérique.
- **Technology transfer** — SMEs adopting AI; translating research into concrete solutions.
- **AI governance** — code of conduct for political parties on responsible AI use in elections; policy statements on AI in public administration; Co-Scientific Director role at the Canadian AI Safety Institute.
- **R3AI project** — robust, reasoning, responsible AI research and deployment.
- **International collaboration** — including with Mexico on responsible AI and human rights.

**Why IVADO is positioned where it is.** IVADO is not the only AI consortium in Canada (there's the Alberta Machine Intelligence Institute, the Vector Institute in Toronto, Mila in Montréal). But IVADO is the *only one* positioned as the *AI for All* strategy's Quebec-side implementation partner. The federal strategy was developed through 11,000+ Canadian submissions; IVADO is explicitly aligned with the resulting strategy.

## Why it matters for talks

- IVADO is the *Quebec-side implementation* of the federal strategy. Worth knowing because the strategy's success depends on regional implementation.
- The R3AI project is the substantive initiative. The "robust, reasoning, responsible" framing is substantive — it's not just ethics-talk, it's a research-deployment approach.
- The international collaboration (especially Mexico on responsible AI) is a structural piece worth knowing. The Canadian AI strategy has an explicit international / scientific-diplomacy component.
- The workforce-training workstream (especially the Indigenous youth training) is adjacent to IDSov framing.

## Critical context

- IVADO's June 8, 2026 statement is *positioning*, not *outcomes*. The R3AI project's substantive deployment outcomes are forward-looking.
- IVADO is positioning as the Quebec-side partner; whether it actually becomes the implementation workhorse is a question worth tracking.
- The 1,400+ scientists figure is the *potential research capacity*, not the *current agrifood deployment capacity*. Worth distinguishing.
- The federal strategy's *AI for All* name deliberately echoes the FCC's *Root AI* naming — both are positioned as broadly-accessible. Worth knowing because the rhetoric travels.
