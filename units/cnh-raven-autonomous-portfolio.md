## Content

CNH Industrial is the second-largest agricultural equipment manufacturer in North America by revenue (after John Deere), operating the **Case IH** and **New Holland Agriculture** brands globally. CNH's autonomous-farming posture is structurally distinct from John Deere's locked-in approach: rather than building autonomy into Deere-only equipment, CNH acquired **Raven Industries** in Q4 2021 (~$2.1B), giving CNH the autonomy and precision-ag IP stack to compete with Deere across mixed fleets.

Raven Industries, headquartered in Sioux Falls, South Dakota, was for 65 years a pioneer in precision agriculture — autosteer, boom control, application control, and software for mixed-fleet ag equipment. The acquisition closed the autonomy gap between CNH and Deere.

### Raven acquisition context

Per the June 21, 2021 Successful Farming coverage:
- **Acquisition value**: ~$2.1B.
- **Acquirer rationale** (Scott Wine, CNH CEO): *"Precision agriculture and autonomy are critical components of our strategy to help agricultural customers reach the next level of productivity and to unlock the true potential of their operations. Raven has been a pioneer in precision agriculture for decades, and their deep product experience, customer driven software expertise, and engineering acumen offer a significant boost to our capabilities."*
- **Raven rationale** (Dan Rykhus, Raven CEO): *"For 65 years, our company has been committed to solving great challenges. Part of that commitment includes delivering groundbreaking innovation by developing and investing in our core capabilities and technology."*

The deal closed Q4 2021.

### The autonomous portfolio (December 2022 CNH Tech Day)

Per CNH Industrial Tech Day, December 2022 (3blmedia.com):

**Driver Assist Harvest Solution.** Automation technology for the tractor platform, leveraging Case IH's harvest technology with Raven Autonomy integration. Provides coordinated control feature that allows the Driver Assist Harvest Solution to chart the path and speed of the tractor pulling the grain cart alongside a combine harvester during an "unload on the go" operation. Keeps the tractor perfectly in sync with the combine harvester when unloading grain — gives the combine operator seamless control over the unloading process. Commercially available for both Case IH and New Holland brands.

**Driverless Tillage Solution.** Advanced Perception System and Remote Command & Control from Raven Autonomy; functions with no operator in the cab. Can be operated remotely from a mobile tablet device. The operator can plan and execute precise, automated missions with consistent agronomic results monitored from anywhere. Combines the Case IH tractor and tillage platform with Raven autonomy.

**Baling Automation.** Industry-first Baler Automation on New Holland large square balers. LiDAR sensor scans the windrow in front of the tractor for density, volume, and direction. Tractor and baler use this input to automatically control steering, forward speed, and baler settings to ensure the baler follows the windrow precisely for accurate crop feeding. Case IH will also offer the baling automation feature.

**Case IH Trident 5550 with Raven Autonomy — Driverless Spreading Solution.** Announced at Farm Progress Show 2022 in Boone, Iowa as *"the agriculture industry's first autonomous spreader."* CNH and Raven debuted the autonomous Trident 5550 applicator. The driverless spreading solution provides coordinated control for the high-clearance applicator, with Raven's Perception System and remote command & control.

### Strategic posture

CNH framing (Derek Neilson, President Agriculture, December 2022 Tech Day): *"The agriculture industry is faced with the challenges of time, costs, constrained operational windows, and a dwindling labor force. Autonomy will become an integral part of farmers' operations everywhere... We believe that Autonomy and Automation technologies are fundamental to the future of farming. They cover all farmer segments and drive real world benefits."*

The CNH posture is **multi-brand and mixed-fleet compatible** — distinct from Deere's locked-in approach (See & Spray only on Deere sprayers). Raven's pre-acquisition customer base was multi-brand; CNH is preserving that positioning. The contrast with **AGCO PTx** (per `units/agco-ptx.md`) is meaningful: AGCO PTx is explicitly brand-agnostic *retrofit*, while CNH-Raven is brand-agnostic *via Raven platform + CNH OEM brands*.

### Deployment status (mid-2026)

- **Case IH Trident 5550 autonomous spreader**: announced 2022, commercial availability status to be verified.
- **Driver Assist Harvest**: commercially available for both Case IH and New Holland.
- **Driverless Tillage**: announced, demonstrated, commercial availability narrower than Deere See & Spray.
- **Baling Automation**: industry-first on New Holland large square balers; available.

Per the field guide's standing G-017 (US row-crop autonomous machinery operational scale beyond Deere), the structural finding from this scan: **announcements exceed deployments at every player except Deere See & Spray.** CNH's autonomous portfolio is more concrete than competitor announcements but operationally narrower than Deere's row-crop See & Spray coverage.

### Differentiation from AGCO PTx

CNH + Raven and AGCO PTx both position as mixed-fleet-compatible alternatives to Deere's locked-in approach, but they take structurally different paths:
- **CNH + Raven**: Raven platform acquired by CNH, integrated into CNH OEM brands (Case IH, New Holland). The autonomy IP stack lives inside CNH.
- **AGCO PTx**: brand-agnostic retrofit that works on competitor equipment, including Deere. PTx Trimble is a joint venture with Trimble (originally the third precision-ag player alongside Deere and AGCO).

The PTx Trimble JV is meaningful: Trimble was the original precision-ag platform vendor with multi-brand standard positioning. PTx Trimble positions Trimble as a multi-brand standard within the AGCO orbit, while Raven inside CNH is multi-brand-legacy but now CNH-aligned.

### Data governance and right-to-repair context

CNH cloud-mediated data flow parallels Deere's locked-in approach — the autonomy stack requires connectivity to CNH/Raven cloud, and operational data flows to the vendor. The FTC-Deere right-to-repair action (2025-2026) is the policy backdrop that affects CNH equally; CNH is one of the equipment vendors named in the broader right-to-repair conversation alongside Deere, AGCO, and John Deere's competitors.

## Why it matters for talks

- **The only US equipment vendor with operational scale comparable to Deere's row-crop AI ambitions** (alongside AGCO). Useful for any talk about US row-crop autonomy beyond the Deere-dominant frame.
- **The Raven-acquired vertical integration trajectory is a specific corporate strategy** — distinct from organic internal development (Deere) and from joint-venture multi-brand standard (AGCO PTx Trimble). Worth naming as a structural model.
- **The Trident 5550 "first autonomous spreader" framing is industry-side positioning** that signals where the company sees competitive differentiation.
- **The mixed-fleet compatibility via Raven's legacy is a structural counterweight to vendor lock-in** — worth mentioning in any talk about right-to-repair, data sovereignty, or farmer data rights.
- **The labour-substitution framing (Neilson, December 2022) is the canonical US equipment-vendor articulation** that autonomy is the strategic direction, not a niche product line.

## Critical context

- **Operational scale is narrower than Deere See & Spray.** No CNH-Raven acres-deployment figure disclosed at Deere-equivalent granularity.
- **The Trident 5550 commercial availability and deployment scale is to be verified** — announcement was 2022.
- **Raven platform inside CNH is increasingly CNH-aligned**, which may erode the multi-brand-legacy positioning over time. Worth tracking for the next scan.
- **CNH operations are multi-region**, but the autonomous portfolio's US deployment scale is the primary question; CNH also has substantial Brazilian operations with Case IH and New Holland.
- **Right-to-repair context applies to CNH as much as Deere.** The structural data-governance posture parallels Deere's.
- **The CNH + TIM (Brazil) rural connectivity partnership** (June 2026 announcement, ~R$77M investment) is a separate infrastructure initiative — CNH is investing in rural connectivity, which is itself a precondition for autonomous deployment at scale in regions with limited broadband.
