# WAICO — World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organisation

## Content

The **World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organisation (WAICO)** is the corpus's strongest single example of a *state-led multilateral governance institution* in the agrifood AI layer. WAICO was formally constituted on **16 July 2026** when 29 countries signed an agreement in Shanghai establishing it as an "independent intergovernmental international organization" headquartered in Shanghai. Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi signed on behalf of the Chinese government. UN Secretary-General António Guterres attended the signing ceremony as institutional observer.

Xi's keynote address at the 2026 World AI Conference the following day (17 July 2026) was the doctrinal framing: *"AI development should not be a solo performance by a single country, but a symphony of international cooperation."* He announced China would provide developing countries, including ASEAN, the Arab League, the Shanghai Cooperation Organisation, the African Union, Latin American countries, and BRICS members, with **5,000 research projects in AI over the next five years** — plus training, seminar programmes, and "cooperation centres."

**The 29 founding member states (as named in Xinhua primary):**
China, Russia, Pakistan, Kazakhstan, Laos, Belarus, Serbia, Indonesia, Malaysia, Cambodia, Myanmar, Brazil, South Africa, Senegal, Algeria, Cameroon, Congo, Ethiopia, Kenya, Lesotho, Mozambique, Zambia, Nicaragua, Cuba, Venezuela, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Uzbekistan, Oman.

**Notable absences:**
- **India** — *among the five founding BRICS countries, India is the notable absence*, reportedly aligned with a separate US-anchored AI governance initiative. This is the corpus's most direct empirical signal that the multilateral AI governance field is splitting rather than converging.
- **NA — US and Canada, EU — most EU member states, Japan, Korea** — none are listed among the 29 founders. The Western-led and East-Asia-non-China clusters have not joined WAICO at this signing.

**Agriculture as a named vertical.** The Xinhua agreement text positions WAICO around AI that is "beneficial, safe and fair" for "all humanity" — a frame that includes agriculture as one of the primary application verticals. Xi Jinping's announcement of 5,000 AI research projects for developing-country members explicitly includes regions where agriculture is a primary economic sector (ASEAN, AU, BRICS — many of whose members are agrarian economies). **However, at signing date (16-17 July 2026), no agriculture-specific deployment, no agriculture-vertical secretariat function, and no agriculture-named budget line had been publicly disclosed.** Per the corpus's vendor-figure hygiene discipline, treat the agriculture-vertical claim as *announced* but not yet *operationally documented*.

**The institutional design.** WAICO is structurally distinct from every prior AI governance forum the corpus has tracked:

| Forum | Lead | Members | Binding? | HQ/Host |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **WAICO (2026)** | China | 29 founding | Intergovernmental org | Shanghai |
| GPAI | OECD / Canada-France | 29 | Non-binding | OECD, Paris |
| G7 Hiroshima AI Process | Japan / G7 | 49 Friends | Voluntary code | OECD |
| Bletchley Declaration | UK | 29 signatories | Declaration | None |
| UN Global Dialogue on AI | UN General Assembly | 193 states | Non-negotiating dialogue | Geneva / NY |

Sources for this comparison: CSIS, Brookings, UN A/RES/79/325, Al Jazeera, Model Diplomat synthesis (19 sources).

**The Carnegie Endowment / Arindrajit Basu framing.** Per the Carnegie Endowment analysis (*China's Pivot on Global AI*, May 2026) and an ArXiv preprint on WAICO (2606.23860, 2026): *"WAICO is distinct from existing Western-led and UN-centric frameworks because its design emphasizes sovereignty and development over rights and safety."* The framing matters: WAICO is not a *Western-style* AI governance body (rights-and-safety-led). It is a *sovereignty-and-development-led* body — a substantive design choice that shapes how agricultural AI governance questions (data sovereignty, vendor-licensing, capacity-building) will be settled inside WAICO.

**Al Jazeera summary of Xi's framing.** Xi explicitly positioned WAICO as a response to what Beijing calls *"overstretching the national security concept"* — a phrase understood in policy-press as Beijing's formulation for US chip export controls. The audience understood. Worth surfacing because the corpus's agrifood-AI cluster-pattern observation (per `units/japan-korea-agrifood-ai-pattern.md`) draws a structural distinction between US, China, and EU approaches; WAICO is now the institutional posture through which China's approach is being multilateralised.

## Why it matters for talks

WAICO is the corpus's strongest single evidence that *agrifood AI governance is moving up the international-policy ladder.* Three talk-grade observations:

1. **The multilateralisation of agrifood AI governance.** Until 2025, the multilateral AI governance field was dominated by OECD-anchored Western clubs (GPAI, G7 Hiroshima AI Process, Bletchley Declaration) and a non-binding UN dialogue track. WAICO's standing-secretariat design is structurally new — a treaty-based intergovernmental institution with HQ and staff. This is *not* the same thing as a multilateral declaration. It is the operational layer that OECD-anchored clubs have not built.

2. **Agriculture as a *vertical* in the multilateral governance field.** This is the corpus's first clean empirical observation that *agricultural AI is being treated as a named multilateral-governance vertical*, distinct from health-AI, defence-AI, education-AI. Surface in any talk about how policy frames agricultural AI distinctively. The Xinhua agreement's "beneficial, safe and fair" frame includes agriculture implicitly; Xi's capacity-building announcement explicitly names regions where agriculture is a primary economic sector. The agriculture-vertical within WAICO is an *implementation choice* that the secretariat will work out in 2026-2028 — not a disclosed product at signing.

3. **The split multilateral field.** India's absence from WAICO (despite being a BRICS founder) is the corpus's most direct empirical signal that the multilateral AI governance field is *splitting*, not *converging*. Combined with the East-Asia cluster-pattern observation that Japan and Korea have not joined WAICO, the picture is: WAICO exists at one pole of a structurally splitting multilateral field, with Western-anchored initiatives at the other pole, and pivotal middle-powers (India is the canonical case) choosing which pole to align with.

**Three secondary presentations:**

- **The sovereignty-and-development vs rights-and-safety design choice.** Per Carnegie Endowment analysis: WAICO's design emphasizes *sovereignty and development over rights and safety* — a substantive design choice that will shape how data sovereignty and agricultural data rights questions get settled within WAICO. Compare to IEEE 2890-2025 (Indigenous data rights-led) and CARE Principles (rights-and-Indigenous-sovereignty-led): three different frameworks, three different design postures, three different answers.

- **WAICO and the cooperative-alternative frame.** WAICO is *not* a cooperative alternative to vendor capture (per archetype 04). It is a *state-led multilateral* alternative to Western-led multilateral governance. The two are parallel; conflating them is a category error.

- **The cluster-pattern observation.** Per `units/japan-korea-agrifood-ai-pattern.md`, the East-Asia cluster taxonomy now has three structural layers: (i) vendor-led industrial automation (Japan), (ii) state-anchored cluster programme (Korea), (iii) state-vendor hybrid extending to multilateral-state coordination (China via WAICO). This is the corpus's most current cluster-pattern taxonomy.

## Critical context

- **Agriculture-specific deployment within WAICO is V0 — announced, not documented.** Xinhua + Al Jazeera + India Today confirm the *capacity-building announcement*; none of them document a *deployed* agricultural AI initiative under WAICO at signing date. The corpus should not claim WAICO is "operating agricultural AI projects" — the right phrasing is "agriculture is a named vertical in the agreement frame, and 5,000 AI projects over five years are announced for developing-country members; agriculture-specific deployment is to be specified."

- **The signing ceremony is Xinhua-confirmed; the *substantive governance substance* is design-stage only.** WAICO's *form* (signed treaty, headquarters, signing ceremony with Guterres) is verified V1. WAICO's *substance* (operational secretariat, member-state implementation plans, vertical-specific working groups, agriculture governance mechanisms) is design-stage, not operational. Tag accordingly.

- **The five BRICS-and-WAICO comparison.** Of the five founding BRICS countries (Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa), four are WAICO founders and one — India — is not. Worth surfacing because BRICS-framing in India's global-South diplomacy is real; India's absence from WAICO is structurally significant. Surface as a contested-claim candidate (C-NNN) for the corpus: *whether India's WAICO absence reflects substantive disagreement on AI governance design or coordination lag.*

- **The verification gap on "Global South buy-in" framing.** Model Diplomat's framing (citing Carnegie Endowment) is that WAICO is timed to lock in *"Global South buy-in before the Western-led Global Dialogue on AI Governance matures."* This is a *substantive* claim, not a stated WAICO objective. Cite as analytical interpretation, not as primary source.

- **The XAG-style global export as the operational complement.** WAICO is the *multilateral-state posture*; XAG + DJI (`units/xag-china-drone-leader.md`, `units/dji-agriculture-global-export.md`) are the *operational export surfaces*. The corpus should distinguish between *institutional design* (WAICO, OECD, UN Global Dialogue) and *operational deployment* (vendor + state-cluster + DPI substrate). Both are real; they are different epistemic categories.
