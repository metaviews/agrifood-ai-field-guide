## Content

Uber Eats launched its *AI Cart Assistant* in 2026 — a generative AI feature inside the Uber Eats app that builds a checkout-ready cart from a text shopping list, a recipe photo, or other natural-language input. Independent coverage confirms the feature is live in 2026.

This is the consumer-facing counterpart to Uber Eats' 2023 ChatGPT integration (restaurant ordering, not grocery cart building). The 2026 *AI Cart Assistant* extends generative AI into the grocery-delivery flow rather than restaurant ordering.

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

Anchors the *food delivery × consumer generative AI* cell in the US. Sits alongside Ask Instacart (grocery delivery, earlier), Ask DoorDash (restaurant delivery + consumer cart builder), and the discontinued DoorDash restaurant voice-ordering pilot.

## Why it matters for talks

- Demonstrates that the *agentic commerce* framing (AI doing the cart-building for the consumer) has reached US food-delivery platforms.
- The text-or-photo input modality is more accessible than chat-only interfaces for time-constrained grocery shoppers.
- Useful for any talk comparing *retailer-owned* (Walmart Sparky, Loblaw PC Express) vs *platform-owned* (Uber Eats, Instacart, DoorDash) consumer AI surfaces.

## Critical context the unit does not sanitise

- AI Cart Assistant is a *consumer convenience* feature that also serves *platform lock-in*: once a consumer builds a cart through the assistant, they are more likely to default to Uber Eats for grocery delivery. This is a non-trivial commercial dynamic.
- The vendor does not publish the model's behaviour on dietary restrictions, allergen warnings, or substitution logic — these are exactly the categories where AI errors in food have safety implications.
