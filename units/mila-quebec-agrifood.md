## Content

Mila — Quebec Artificial Intelligence Institute is the Quebec node of Canada's Pan-Canadian AI Strategy (PCAIS), co-founded by Yoshua Bengio at Université de Montréal. Mila's "AI for Humanity" applied-projects portfolio includes **agriculture as one of eight named application domains**, alongside health, climate, environment, smart infrastructure, transportation, education, and ethics.

### The substantive corpus clarification — DISA is in Rwanda, not Canada

The corpus's `scans/2026-07-quebec-cycle.md` line 99 references Mila's **DISA project** ("Data-driven Insights for Sustainable Agriculture") and the gap G-029 asks about "Mila DISA partner farms and concrete deployed acreage" — with the implication that this is a Canadian agrifood deployment.

**Primary-source correction:** DISA is not a Canadian-deployed project. Per Mila's primary-source page on DISA: DISA is **"an interdisciplinary project that leverages the power of machine learning, satellite imagery, local knowledge and human-centred collaborations to promote an alternative approach to farming called regenerative agriculture"** — and **"our scope is initially focused on the Rwandan context, the objective is to scale the project in multiple countries across the continent."** The model is: **Canadian AI research capacity, Rwandan deployment (and broader Africa).**

DISA's substantive design:
- **Five-organisation collaboration:** Sustainability in the Digital Age (SDA), Future Earth, **Planet** (high-resolution satellite imagery provider), CIFOR-ICRAF, **Mila**.
- **Mila's role:** algorithms using Planet's high-resolution satellite imagery to identify regenerative and non-regenerative agricultural practices at scale in Rwanda.
- **Local collaborators:** Regional Research Center for Integrated Development (RCID) Rwanda + Bridge2Rwanda + McGill Mastercard Foundation Scholars' Program + ClimateWorks Foundation.
- **Stat frame:** Rwanda — "over 40% of the soils are degraded, with productivity continuing to decline… most smallholder farmers in Rwanda (over 60% of which are women) live on less than $5 a day."
- **Algorithmic output:** "high-resolution satellite imagery with algorithms that can identify regenerative and non-regenerative agricultural practices at scale."

This is a high-quality applied-research project but **not Canadian farmland**. G-029 should be reframed: Mila + Planet + CIFOR-ICRAF + Future Earth + SDA is a Canadian-research-pipelined Rwandan-deployment team; the substantive agrifood-AI finding is *the method* (satellite + ML for regenerative-ag identification at scale), not Canadian partner-farm acreage.

### IVADO — Quebec academic AI institute

IVADO (Institut de valorisation des données / Institute for Data Valorization) is Mila's sister institute, jointly run by Université de Montréal, HEC Montréal, and Polytechnique Montréal. IVADO's signature research scope is data science + AI + optimization + statistics with applications across health, transportation, social sciences, and **agriculture (R3AI)**.

**R3AI — Responsible AI for Regenerative Agriculture** has been the most-named IVADO agrifood positioning. The June 8, 2026 statement is forward-looking; substantive deployment outcomes are not yet visible in primary sources. **G-028 (existing): "IVADO R3AI substantive deployment (vs. positioning). The June 8, 2026 statement is forward-looking; substantive deployment outcomes not yet visible."**

### Innovasea partnership

A second Mila agrifood-adjacent project surfaced in 2025: **Innovasea (aquaculture technology vendor) partnered with Mila** to advance AI-powered fish farming. Per LinkedIn / Feed and Additive coverage (May 2025): "Innovasea partners with Mila to advance AI-powered [fish farming]." This is **a Canadian-vendor + Quebec-academic partnership** on aquaculture AI — substantively Canadian, with Mila supplying ML and Innovasea supplying the operational deployment context.

This is one of the few documented Canadian academic-vendor partnerships at the Quebec-AI × aquaculture intersection. Worth tracking as a substantive Quebec-academic agrifood AI deployment (small but real).

### Quebec academic-agrifood context

The Quebec academic-AI agrifood substrate beyond Mila + IVADO includes:
- **Université Laval** — Faculty of Agricultural and Food Sciences; precision agriculture research; not specifically AI-institute-tier.
- **McGill University** — MacDonald Campus (Ste-Anne-de-Bellevue); School of Human Nutrition; Faculty of Agricultural and Environmental Sciences; biological digital twin research (overlapping topic with Neethirajan at Dalhousie).
- **Université de Sherbrooke** — agricultural engineering research.
- **INRS** — natural-sciences AI-research-agrifood adjacency.
- **CRAAQ** (Centre de référence en agriculture et agroalimentaire du Québec) — referenced in Bioethics journal coverage (LeBlanc 2024) as a Quebec agricultural-data-and-policy actor.

The Quebec academic substrate has **national-scale ML capacity at Mila/IVADO + university-spread agrifood research** but no Quebec analog of AI4Food or AIDA as a flagship AI-agrifood institute. **Mila's agrifood profile is a portfolio of projects (DISA, R3AI, Innovasea partnership) rather than a Quebec-equivalent of an AI4Food or an AIDA institute.**

## Critical context

- **DISA is not a Canadian farm deployment.** The corpus should not represent DISA as a Canadian-agrifood deployment case; it is a Canadian-research + Rwandan-deployment collaboration. The substantive finding from DISA is the *method* (satellite + ML for regenerative-ag identification), not Canadian acreage.
- **R3AI's deployment tier is not yet surfaced.** Positioning paper June 2026; substantive deployment-of-record projects not named in primary sources.
- **Innovasea is the most concrete Quebec × vendor partnership** but Innovasea is a Boston-MA-headquartered vendor with Canadian operations; the *partnership* is Quebec-academic + international-vendor + Canadian-deployment.
- **No Quebec analog of AIDA or AI4Food.** Quebec has national AI scale at Mila + IVADO but no Quebec-specific agrifood-AI institute flagging the way AI4Food or AIDA do. Distinct provincial pattern.
- **The international footprint observation is the substantive corpus finding.** Mila + IVADO agrifood projects reach Rwanda (DISA) and Canadian aquaculture (Innovasea) — Canadian AI-research capacity pipeline to international + Canadian-agrifood, not Quebec-specific Canadian-deployment.
- **Mila + IVADO + Vector + Amii** are the four national AI hubs under PCAIS. Only Mila's agrifood surface is surfacing with named projects. Vector (Toronto) + Amii (Edmonton) + CIFAR's Pan-Canadian AI Strategy coordination layer are nominally adjacent; specific agrifood projects at Vector / Amii not surfaced in primary sources.
