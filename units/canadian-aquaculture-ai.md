## Content

Canadian aquaculture AI in mid-2026 is anchored by **a Pacific + Atlantic RAS + open-net-pen vendor ecosystem** with substantive deployment evidence and named institutional anchors.

### 1. ReelData AI (Halifax, NS)

**ReelData AI** (founded 2018, Halifax/Dartmouth HQ). **VC-funded by Buoyant Ventures**. Biomass-estimation camera, feed-management automation, behavioural-monitoring platform for land-based **recirculating aquaculture systems (RAS)**.

**Pilot deployments.**
- **Fifax (Finland) rainbow trout RAS.** Pilot documented in Land Based Aquaculture 2023 coverage.
- **Global RAS deployments** (number not publicly disclosed).

**Distinctive features.**
- Real-time biomass data via computer vision.
- Feed-management automation reduces overfeeding costs.
- Behavioural-monitoring flags abnormal patterns.

**ReelData is the largest Nova Scotia-based AI aquaculture vendor.** Anchors the **Atlantic Canada × aquaculture × computer-vision** cell.

### 2. OnDeck Fisheries AI (Vancouver, BC)

**OnDeck Fisheries AI** (Vancouver lead). Partners: Teem Fish Monitoring, Ha'oom Fisheries Society, Snap Information Technologies, Canadian Groundfish Research and Conservation Society, Ocean Wise, Goodfish Seafood Co., J.O. Thomas & Associates. **$1.5M from Canada's Ocean Supercluster under Pan-Canadian AI Strategy (PCAIS) Program.**

**Aims to make electronic monitoring (EM) of commercial fisheries 10–12× more affordable** for the projected surge in vessel coverage needed. **Caveat:** Project is positioned as building infrastructure, not as a deployed operational monitoring system yet. G-325.

**OnDeck is the PCAIS-funded side of Canadian fisheries AI** — largely unmined by the existing units (which focus on Quebec institutional substrate).

### 3. WellFish Tech (PEI BioAlliance)

**WellFish Tech** — Scotland-origin startup, expanded to Norway and Canada; **PEI presence via PEI BioAlliance.** Founded 2021. Combines production data + clinical chemistry + AI tools for **non-lethal blood assessment in aquaculture, wild stocks, research animals.**

**WellFish is the substantive Canadian aquaculture-animal-health AI deployment.** Distinct from Mowi Canada West Feed Centre (production AI).

### 4. Marine Thinking (Newfoundland)

**Marine Thinking** (Newfoundland and Labrador). AI for catch monitoring, autonomous detection and location of ghost gear in fishing waters. Project stage not detailed publicly. Source: Undercurrent News Jan 2025.

### 5. Mowi Canada West — Centralized AI-assisted Feed Centre (BC)

**Mowi Canada West** (subsidiary of Mowi ASA, world's largest Atlantic salmon farmer). **Centralized Feed Centre** using AI to integrate oxygen/temperature/salinity sensors with feeding behaviour; staff 100% focused on feeding, removed from farm sites.

**Caveat:** **2026 DFO transition plan for 79 BC salmon farms remains unresolved as of July 2026** — operational future of these AI deployments depends on policy outcomes. G-326.

**Mowi Canada West is distinct from Mowi Scotland's Remote Operations Centre.** The BC operation is rarely named separately.

### 6. Ocean Supercluster fish welfare AI

**Ocean Supercluster** has a documented H2S-sensor + AI-camera fish welfare project with **Grieg Seafood** for real-time monitoring of fish welfare.

### 7. Oceans North — Atlantic electronic monitoring pilot with AI (Nova Scotia)

**Oceans North** (NGO). Piloted electronic monitoring system with cameras + AI for at-sea observer program. Public framing: "At-sea observer program is 'broken,' advocates say, but electronic tools and AI could help." Pilot, not at scale.

## Substantive observation — the funding-stack pattern

Reading across the Canadian aquaculture AI vendor ecosystem:
- **ReelData** is venture-funded (Buoyant Ventures).
- **OnDeck** is PCAIS-funded (Ocean Supercluster).
- **WellFish** is venture-funded (Scotland-origin, PEI BioAlliance).
- **Marine Thinking** is publicly described as "Canadian company's AI project" (corporate or grant-funded).
- **Mowi Canada West** is corporate-funded (Mowi ASA subsidiary).
- **Ocean Supercluster H2S** is cluster-funded.
- **Oceans North** is NGO + DFO-context-funded.

**The same kind of AI capability (computer-vision fish monitoring) shows up under each funding regime**, but with **different data-governance postures** — corporate-funded (Mowi) tends toward proprietary stacks; PCAIS-funded (OnDeck) tends toward public infrastructure; venture-funded (ReelData) tends toward proprietary but customer-licensed.

This is the **substantive Canadian structural pattern**: **the regulatory-funder mix shapes the data-sovereignty architecture**, not just the deployment. Same CV-on-fish capability looks structurally different depending on which Canadian funding regime produced it.
