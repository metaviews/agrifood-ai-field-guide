## Content

JBS S.A. — the world's largest beef processor and Brazil's flagship cattle-industry corporate — committed in September 2020 to monitor **100% of its indirect cattle suppliers by 2025**, using blockchain and other digital tools as the substrate. The pledge was made explicitly in response to Reuters and Greenpeace Unearthed reporting on Amazon-cattle supply-chain traceability gaps. JBS also made a $9 million investment at COP-28 (Dubai, December 2023) to support **mandatory traceability in Pará state** and improve livelihoods of small producers in the Amazon region.

**Documented scope (per JBS corporate and trade press through 2020-2025):**

- **100% indirect-supplier monitoring by 2025** — the headline corporate commitment. Verified through 2025? JBS has published *some* progress indicators through its annual sustainability reports but *independent third-party audit* of indirect-supplier coverage at scale is not surfaced as of mid-2026.
- **$9M COP-28 investment** for Pará-state mandatory-traceability support + small-producer identification tags and technical assistance (over 3 years per JBS COP-28 announcement).
- **Animal identification tags** for small-holders to enable individual-animal traceability into the blockchain.

**The mechanism, per JBS corporate framing:**

- **Direct-supplier monitoring** (already in place pre-2020): JBS historically had visibility into its direct suppliers — the slaughterhouse suppliers who deliver cattle directly.
- **Indirect-supplier monitoring** (the 2025 commitment): suppliers who sell to JBS's direct suppliers — typically the *ranching operations* responsible for cattle-raising and which are most often implicated in Amazon deforestation. JBS commits to monitoring them by 2025.
- **Blockchain as the substrate** for cattle-supply data, providing immutable records of cattle origin and movement.
- **Electronic ear tags / RFID** for individual-animal identification (the COP-28 small-producer programme subsidises these tags).

**Verifying the 100% commitment — what's actually achieved?**

Per Greenpeace Unearthed (July 2022): JBS commits to monitoring "100% of indirect suppliers by 2025" but the actual deployment as of 2022 was largely confined to the direct-supplier tier. The 2025 target was a *future commitment*. As of mid-2026, independent auditing of whether JBS has achieved the 2025 target has not surfaced in public sources. The corporate side maintains the commitment is in progress; the critical-voice side maintains the gap remains.

Per Mighty Earth Rapid Response #7 (April 2026): *JBS bought cattle from two farms linked to nearly 1,400 hectares of deforestation in the Amazon rainforest in 2025* — direct adversarial evidence that JBS's supply chain *still* contains embedded-deforestation cattle after the 2025 target date. **This is the corpus's clearest single-source critical-voice evidence that JBS's traceability programme did not achieve its 2025 target by the announced date.**

**Cross-verification tier:**

- JBS corporate framing of 2020 commitment: tier-1 + tier-3 trade press. The commitment is real; the *achievement* is contested.
- JBS COP-28 $9M Pará announcement: tier-1 (corporate PR).
- **Independent verification of 100% indirect-supplier monitoring at scale (i.e., the post-2025 claim)**: not surfaced. Track as G-106.
- Critical-voice evidence of post-2025 supply-chain continuation: tier-2 (Mighty Earth Rapid Response #7).

**JBS global presence:** JBS USA is the corpus's *already-documented* sister entity (e.g., `jbs-usa-volur-carcass-sorting.md` documents JBS USA's beef cut-plan AI at Hyrum, UT, in a 6-month POV announced January 2023). JBS Brazil (this unit) is the *procurement-side* AI deployment; JBS USA is the *processing-line* AI deployment — sibling Brazilian-and-American corporate operations with structurally distinct AI deployment shapes.

## Critical context

- **The 2025 target not independently verified at scale as of mid-2026.** This is the corpus's most critical-voice-critical Brazilian beef AI unit; the substantive achievement gap is itself the structural finding worth surfacing.
- **The cluster pattern needs to be tempered by accountability evidence**: the Mighty Earth 2026 finding (JBS cattle from 2 farms linked to 1,400 ha Amazon deforestation) shows the *direct cost* of the gap between corporate commitment and operational reality. The corpus should not paper over this with vendor framing.
- **The $9M COP-28 Pará investment** is the corpus's clearest *funding-flow* evidence linking AI deployment to public-policy climate finance in Brazilian beef. The Pará state programme provides individual-animal identification tags for small producers — a concrete *upstream technology transfer* pattern useful for talks on AI-and-smallholder-substitution.
- **The JBS-related critical-voice framing** consistently positions JBS as the *largest and most-scrutinised* Brazilian beef processor; the other big-three (Marfrig, Minerva) also face critical-voice scrutiny but at smaller scale. Worth surfacing: even when the *biggest* corporate makes commitments, the *operational delivery* is not commensurate. This is a structural observation about corporate accountability, not about JBS specifically.
- **The blockchain framing** is consistent across JBS, Minerva, Marfrig, and the Brazilian leather segment: all four cite blockchain as the *integrity substrate* for cattle traceability data. The corpus's broader blockchain-vs-AI distinction: blockchain is the data-layer substrate; AI is the *pattern-recognition overlay* on top. JBS's blockchain programme is largely *data-layer substrate*; Minerva's is *data-layer substrate + pattern-recognition overlay*. The distinction matters for vendor-figure-hygiene discipline.
- **Georeferencing + satellite monitoring** is the cross-corporate baseline. JBS-specific deployment figures for satellite-based indirect-supplier surveillance are not surfaced at the same primary-source level Minerva provides for satellite integration.
- **The Brazilian beef AI cluster as a whole should be characterised as "traceability-promise-with-gap-to-operational-reality"**: the corporate commitments are real; the achievement-as-promised is not independently verified; critical-voice reporting shows the gap.
