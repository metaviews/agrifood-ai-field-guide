## Content

Greater Montréal is the epicentre of a substantial Quebec agtech cluster dedicated to the development and marketing of innovative agricultural technologies. The cluster spans precision agriculture, vertical farming, smart farming, agrobiotechnology, automation, and greenhouse technology.

**Concrete cluster data (Montréal International / Investment Monitor, 2026):**
- 220+ organisations in Quebec agtech sector
- 130+ agtech businesses in Quebec integrating AI
- 250+ members and partners in Zone Agtech
- 13 universities and research centres
- C$400M+ in funding
- Zone Agtech is in L'Assomption, ~15 km², on a former manufacturing plant

**Named businesses in the cluster (per Montréal International):**
- **Sollum** — sun-as-a-service AI greenhouse lighting. See `sollum-sun-as-a-service.md`.
- **ChrysaLabs** — photonics + AI for soil nutrient measurement.
- **SoraLINK** — AI predictive maintenance deployed across Quebec food processors. See `soralink-export-food-processing.md`.
- **Savoura** — large-scale greenhouse tomato producer.
- **Lufa Farms** — rooftop farming pioneer.
- **GoodLeaf Farms** — 100,000ft² indoor microgreen farm in Greater Montréal (vertical farming).
- **Hoogendoorn** (Dutch) — Quebec deployment via IIVO + LetsGrow.com.

**The cluster is positioned as Quebec's response to:**
- The 37.5% GHG reduction target by 2030 (Quebec)
- The carbon-neutral target by 2050
- The local food production capacity increase target (reduces GHG from food transport + increases food autonomy)
- The federal *AI for All* strategy's regional implementation

The Montréal International framing: "The city's expertise in AI is a real plus as it helps propel several high-value industries forward, agtech being one of them. The presence of Mila, a world-renowned AI hub with more than 1,100 researchers, and IVADO, a unique centre of excellence in AI with more than 150 partners and 1,400 scientists working together with key industry players to foster responsible AI technology, makes the city the perfect place to develop solutions for the environmental challenges we are facing, such as climate change."

## Why it matters for talks

- The cluster scale (220+ organisations, 130+ AI businesses, C$400M funding) is the most concrete cluster-level data point in the field guide.
- The cluster is *vertically integrated* across the agrifood value chain — production, processing, distribution, retail — distinct from single-segment clusters.
- The Mila + IVADO + Zone Agtech structure is the *Quebec implementation model* for *AI for All*. Worth knowing for any talk about Canadian AI implementation.
- The cluster's positioning on the food-sovereignty / GHG-reduction / carbon-neutral axes is *Quebec-specific policy context*, distinct from the Ontario context (which is more about productivity / market competitiveness).

## Critical context

- The 130+ AI-integrating businesses figure is from Montréal International; methodology for counting "AI integration" is not specified.
- The C$400M+ funding figure for Zone Agtech is aggregate public + private investment over the project's life; per-year run rate not specified.
- The cluster is positioned as export-ready (in terms of both products and IP) but international deployment by named cluster businesses not enumerated.
- "Cluster" as a structural form is Canadian-specific — many of the named businesses are also exported (Sollum to international greenhouse markets; Lufa Farms to other Canadian cities).
