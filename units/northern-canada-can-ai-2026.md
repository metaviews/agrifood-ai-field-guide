## Content

The **CanNor February 6 2026 announcement** is the first documented Northern Canadian AI funding cycle with named recipients in primary sources. **Total: $2,815,999 across four projects** spanning Yukon, Northwest Territories, and Nunavut. Programs used: **IDEANorth** + **REGI – AI**.

### The four CanNor projects (verified)

**1. AI Driven Entrepreneurship and Business Support Centre — Prosper NWT (Northwest Territories).**
- 3 years. Up to **$2,316,000** from IDEANorth.
- Suite of AI-powered business tools + in-person/online AI training for business owners.

**2. Digital Literacy, Online Safety, and AI Training Program — Inuvik Tech Society.**
- 2 years. Up to **$200,000** from IDEANorth.
- Targets the **Gwich'in Settlement Region and Inuvialuit Settlement Region** (a region that "only recently gained reliable, high-speed connectivity").
- Mobile training lab, workshops in seven communities.

**3. Empowering Inuit-led Economic Growth Through AI and Innovation — Nunavut Economic Developers Association.**
- 3 years. Up to **$200,000** from REGI – AI.
- Two AI/ML tools: (a) data-driven AI tool for community-level economic development plans in Nunavut municipalities; (b) AI/ML-powered grant and proposal writing tool **"to enable Nunavut communities to independently draft applications aligned with their unique priorities and cultural context."**
- **First documented Indigenous-led AI deployment with federal funding.**

**4. AI platform for Arctic sensor — DeltaVue Inc. (Whitehorse, Yukon).**
- 1 year. Up to **$99,999** from REGI – AI.
- Software that interprets and standardizes a wide array of sensor data for northern governments/researchers/industries.

### PolArctic / Sanikiluaq mariculture AI (Nunavut)

**PolArctic LLC** + Nunavut Fishery Association + Qikiqtaaluk Corporation + WWF-Arctic + C-CORE + Sanikiluaq community (Inuit). **First AI model to treat Indigenous Knowledge and Western science as equals during training/validation.** Inputs: Nunavut Coastal Resource Inventory (NCRI) maps, Indigenous community knowledge, satellite remote sensing, sea-surface temperature, bathymetry. Output: predicted habitat zones for scallops, clams, kelp near Sanikiluaq. Results in Inuktitut and English. **Pilot completed 2021.** Operational status (production mariculture?) unclear — primary follow-up needed. **G-324.**

### Indigenous-led AI deployment — substantive observation

**The CanNor Nunavut Economic Developers Association project is the first documented Indigenous-led AI deployment with federal funding in primary sources.** This is substantively distinct from the existing Indigenous data sovereignty framework work (OCAP + CARE + IEEE 2890) because it is *operational*, not framework-only.

**The Inuvik Tech Society's project** explicitly notes "a region that only recently gained reliable, high-speed connectivity" — confirming the binding constraint documented in the constraint/critical scout §2: rural connectivity is the precondition for any Northern AI deployment.

**Prosper NWT's $2.316M / 3 years** is the largest of the four CanNor projects. The framing is *economic-development-AI*, not *agrifood-AI* — but the Nunavut economy is heavily food-and-fisheries-based, so the structural adjacency to agrifood AI is real.

### The binding-constraint dynamic

**The RAII / Universal Broadband Fund tension** is the binding constraint for Northern AI. RAII is the federal AI SME funding layer that scales ($200M Budget 2024 → $500M AI for All); the **Universal Broadband Fund (UBF)** is the rural connectivity layer that does *not* renew. The Wire Report (industry source, 27 May 2026): "Ottawa will not renew its multi-billion dollar funding program designed to connect every household to high-speed internet by 2030." ISED confirmed (5 June 2026) it will not raise the 50/10 Mbps minimum standard. Auditor General of Canada baseline (2021, still the reference): 90.9% national coverage, but only **42.9% on First Nations reserves** and 59.5% in rural/remote.

For Northern AI deployment to work, both RAII (AI funding) and UBF (rural connectivity) must scale together. RAII's scale-up is good news; UBF's non-renewal is the binding constraint.

## Critical context

- **PolArctic Sanikiluaq operational status is unclear.** The 2021 pilot completion is documented; whether the AI tool is now in production mariculture is not. G-324.
- **The Nunavut Economic Developers Association project is Inuit-led but not specifically agrifood-AI.** It's an economic-development-AI tool.
- **The CanNor projects are 1–3 year windows.** Post-project sustainability not addressed.
- **The UBF non-renewal** is the binding constraint for any Northern AI deployment. RAII's scale-up does not solve the connectivity constraint.
- **The Prosper NWT $2.316M / 3 years** is large by CanNor standards but small relative to the $750M+ aggregate Scale AI "collective investment" figure.
