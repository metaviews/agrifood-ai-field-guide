## Content

The **National Agricultural Producers Data Cooperative (NAPDC) / Ag Data Coop** is a US-based effort to develop a national framework for producer data, with University of Nebraska as the institutional lead. Funded by USDA National Institute of Food and Agriculture (NIFA).

### Concrete deployment figures

- **$957,350 cumulative award** (USDA NIFA Grant No. 2023-77039-41033)
- **Project period**: September 1, 2023 — August 31, 2026
- **Lead institution**: University of Nebraska
- **Animal health component**: 30%
- **Research effort categories**: 20% Basic, 30% Applied, 50% Developmental
- **Knowledge areas**: Communication/Education/Information Delivery (60%); Sociological/Technological Change Affecting Individuals/Families/Communities (40%)

### Concrete activities

- **2024 NAPDC Conference** produced white paper
- **2025 NAPDC Conference (CANVAS 2025)** — preview of speaker line-up
- **June 2026 newsletter** — leadership reflections, TAPS National Competition
- **2025 Impact Report** available
- **300+ sign-ups** on the listserv (OPT-IN process for participants and stakeholders)

### Four named goals

1. **Leverage and enhance ongoing projects** that support stakeholders and collaborative partnerships to demonstrate effectiveness and scalability of digital resources in capturing, storing, and accessing producer and public data.
2. **Develop a clear and complete sustainability plan** for updates, expansion, and user support that delineates principles and examples of specific mechanisms for capacity integration.
3. **Develop feasible appropriate assurances of regulatory and legal compliance** including a **data trust structure** and FAIR principles.
4. **Further next steps for framework growth** through engagement and support of diverse participation in NAPDC activities.

### Connected ecosystem

NAPDC's ecosystem is broader than just the cooperative:
- **AgGateway** (US agricultural data standards body) — partner. AgGateway's ADAPT Standard won 2026 AE50 Award.
- **Ag Data Transparent** (certification) — partner/supporter.
- **USDA NIFA** — funder.
- **University of Nebraska** — institutional lead.

This is a **public-private-academic partnership** model. Distinct from JoinData's producer-led non-profit cooperative. The NAPDC is more *framework development* than operational deployment.

### What NAPDC is and isn't

- **Is**: federally-funded framework-development effort. Multi-stakeholder public-private-academic partnership.
- **Is not**: an operational data sharing platform like JoinData. NAPDC is building the *framework* that operational platforms could implement.
- **Is not**: a vendor (no commercial product).
- **Is not**: a certification body (that's Ag Data Transparent).

### Critical observation

NAPDC's "data trust structure" is an explicit framework design. A data trust is a legal structure where a third party manages data on behalf of beneficiaries. Distinct from a cooperative (where the farmers themselves manage the data). Worth understanding the distinction.

NAPDC is the *policy / framework* layer of the US data cooperative movement. JoinData is the *operational* layer. OADA is the *open-source standards* layer. Three different positions in the data cooperative ecosystem.

## Why it matters for talks

- $957,350 federal investment in producer data cooperative framework development is concrete.
- The four-goal structure (leverage, sustainability, compliance, growth) captures the substantive work.
- The "data trust structure" framing is a distinct legal design from cooperative model.
- The public-private-academic partnership model is structurally different from the Dutch cooperative model.

## Critical context

- NAPDC is in *framework development* stage, not operational deployment. The data trust structure is being designed; it isn't yet operational at the scale JoinData is.
- $957,350 is the cumulative federal grant. Operational funding model post-grant not yet specified.
- 300+ listserv sign-ups is engagement, not deployment.
- The "white paper" outputs are policy documents, not operational infrastructure.
- The animal health component (30%) is substantial — worth knowing for any talk that engages with US livestock data infrastructure.
