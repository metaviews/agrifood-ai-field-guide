## Content

The **Agri-Food Analytics Lab (AAL)** is a Dalhousie University research lab, primarily housed at the Faculty of Agriculture (Truro NS), led by **Dr. Sylvain Charlebois** as **Scientific Director**. The lab describes itself as a research-sharing platform that studies critical issues shaping Canada's agri-food sector: "sustainable farming practices, food safety, food pricing, food waste, and the impact of emerging technologies."

### Critical voice — the data-deficit frame

Charlebois's positioning is **pragmatic-critical** — distinct from Neethirajan's *rural-policy* critical voice and Dara's *greenhouse-AI-and-cyber-risk* voice. Charlebois frames AI as **necessary** for Canadian food safety and traceability, but argues that Canada's *data* substrate is insufficient to realise AI benefits. Two named pieces:

- **"The Problem Isn't AI. It's Data"** (Charlebois, Substack, July 2026) — the canonical data-deficit statement.
- **"Food safety needs an AI upgrade"** (Charlebois, Substack) — the food-safety AI framing.
- **"The missing ingredient in Ottawa's AI strategy"** (Charlebois, Toronto Sun, June 2026) — argues the AI for All strategy is data-starved.

The framing is **adoption-without-data**: Canada lacks the data substrate to actually realise AI benefits, not just that AI lacks regulation. Charlebois thus complements Neethirajan (rural + Green AI framing) and Dara (greenhouse + cyber risk). The three together form the substantive plural Canadian critical-voice cluster.

### Signature outputs (verified)

AAL publishes annually:
- **Canada's Food Price Report** (annual flagship). Examples: 2024, 2025, 2026 editions. Annual consumer-facing food-inflation forecast; widely cited in Canadian media.
- **Top 10 Food Stories** (annual): an end-of-year roundup. The 2025 edition is published.
- **Canadian Food Sentiment Index** (quarterly): a consumer-behaviour tracking series. The Volume 2 series is current through Spring 2025.
- **Canadian Food Manufacturing — An Overview in 2010, 2020 and Forecast to 2030** (Charlebois 2025, cited 1 per scholaris).
- **Most Influential Nations in Global Agri-Food Ranking**: a recurring country-comparison series.
- **Food safety surveys** (April 2024 with Caddle, 9,109 Canadians surveyed).
- **Best Before dates and food waste** (with Angus Reid).
- **AI Report** (a specific AI-and-food AAL output; not yet surfaced in detail).
- **Greedflation series** (multiple Charlebois pieces on grocery pricing).
- **Cashless Grocery Stores / Customer Service / Grocer Distrust / Farmers Trust Survey / Loblaws Report / Plant-based products**: a wide portfolio of consumer-trust and food-system research.

### Distinguishing features

1. **Consumer-facing.** AAL's outputs are often consumer-facing — the Food Price Report and Top 10 Food Stories are widely covered by Canadian media. The *Charlebois voice* is a media-recognised Canadian agrifood-policy voice.
2. **AI as a *framing*, not a *technique*.** AAL's explicit AI-focused outputs (AI Report; "Food safety needs an AI upgrade"; "missing ingredient in Ottawa's AI strategy") are *position pieces* about AI's role in Canadian food systems rather than AI-technique deployment work. This is the data-deficit frame: AI is the lens; the substantive finding is *about data substrate*.
3. **Multi-discipline / cross-faculty.** AAL is hosted at Dalhousie Faculty of Agriculture (Truro NS) but reaches across the Faculty of Management (Charlebois is also cross-appointed); the lab's work covers food pricing, food waste, food safety, food sovereignty, GLP-1 / consumer behaviour, and AI policy. The depth-breadth axis is broader than peer research labs.
4. **Co-located with AIDA.** AIDA (`units/aida-atlantic-digital-agriculture.md`) launched at Dalhousie Faculty of Agriculture (Bible Hill / Truro NS) in November 2025. AAL has been operating longer and covers the consumer + policy frame; AIDA covers the academic research + engineering frame. **Two agrifood-AI-relevant institutes at the same faculty.** Structurally distinct positions.

### Strengths and limits

**Strengths:**
- The Canadian agri-food-policy *media voice* — Charlebois is widely cited in Canadian media (CBC, Globe & Mail, National Post, Toronto Star, etc.).
- The data-deficit critique is *distinct* from the regulatory-critique voice (UFCW/CLC) and from the rural-critique voice (Neethirajan). AAL contributes the *adoption-substrate* critique.
- Annual Canada's Food Price Report is the canonical Canadian food-economics consumer-trust survey series.

**Limits:**
- AAL is primarily an *analytical and policy* lab, not an engineering / deployment institute. It is positioned differently from AI4Food (U Guelph) or AIDA (Dalhousie).
- Limited industry-facing deployment: AAL's outputs are *consumer-facing media pieces* and *academic publications*, not deployed AI systems.
- The "Food Professor Podcast" (Charlebois-hosted) is a media output, not a deployment vehicle.
- Specific AI-technique deployment (computer vision, NLP, predictive ML, etc.) at AAL is not named in primary sources; the data-deficit frame does not require technique-deployment evidence.

## Why it matters for talks

1. **The data-deficit voice is one of three Canadian critical voices.** Neethirajan / Dara / Charlebois are the substantive plural critical-landscape. Charlebois's voice is the *adoption-substrate* one; without it, talks about Canadian agrifood AI risk over-emphasising regulation and under-emphasising data substrate.
2. **Media visibility.** Charlebois is the *most media-visible* Canadian agrifood academic voice. Any talk on Canadian food system will likely encounter Charlebois positions in the press. The unit lets the corpus speak back accurately.
3. **Annual flagship outputs as engagement lever.** Canada's Food Price Report + Top 10 Food Stories + Canadian Food Sentiment Index are recurring outputs that command public attention. Talks can use them as proxies for "what is the state of Canadian food economics."
4. **Co-location with AIDA.** Two Dalhousie Faculty of Agriculture institutes (AAL + AIDA) define the Atlantic + Canadian agri-food academic critical-voice position.

## Critical context

- **Charlebois's voice precedes AIDA by years.** AAL is the older of the two Dalhousie Faculty of Agriculture institutes; AIDA launched November 2025. The substantive Canadian critical voice on AI was already established via AAL before AIDA launched.
- **AAL is consumer-and-policy research, not AI technique deployment.** Distinct from AI4Food (U Guelph), AIDA, Neethirajan's lab. AAL's value to talks is the *critical-voice frame*, not technique-deployment evidence.
- **The food-safety / GLP-1 / consumer-trust series are adjacent to AI but not AI-specific.** AAL's series range across food-system topics; only some pieces engage AI directly. The AI-as-framing observation is real but should not be over-claimed.
- **The "missing ingredient in Ottawa's AI strategy" + "the problem isn't AI, it's data"** are the load-bearing critical pieces. They position AAL on the data-deficit flank of the Canadian AI critical-voice landscape.
