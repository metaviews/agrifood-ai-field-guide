## Content

Headwall Photonics (Bolton MA) and Laitram Machinery (New Orleans LA) announced on 29 April 2025 a partnership to deploy hyperspectral imaging + AI in NA food processing. The initial use case named in the partnership announcement: **shrimp processing**, with explicit expansion scope to poultry, fruits, vegetables, nuts, and pet foods.

**What hyperspectral + AI does for shrimp processing:**
- Captures spectral data beyond visible RGB — distinguishing chemical/biological composition differences that humans and conventional CV cannot
- Detection of defects / contaminants / species variations inside and outside shrimp shells
- Food-safety screening (e.g. melanosis, blackspot, off-odour precursors)
- Yield / grade classification (not just defect removal)

**Laitram also separately sells the SMART Sorter in poultry** — referenced in a 2026 peer-reviewed paper (`Comprehensive Reviews in Food Science and Food Safety`, Tandfonline DOI 10.1080/10408398.2026.2651887). The Headwall+Laitram 2025 partnership extends that product line into hyperspectral + AI category.

**Why this unit matters:**
- Hyperspectral + AI in food processing is a small but growing sub-cell of NA food-processing AI. The Headwall+Laitram partnership is one of the named-US-vendor partnerships.
- The "expansion to poultry / fruits / vegetables / nuts / pet foods" claim is a roadmap signal — as written it is scope announcement, not deployed footprint, but the *language* indicates which sub-sectors the vendors intend to enter.
- Seafood / shrimp is the smallest-named NA protein-processing sector for AI. Filling that cell.

## Why it matters for talks

- *Hyperspectral + AI is structurally different from RGB-CVIS in food.* The spectral bands distinguish chemical composition, not just visible defects. Worth naming in talks because hyperspectral is the bridge between *sorting* (TOMRA-style) and *sensing* (IoT-style) — it sits across both cells.
- *Headwall (MA) + Laitram (LA) is a regional NA partnership example.* Two US-based vendors with NA plant-integration via Laitram — a structural fit that isn't always available in NA food AI where hyperspectral often comes from EU vendors (e.g. Tomra).
- *Seafood / shrimp processing AI is sparse in the field guide.* This unit fills a small cell. Worth surfacing that the *expansion roadmap* signals where the vendors think the cell is going (poultry, fruits, vegetables, nuts, pet foods).
- *The Apr 2025 vintage makes this the most recently-announced NA food-processing AI partnership in the knowledge base.* Worth tracking as a fresh example of in-vogue vendor pairings.

## Critical context

- "Piloting" status is conservative — the partnership has been announced (Apr 2025) with an initial shrimp-processing use case; deployment at scale across plants has not been demonstrated in publicly-sourced material.
- Customer cases (which shrimp processor, which plant, which line) are not publicly named at this point.
- Hyperspectral imaging hardware is more capital-intensive than RGB cameras; deployment scope per plant is meaningfully smaller than TOMRA multi-sorter fleets.
- The "expansion to poultry / fruits / vegetables / nuts / pet foods" is roadmap, not deployed footprint.
- Laitram SMART Sorter in poultry is separately deployed (per peer-review citation); whether the hyperspectral integration spans both SMART Sorter and Headwall product lines is not specified.
