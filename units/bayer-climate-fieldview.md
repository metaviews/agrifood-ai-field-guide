## Content

Bayer's *Climate FieldView* is the most geographically-spread digital farming platform. Launched 2015 in the US, expanded to Canada, Brazil, Argentina, and most European markets, then to South Africa, Kenya, and other markets.

**Concrete figures from Bayer (November 2024):**
- Available in 23 countries.
- More than 250 million subscribed acres globally.
- (For comparison: 2020 figure was ~150 million acres across 20+ countries. Growth trajectory is real.)

The platform aggregates farm data from equipment, sensors, and field operations, then layers decision-support tools on top — yield analysis, application analysis, harvest progress. Bayer positions it as the data backbone that connects Bayer's seed and crop protection business to the farm.

**Bayer's framing.** From the November 2024 release: "We recently fielded a survey which gathered feedback from 2,000 farmers around the world. Among those using digital tools, farmers overwhelmingly reported improved crop yields, cost savings, and improved crop quality." Vendor-reported survey result.

**Distinctive structural fact.** Climate FieldView is *not* a precision spraying system or autonomous machinery. It is a **data aggregation and decision-support layer**. Bayer sells it alongside seeds and crop protection products. The data layer feeds Bayer's R&D and sales — which is structurally the IPES-Food critical configuration: input vendor owns the data layer.

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

Anchors the *input-vendor data layer* pattern at multi-continent scale. Distinct from equipment-vendor lock-in (Deere See & Spray, where data flows with the machine) and from independent platform (xFarm / Agrosmart / Taranis, where data flows to an independent platform). Three market structures, three competing models.

## Why it matters for talks

- The canonical example of input vendor controlling the farm data layer. The structural critique (IPES-Food, Monsalve/Seufert) maps directly here.
- The 250 million acres figure is the largest platform-scale number we've surfaced anywhere — useful for showing that agritech platforms operate at very large scale.
- Useful comparison: Climate FieldView's vendor-neutral interface (works with multiple equipment brands) vs Deere See & Spray's equipment-locked approach. The data layer is vendor-neutral; the actuation layer is vendor-locked.

## Critical context

- 250M subscribed acres is vendor-reported from Bayer's own release.
- Bayer-commissioned farmer survey (2,000 farmers, "overwhelmingly reported" improved outcomes) — vendor survey design and vendor interpretation of results.
- The structural critique (input vendor owns data layer) is not a "Bayer is bad" claim. The platform does useful farm-level work. The critique is that the data flow favours Bayer's R&D and sales operation in ways farmers don't fully control.
- Recent legal context: June 2026 US Supreme Court sided with Bayer on Roundup case (per Fence Post, June 2026). Bayer's commercial and legal position in the agrichemical space remains strong.
