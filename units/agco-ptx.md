## Content

AGCO's *PTx* precision agriculture portfolio was launched in April 2024, integrating Precision Planting (acquired by AGCO) and PTx Trimble (joint venture with Trimble). AGCO 2024 net sales were approximately $11.7 billion.

**The distinctive design choice.** PTx is explicitly *brand-agnostic*. From AGCO's May 2025 announcement: "Base Technology dealers will have the opportunity to retrofit multiple brands of equipment with PTx guidance and steering technology. Full Line Precision Ag dealers provide solutions for every season through PTx retrofit technologies from PTx Trimble and Precision Planting for all machinery brands." This includes advanced solutions for water management and controls for planters, seeders, sprayers, and other implements.

**Deployment progress (May 2025):**
- Integrated into 50+ additional Fendt and Massey Ferguson dealers in the US and Canada in the first year of PTx Trimble.
- Goal to equip all of AGCO's North American production agriculture dealerships with PTx Trimble guidance and steering portfolio by end of 2025.
- PTx Trimble releases retrofit platform with eventual autonomous capabilities.

**Strategic significance.** PTx is the practical answer to the *vendor lock-in* critique. While Deere's See & Spray works only on Deere equipment, AGCO's PTx retrofits *any* brand. This is structurally distinct from the equipment-vendor lock-in model.

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

Anchors the *brand-agnostic retrofit* pattern — equipment-vendor approach that explicitly accommodates mixed fleets. Together with Deere See & Spray (equipment-locked) and Climate FieldView (data-layer, vendor-neutral interface), PTx completes the three competing US/EU market structures.

## Why it matters for talks

- The structural contrast with Deere See & Spray is a useful talk slide. Two major equipment makers, two different models — Deere locks in, AGCO opens up.
- AGCO's commitment to mixed-fleet compatibility is itself a signal — industry players see lock-in backlash as a market force worth addressing.
- PTx Trimble's retrofit-with-autonomous-capabilities roadmap suggests the mixed-fleet pattern may persist even as autonomy becomes more central.

## Critical context

- 50+ additional dealers in year one is real growth but is the *AGCO dealer network* — not the same as 50+ independent operators choosing PTx.
- The autonomous capability is *announced roadmap*, not deployed. Cross-reference G-017 (US row-crop autonomous machinery operational scale beyond Deere).
- The brand-agnostic framing is also a commercial strategy — it puts AGCO equipment in a position to capture retrofit spend regardless of which brand of equipment a farmer operates.
