## Content

Apeel Sciences, founded 2012, uses plant-based coatings (derived from fats and lipids found in peels, seeds, and pulp) to slow dehydration and oxidation in fresh produce. The coatings are applied to avocados, citrus, cucumbers, mangoes, and other fruits.

**RipeTrack — the AI component.** Apeel has developed an AI-powered digital platform that gives retailers, suppliers, and packers the ability to measure ripeness throughout the supply chain. Real-time visibility on produce condition.

**Concrete figures from Apeel (vendor-reported, via Sustainable Brands, 2025):**
- Since 2021: 166 million pieces of fruit saved from waste.
- 29,100 metric tons CO₂-equivalent emissions avoided (equivalent to planting 485,000 trees per Apeel).
- 6.96 billion litres of water conserved.

**Deployment model shift.** Apeel initially worked retailer-facing, then explicitly pivoted to grower / packer / shipper-facing — recognising that upstream supply-chain partners have more influence over freshness outcomes. A rare explicit pivot from downstream to upstream. Worth naming as a structural observation.

**Geographic scope.** Active in the US, EU (EFSA-approved), and global supply chains. Plant-based coating approach is regulatory-positive compared to synthetic fungicides and waxes.
