# Korea — Smart Farm Innovation Valley (Sangju, Gimje, Milyang, Goheung)

## Content

The **Smart Farm Innovation Valley** is the Republic of Korea's signature state-anchored agrifood AI / smart-farming cluster programme. It is operational across **four provincial sites** (Sangju in Gyeongsangbuk-do, Gimje in Jeollabuk-do, Milyang in Gyeongsangnam-do, Goheung in Jeollanam-do) under the **Ministry of Agriculture, Food and Rural Affairs (MAFRA)** and the **Rural Development Administration (RDA)**, with the broader **FAO Digital Villages Initiative** recognising Sangju as a Digital Village (the corpus's cleanest international anchor).

The programme's mandate addresses the **ageing-farmer succession crisis** that Korea, like Japan, faces: per the *Act on Fostering and Supporting Smart Farming* (enacted), the target is to convert **30% of agricultural output** to smart-farming systems by 2027.

**Cluster composition (per site):**

Each cluster has:
- Greenhouse complexes (operational agricultural facilities)
- Rental smart farms (entry-point for young farmers without infrastructure)
- Test centres (where research institutions and businesses develop / test / demonstrate new smart-farming equipment)
- **Startup Incubation Centres (SIC)** — the mentorship-and-skill-development core; the "education, training, capital, and resources" pipeline
- Distribution centres (export + domestic-market support)

**Trainee pipeline (Sangju + Gimje, then expanded):**

| Year | Trainees | Active |
|---|---|---|
| 2019 | 104 (Sangju + Gimje) | — |
| 2021 graduation | (2019 cohort completed) | 81 graduated |
| 2022 (all four sites) | 208 enrolled | 168 active |

**Site-by-site specialisation (FAO-anchored):**

| Site | Province | Crop specialisation | Programme focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sangju** | Gyeongsangbuk-do | Strawberries + tomatoes | One-stop mentoring for entry-level through established farmers; export support |
| **Gimje** | Jeollabuk-do | (life-science integration) | R&D, testing, validation/certification; functional food product development |
| **Milyang** | Gyeongsangnam-do | Bell peppers + premium strawberries | Crop-portfolio diversification; testing + export promotion |
| **Goheung** | Jeollanam-do | Oranges + melons + strawberries; tropical R&D | Climate adaptation; local nutrient-solution development; seedling production |

**Governance structure:** The services are jointly operated and managed by individual farmers, research institutions, and private-sector businesses — covering production, quality control, processing, sales/marketing. Upstream and downstream industries are integrated in the cluster design.

**Statutory and infrastructure frame (parallel / related):**

- *Act on Fostering and Supporting Smart Farming* (Korea) — sets the 30%-by-2027 target. (Encoded as the state-policy unit `units/korea-act-fostering-smart-farming.md`.)
- **Smart Farmland Distribution Centers** expansion: 14 locations in 2023 → 26 in 2024 → planned 100. (FFTC policy brief on MAFRA Major Policy Plans for 2024.)
- **2025 MAFRA designated four smart-farming fostering zones** at the si/county level — adds administrative machinery beyond the original four Innovation Valley sites.
- **KT Corp. AX Platform Company** initiative (Nov 2024 launched upgraded AI-powered digital farming advisory platform; July 2026 $5.4B / 18 trillion won overall announcement) — corporate-telecom-side parallel infrastructure.

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

**Sector position.** The cluster straddles multiple `sector-position` tags: `on-farm-production-protected` is primary (controlled-environment agriculture inside the cluster is the primary activity); `distribution-and-retail` (the Smart Farmland Distribution Centers); `consumption` (export support and market integration).

**AI technique class.** `predictive-ml` is the named cluster ML surface (insect pest/disease forecasting, remote temperature/humidity control, smart-farming solutions). `sensors-and-iot-ml` (smart-farm IoT sensors). `generative-ai-llms` is the emergent-class surface (not yet published at deployment scale but Korea's industrial direction).

**Purpose.** The cluster serves multiple purposes simultaneously: `food-security-sovereignty` (state-stewarded agricultural-output target), `market-access` (export support infrastructure), `supply-chain-efficiency` (distribution-centre integration), `climate-adaptation` (Goheung tropical-crop R&D), `worker-conditions` (rental smart farms + ageing-farmer succession target).

**Region tag.** `East-Asia (Korea; Republic of Korea, four provincial sites: Sangju-Gyeongsangbuk, Gimje-Jeollabuk, Milyang-Gyeongsangnam, Goheung-Jeollanam)` — explicit site listing in the region tag captures the federated nature of the cluster.

**Data governance.** `mixed` — state-anchored cluster with vendor-ICT-stack controlled at site level. The *specific per-vendor data flows* are not publicly documented, so the broader governance claim stops at "state-stewarded cluster; vendor operation intra-site" rather than claiming a specific data-rights posture.

**Data rights framework.** `state-stewarded` — the cluster's state-anchored franchise extends to the data-rights layer, though the per-vendor implementation is opaque.

## Why it matters for talks

The Smart Farm Innovation Valley is the corpus's **anchor for "state-anchored agrifood cluster programme" patterns** — a category distinct from both India's *federated DPI substrate* (AgriStack) and China's *state-vendor provincial-autonomy hybrid*. Korea's pattern is **state-anchored cluster-incubation**: a national programme funding four provincial sites, each specialising in different crops, with FAO international recognition as the validation layer.

Two presentations:

1. **The Korean state-anchored agrifood pattern.** Surface alongside Japan's industrial-automation vendor pattern (Layer A — Yamaha, Spread) to construct the **"state-anchored cluster vs equipment-vendor concentration"** binary that the meta-pattern unit (`units/japan-korea-agrifood-ai-pattern.md`) develops. This binary is one of the corpus's clearest additions from this cycle.
2. **The FAO Digital Villages Initiative as international validation.** Korea being a named Digital Villages country is the corpus's cleanest example of multilateral institution validation of an East-Asia agrifood AI cluster. The Sangju profile is the unit's principal citation anchor.

Three secondary presentations:

- **Young-farmer recruitment as a smart-farming policy frame.** Korea's 30%-by-2027 target is explicitly framed as a young-farmer recruitment mechanism. This is the inverse of Japan's ageing-farmer framing — the corpus's clearest "structural difference between two East-Asia clusters" observation.
- **Site specialisation as a national-programme design pattern.** Four sites, four crop specialisations, with each site driving a different export-market or R&D direction. This is materially different from India's "AgriStack everywhere as foundation for many service layers" pattern.
- **The Smart Farmland Distribution Center scaling.** 14 → 26 → planned 100 (FFTC 2024) is a *physical* infrastructure expansion alongside the *digital* smart-farm cluster. The corpus's only analog is Bayer / Climate FieldView's dealership infrastructure in NA — same kind of *physical meets digital* playing out differently.

## Critical context

- **The 30%-by-2027 target is statutory, not measured.** MAFRA announced the target; OECD 2025 cites it; no independent audit confirms current achievement against the target. V0 on the *progress* metric; V1 on the target statement itself.
- **The trainee pipeline (104 / 81 / 208 / 168) is MAFRA-reported.** FAO cites the figures from MAFRA materials; the percentage graduation outcome (81 / 104 = 78%) is meaningful but small-cohort.
- **Cluster-coordination specifics are opaque.** The Smart Farm Innovation Valley's *internal* coordination across vendors, research institutions, and farmers is not publicly documented. The "jointly operated and managed by individual farmers, research institutions and private-sector businesses" framing (FAO) is the structural claim; the *mechanism* is opaque.
- **The cluster's international validation is Sangju-focused.** Gimje, Milyang, and Goheung are named in FAO and FFTC materials, but Sangju is the only one with full Digital Villages Initiative profile treatment. The Sangju-as-Digital-Village recognition is the corpus's anchor — other three sites have *less* multilateral validation surface.
