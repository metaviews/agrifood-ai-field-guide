## Content

**AGRA (Alliance for a Green Revolution in Africa)** is the corpus's **most-substantive multilateral deployment-of-record anchor** for Sub-Saharan African smallholder agricultural transformation. Founded in **2006 by The Rockefeller Foundation + Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation**, AGRA has trained **15 million smallholder farmers**, restored **50,000+ square miles of farmland**, helped commercialize **562 new seed varieties**, supported **800+ projects**, advocated for **42 public policy reforms**, and mobilized **$1.3 billion in new investments** across **13 African countries**. Became independent in **2012 with uniquely African board + governance**. Current Mastercard Foundation partnership focused on Ghana + Tanzania + Kenya with youth inclusiveness framing.

This unit anchors the **multilateral deployment-of-record** pattern for Sub-Saharan African smallholder agricultural transformation. Distinct from the **multilateral research** pattern (CGIAR + AgriLLM + UAE AI71 partnership per `units/cgiar-agrillm-ai-global-south.md`) and from the **commercial-deployment cluster** pattern (per `units/sub-saharan-africa-ai-hub-concentration.md` with Aerobotics + Hello Tractor + DigiFarm + M-PESA backbone). AGRA is **deployment-led** — the substantive deployment-of-record scale — while CGIAR + AgriLLM is **research-led + chatbot-prototype-stage** with COP30 target.

The substantive distinction: **AGRA is the deployment-of-record anchor at the multilateral level**. The CGIAR × AgriLLM pair anchors the **research-and-prototype layer**; the Sub-Saharan Africa commercial cluster unit anchors the **commercial-deployment layer**; AGRA anchors the **deployment-of-record layer**. Three layers, three substantive anchors, three distinct institutional patterns — together they form the **complete multilateral + commercial + research structure** for African agritech AI.

The critical-voice framing: **AGRA is not a data-rights actor** — it is agricultural transformation. Its smallholder-centred framing intersects with digital-divide-equity framing (per `scans/2026-07-regional.md`) and food-sovereignty framing (per `units/indigenous-data-sovereignty.md`) but the substantive deployment-of-record is at the **agricultural transformation** level, not at the data-rights level.

## 1. AGRA's founding and governance history

### 1.1 Founding

Per AGRA institutional materials + Rockefeller Foundation retrospective:

- **Founded 2006** by **The Rockefeller Foundation** + **Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation**
- Shared belief: *"improving agriculture in Africa was the best way to reduce poverty and hunger on the continent"* (Rockefeller Foundation retrospective)
- Initial focus: "breadbasket region" — Ghana, Mali, Mozambique, Tanzania
- By 2010, expanded to **13 African countries** to pursue a system-wide approach to improve both the quantity and quality of crops in sub-Saharan Africa

### 1.2 Independence

Per Rockefeller Foundation retrospective:

- **Became independent organization in 2012** with **uniquely African board and governance structure**
- Per AGRA's own framing: *"a proudly African-led institution focused on scaling agricultural innovations that help smallholder farmers towards increased incomes, better livelihoods, and improved food security"*

The 2012 independence is structurally significant: **AGRA moved from being a Rockefeller + Gates joint initiative to being African-led institution**. The governance structure is the substantive substantive example of **African-led multilateral agricultural transformation** in the corpus.

### 1.3 AGRA's substantive self-framing

Per AGRA's own materials:

> *"We understand that African farmers need uniquely African solutions to the environmental and agricultural challenges they face, enabling them to sustainably boost production and gain access to rapidly growing agricultural markets."*

> *"AGRA's mission is to transform the lives of smallholder farmers from that of a solitary struggle for survival to a business that thrives."*

> *"We put smallholder farmers first on the agenda, recognizing that no country has moved from low income to middle income without agricultural transformation."*

The framing is structurally distinct from Western-led development models: **African-led + smallholder-first + African solutions to African challenges**.

### 1.4 AGRA's three-pillar strategic frame

Per AGRA's institutional materials, three pillars:

1. **Empower and build the resilience of smallholder farmers**
2. **Support the development of inclusive markets and finance to strengthen agricultural systems**
3. **Strengthen state capability to sustain agricultural transformation**

The three pillars are the **substantive deployment-of-record strategy**: smallholder resilience + inclusive markets + state capability. Distinct from CGIAR's research-led strategy, AGRA's strategy is **deployment-led**.

## 3. The Mastercard Foundation partnership (current)

### 3.1 Partnership scope

Per Mastercard Foundation 2025:

- **Countries**: Ghana (primary) + Tanzania + Kenya
- **Focus areas**: Digital + youth inclusiveness, capacity, and participation in Ghana's agrifood system transformation
- **Programmes**:
  - Expand access to **financial services** for smallholder farmers in Tanzania, Kenya, and Ghana by using **agribusinesses as a method of financing**
  - Support **agricultural market information systems** and **payment innovations** to serve farmers with a range of financial and non-financial services
  - Share **financial inclusion models and approaches** through research, documentation, and dissemination

### 3.2 Mastercard Foundation framing

Per Mastercard Foundation 2025:

> *"As an African-led institution that invests in building national capacities for inclusive transformation of food and agricultural systems, AGRA brings a strong pool of technical expertise, convening power (bringing public and private stakeholders together), proven models transferrable to local partners, and financial capability to manage implementation."*

The framing positions AGRA as: **African-led institution + technical expertise + convening power + proven models + financial capability**. The Mastercard Foundation partnership is structured as **dignified and fulfilling work for young people in Ghana's agrifood system transformation**.

### 3.3 What the Mastercard Foundation × AGRA partnership tells us

- **Mastercard Foundation** = substantive African-led private foundation funder
- **AGRA** = African-led institution
- **Financial services for smallholder farmers** in Tanzania, Kenya, Ghana
- **Payment innovations + agricultural market information systems**
- **Youth inclusiveness** focus

The substantive observation: **Mastercard Foundation × AGRA is a substantive African-foundation-led financial inclusion partnership** for African smallholder farmers. Distinct from Gates Foundation's global funding + Rockefeller Foundation's historical AGRA partnership.
