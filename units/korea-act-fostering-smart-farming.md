# Korea — *Act on Fostering and Supporting Smart Farming*

## Content

The *Act on Fostering and Supporting Smart Farming* (Republic of Korea) provides the **statutory frame** for one of the corpus's most distinctive national-policy deployments in agrifood AI. The Act's headline target is to convert **30% of agricultural output to smart-farming systems by 2027**.

The Act is operationalised by:

1. **The Smart Farm Innovation Valley cluster programme** (covered in `units/korea-smart-farm-innovation-valley-rda.md`) — four provincial sites (Sangju, Gimje, Milyang, Goheung) operational since 2018 Startup Incubation Centre launch.
2. **The 2025 zonal designations** — MAFRA designated four smart-farming fostering zones at the *si* / county administrative level, expanding beyond the four original Innovation Valley sites.
3. **The Smart Farmland Distribution Centers** expansion — 14 locations (2023) → 26 locations (2024) → planned 100 (FFTC 2024 brief).
4. **The Agricultural Robot Demonstration Support Project** (Korea Institute of Agricultural Technology Promotion + MAFRA) — supporting vendor-led demonstrations like the Daedong AI Lab Saemangeum Agro-Bio Complex demonstration H2 2025.
5. **MAFRA's broader agricultural R&D and extension layer** — under the RDA umbrella.

**Korea.net (state portal) framing of the Act** (the corpus's principal anchor):

> "Smart farming to take up 30% of agricultural output by 2027. The Act on Fostering and Supporting Smart Farming has been enacted to convert 30% of agricultural output to smart farming by 2027, [from less than current share]."

**OECD 2025 framing** — the Act is the policy anchor within Korea's agricultural policy stack; OECD's *Agricultural Policy Monitoring and Evaluation 2025* (Korea chapter) treats the Act as a primary instrument alongside other MAFRA tools.

**Comparison to Japan.** Japan's *Act on the Promotion of Smart Agricultural Technology Utilization to Improve Agricultural Productivity* (effective October 2024) is the analogous Japan-side instrument — but Japan's approach is more *technology-push* (driving adoption) and Korea's is more *succession-pull* (driving young-farmer integration). The meta-pattern unit (`units/japan-korea-agrifood-ai-pattern.md`) surfaces this contrast.

## Why it matters for talks

Two primary presentations:

1. **The Korean young-farmer-recruitment policy frame.** Surface in any talk about *why* a country adopts agrifood AI at a national-policy level. Korea's frame is recruitment-and-integration: the Act as instrument for bringing young farmers into farming via smart-farming infrastructure. This is structurally distinct from:
   - India's *AgriStack* / DAM (DPI substrate; farmers are users of the substrate)
   - China's state-vendor hybrid (state-vendor alignment at provincial level)
   - Japan's *Act on Promotion of Smart Agricultural Technology Utilization* (technology-push, not succession-pull)
   - USDA-NIFA's academic-institute pathway (research-to-deployment translation)
   - The Netherlands' Wageningen-anchor cooperative model (JoinData)
2. **The OECD-anchored policy-comparison layer.** OECD's *Agricultural Policy Monitoring and Evaluation* is the corpus's cleanest multilateral-institution validation of smart-farming-policy instruments across countries. Surface OECD 2025 as a comparison source.

Three secondary presentations:

- **The 30%-by-2027 target as a policy-rhetoric-vs-outcome tension.** MAFRA's target is statutory; the *current share* is < 30% (per the Korea.net framing). The progress metric is V0 vendor-reported. Surface the rhetorical-vs-operational gap honestly: state announces target, claim progress, but the audit-grade achievement is unverified.
- **The Act as cross-sector policy instrument.** Korea's Act operates across the data-and-production instrument layer. Compare to a more narrowly-targeted state-DPI (India's Digital Agriculture Mission) — Korea's scope is wider.
- **The international-multilateral validation as a structural advantage.** FAO Digital Villages Initiative provides the multilateral-institution validation that national instruments rarely get. This is unique among the corpus's East-Asia state-policy units. Surface in any talk about how to validate East-Asia agrifood AI claims.

## Critical context

- **The 30%-by-2027 *progress* metric is V0.** MAFRA-reported; no independent audit at retrieval date. The OECD 2025 cites the policy but does not independently verify the progress percentage.
- **The Act's zonal-designation mapping is administrative, not technical.** The four 2025 zonal designations are administrative designations, not technological deployments. The actual technology components are whatever each vendor brings into the zone.
- **The Act operates within Korea's broader smart-farming architecture.** The Act is the framework; Smart Farm Innovation Valley is one cluster programme; Smart Farmland Distribution Centers is another infrastructure layer; KT Corp.'s AX Platform Company initiative is a corporate-sector parallel. Surface all four layers in talks; the Act alone is too thin to characterise Korean smart-farming state direction.
