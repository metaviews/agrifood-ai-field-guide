## Content

At NRF 2026 (January 13 2026), Kroger announced an expanded partnership with Google Cloud, launching a *personal shopping assistant* powered by *Gemini Enterprise for Customer Experience*. The assistant is designed to support grocery planning, surface relevant offers and savings, and enable quick delivery scheduling.

Kroger frames its broader AI fleet under the *Sage* platform — used internally for store-level associate productivity and externally for consumer-facing surfaces. The Sage platform has been developed in partnership with IBM Consulting (visible from earlier Kroger-IBM AI work).

This unit sits in contrast to Walmart Sparky (proprietary Walmart AI + OpenAI surface) and Loblaw × ChatGPT (OpenAI-first). Kroger chose Google/Gemini as its foundation-model partner — making the *Big Three* US grocers now tied to *three different* foundation-model vendors (Walmart↔OpenAI, Kroger↔Google, Loblaw↔OpenAI but Canada-only via Loblaw).

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

Completes the *Big Three US grocer × foundation-model* matrix as of mid-2026. Important for any talk making the point that there is no single dominant US-grocery × LLM pairing — each major grocer is making a different bet.

## Why it matters for talks

- Documents the *Sage* platform — Kroger's umbrella for its AI capabilities, including the employee-side *Boost* assistant and the consumer-side Gemini-powered shopping assistant.
- Independent press (Grocery Dive, Jan 13 2026) confirms the deployment is in market, not just announced. Specific rollout metrics (number of stores, number of users) are not publicly disclosed as of mid-2026 — the announcement predates the operational metrics.
- Vendor-cited $400M ecommerce-profit-boost target is a *projection*, not a measured outcome; classify as announced, not audited.

## Critical context the unit does not sanitise

- Kroger's consumer pricing scrutiny context parallels Loblaw and Walmart. Deployment happens against the backdrop of US Congressional hearings on grocery prices (Kroger-Albertsons merger context).
- Vendor-cited savings / boost figures are projections, not measured outcomes; treat as aspirational for now.
- Gemini Enterprise for Customer Experience is a Google product — Kroger's AI behaviour is therefore mediated by Google's model governance, which Kroger does not control.
