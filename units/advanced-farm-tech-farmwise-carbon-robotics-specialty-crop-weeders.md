## Content

US specialty-crop weeding robotics is the **most-concentrated commercial-scale AI cell in US specialty-crop agriculture**. Four vendors anchor the cell: Advanced Farm Technologies (strawberry), FarmWise (Vulcan weeder), Carbon Robotics (LaserWeeder), and Harvest CROO (berry prototype). All four are US-headquartered; three are California-based with Florida (Harvest CROO) and Seattle (Carbon Robotics) as additional hubs. The Salinas Valley is the primary geographic concentration point.

This unit is a **consolidated cell anchor** — distinct from single-vendor units. The cell-level observation is the substantive finding: US specialty-crop weeding AI has reached commercial scale, while US specialty-crop harvest AI has not (per `units/sullivan-salinas-valley-specialty-crop-critical.md`'s "zero percent" head-lettuce finding).

### Advanced Farm Technologies (Davis CA) — strawberry robotics

Founded 2015. **First commercial-scale robotic strawberry deployment in the United States**, operating in California's Oxnard and Santa Maria during 2019. The platform uses **large autonomous vehicles with four custom-designed gantry arms (two per row of strawberries)** to pick strawberries at commercial scale.

**2025 academic validation**: Ochoa, E., Mo, C. *Design and Field Evaluation of an End Effector for Robotic Strawberry Harvesting*. MDPI *Actuators* 14(2):42, 2025 (cited 14 times per MDPI metadata). The paper documents the deployment scale and validates the academic literature on Advanced Farm Technologies as the US strawberry-robotics reference case.

**Structural significance**: Advanced Farm Technologies is the **commercial-scale strawberry deployment reference**. The 2019 commercial scale has continued as of 2026.

### FarmWise Labs (San Francisco CA) — Vulcan intra-row weeder

Per farmwise.io (verified July 2026):
- **Vulcan** is FarmWise's next-generation intra-row weeder and precision cultivator. Designed for durability and precision across day/night operation, varying weed pressure levels, and crop growth stages.
- **Performance**: Up to 3 acres/hour coverage.
- **Modular architecture** for service and maintenance simplicity; software updates over the air.
- **Vendor-reported value proposition**: *"Save $250+ per acre"*; fewer passes across the field; runs in all field conditions.
- **Customer reference**: Larry Santos, Senior Farm Manager, Taylor Farms — a major US fresh-produce company.
- **Distribution partnership**: RDO Equipment Co. (announced 2025) for exclusive distribution in specialty-crop precision weeding.

**Strategic significance**: FarmWise is the **specialty-crop weeding commercial-deployment anchor**. The Taylor Farms customer reference is substantive (Taylor Farms is one of the largest US fresh-produce companies); the RDO Equipment distribution partnership gives FarmWise a substantial equipment-dealer footprint.

### Carbon Robotics (Seattle WA) — LaserWeeder

Carbon Robotics builds the **LaserWeeder** — uses computer vision to identify weeds and high-powered lasers to kill them without chemicals. Per public reporting, the LaserWeeder has been deployed across multiple US farms at operational scale; specific acreage and customer references vary by source.

**Distinctive design choice**: The laser-based approach eliminates herbicide application entirely. This is a different input-reduction proposition than mechanical weeding (FarmWise Vulcan) or chemical reduction (John Deere See & Spray) — Carbon Robotics positions as **chemical-free weeding**.

**Note**: Carbon Robotics has not been pulled in depth for this scan; the public reporting on customer base and acreage deployment is fragmented. **G-168 (new, per US industry scan)** is the specific gap: substantiated customer base and deployment scale data for Carbon Robotics at the same granularity as Deere See & Spray.

### Harvest CROO (Plant City FL) — berry prototype

Founded 2013 — **the longest-running US specialty-crop robotics effort**. Focused on strawberries and berries. Harvest CROO's berry harvester has been in development and prototype deployment for over a decade.

**Structural significance**: Harvest CROO is the **longevity reference case** — a vendor that has worked on the same problem (berry harvest) for over a decade without yet reaching commercial scale comparable to Advanced Farm Technologies strawberry. This longevity itself is a structural finding: even with substantial sustained effort, US berry harvest AI has not yet matched the commercial deployment of US strawberry weeding AI.

**Differentiation from Advanced Farm Technologies**: Advanced Farm Technologies focuses on strawberry **picking** (commercial scale 2019); Harvest CROO focuses on berry **picking** (still in prototype per public reporting). Different crops and different deployment models.

### The consolidated cell finding

The four vendors together populate the **specialty-crop weeding + selective-picking AI cell**:
- **Weeding at commercial scale**: FarmWise Vulcan, Carbon Robotics LaserWeeder, Advanced Farm Technologies (some configurations). **Commercial scale achieved.**
- **Selective picking at commercial scale for strawberry**: Advanced Farm Technologies (commercial since 2019). **Commercial scale achieved for strawberry.**
- **Berry harvest at commercial scale**: Harvest CROO (still in prototype per public reporting). **Commercial scale not yet achieved.**

The cell-level observation: **weeding and selective strawberry picking have reached commercial scale; berry harvest remains in prototype.**

### Sullivan counterweight — the structural inversion

Per `units/sullivan-salinas-valley-specialty-crop-critical.md`:
- **"Zero percent" head-lettuce harvest automation** (Salinas Valley automation expert, April 2025).
- **"Cheaper and more reliable" farmworker labour** vs. existing automated harvesters for romaine lettuce.
- **"Holy grail" framing is industry-side**, indicating industry actors themselves name harvest automation as the hardest problem.

The Sullivan finding inverts the consolidated cell's commercial-scale success: **weeding is automated at commercial scale; harvest is not.** This is a substantive distinction worth surfacing in any talk about US specialty-crop AI.

### Salinas Valley as deployment concentration point

Per Sullivan: Salinas Valley (Monterey County) accounts for **at least 70% of US leafy greens production** and **just shy of $5 billion in gross specialty crop production value in 2024**. Romaine lettuce alone is approximately $1 billion. Strawberry production in adjacent Santa Maria and Oxnard regions is substantial. The geographic concentration of US specialty-crop AI deployment mirrors the geographic concentration of US specialty-crop production.

### Comparative technology approaches

| Vendor | Approach | Modality | Deployment Scale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Advanced Farm Technologies | Gantry arms with computer vision | Mechanical picking (strawberry) | Commercial 2019 |
| FarmWise Vulcan | Modular intra-row weeder | Mechanical cultivation | Commercial 2025 |
| Carbon Robotics LaserWeeder | Computer vision + lasers | Laser (chemical-free) | Operational scale; specific data thin (G-168) |
| Harvest CROO | Berry harvester prototype | Mechanical + AI | Prototype |

Three distinct modality approaches (mechanical picking, mechanical cultivation, laser) within one cell. The cell is not technology-convergent — vendors are taking structurally different technical paths to similar commercial objectives (input reduction, labour substitution, yield optimisation).

## Critical context

- **Vendor-reported figures dominate** across all four vendors. Acreage and customer-deployment scale varies by source; independent verification at Deere See & Spray-equivalent granularity not surfaced.
- **Carbon Robotics customer base and acreage data are fragmented** — G-168 specifically named this gap in the US industry scan.
- **Harvest CROO's decade-plus prototype phase** suggests the commercial-scale trajectory for berry harvest is harder than the strawberry picking deployment scale — worth tracking as a longevity-counter signal.
- **Salinas Valley labour-context dependency**: California's overtime regulation (2024), H-2A worker costs, and farmworker labour availability drive the deployment urgency. Sullivan documents the labour context in detail.
- **The Sullivan ethnography is the substantive critical voice** — vendor and investor framing should be cited alongside Sullivan for any talk that addresses this cell.
