## Content

**Auravant** is an Argentine digital-agriculture platform — a SaaS agronomy tool with origins in Argentina, deploying across **156 countries**, supporting **123,000+ users**, and monitoring **20 million+ hectares** (vendor figures, per Auravant's homepage customer-engagement counter as of 2025-2026).

The company was named in the AgFunder 2025 H1 LATAM agrifoodtech review as part of the "integrated SaaS ecosystems centralizing data across devices" cluster alongside Brazil's Sensix/FieldScan — meaning the SaaS-platform model is recognised across LAC as a distinct cluster pattern.

**Documented scope (from Auravant corporate site, 2025-2026):**
- 20+ million hectares monitored
- 123,000+ users
- 156 countries
- Operations through named customer testimonials: Nuseed (Argentina), Pratum (Uruguay), Tepro Consultores Agrícolas (Spain)
- Listed customer logos: Amauta, GDM, Union, Fertinagro, Tomás, Nidera semillas, ADAMA, UAA, Stine, Timac Agro, La Bragadense

**Specific testimonial value-adds (named by customers):**
- **Juliana Cavanagh, Carinata Assistant at Nuseed, Argentina** — Auravant used for Carinata crop traceability under RSB (Roundtable on Sustainable Biomaterials) certification. "We highlight the accompaniment and support of the team in every step of the process."
- **Agronomist Diego Vercellino, Director at Pratum, Uruguay** — Auravant enables digital environmental and economic sustainability monitoring for pasture-based farming systems. "Achieved an important advance in environmental and economic sustainability of the producer, by improving efficiency in the use of resources."
- **Fernando Artillo Guimerá, Technician, Tepro Consultores Agrícolas, Spain** — The most-used feature is satellite imagery for anomaly detection. "They allow us to detect anomalies in the crops and schedule more efficient field visits, knowing at any time which fields we have to go to visit and which ones we have already visited."

The three-customer / three-country / three-use-case spread (Argentina traceability under certification; Uruguay pasture environmental monitoring; Spain crop-anomaly satellite monitoring) demonstrates the platform's multi-purpose adaptability. This is structurally distinct from single-purpose platforms like Kilimo (irrigation optimization only) or Agrosmart (climate/soil monitoring only) — Auravant is *multi-purpose at the platform layer*, with named customer use-cases showing the platform supports traceability, environmental monitoring, and anomaly detection via the same SaaS interface.

Auravant's home-region LAC (Argentina + Uruguay + Brazil + others via customer logos like GDM) is the primary anchor; the broader reach into Europe (Spain/Nidera/Limagrain-related types), Asia (via the granular country spread), and elsewhere makes Auravant one of the corpus's most geographically-distributed agricultural-AI deployments at the SaaS layer.

## Critical context

- **156-country figure is striking and needs verification.** The figure on Auravant's homepage is consistent across multiple vintages of the site. Independent verification of country-count beyond what Auravant publishes is not surfaced in primary sources. G-100 candidate.
- **20+ million hectares / 123,000+ users figures are vendor-reported** from Auravant's homepage counter. Tier-1 vendor site but the figures are *vendor framing as scale claim*, distinct from independently-audited deployment scale. Apply the corpus's existing vendor-figure-hygiene discipline (G-015 pattern).
- **Customer-roster visibility is partial.** Auravant's site lists customer logos and three named customer testimonials. The full named customer roster across the 156 countries is not publicly enumerated. Distinguishes Auravant from NACo / Indigo Ag / Climate FieldView whose customer rosters are more transparent.
- **The platform is *venture-funded SaaS* with proprietary data.** Despite the data-privacy framing, the data governance is vendor-owned; "you control what is shared" is *opt-in* data-sharing from the platform layer, not data-ownership in the data-cooperative sense.
- **Auravant's reach into 156 countries is at the SaaS layer**, distinct from John Deere / Climate FieldView reach (which is hardware-anchored — the reach is limited by equipment distribution). Software-only deployment can scale geography faster.
- **The AgFunder "integrated SaaS ecosystems centralizing data across devices" cluster** observation is from a single source (AgFunder H1 2025 LATAM review); the cluster naming pattern should be tested against other 2025-2026 LATAM reviews before being treated as the corpus's settled cluster-pattern observation.
- **The vendor data-privacy framing on Auravant's site is forward-looking as a corporate stance.** The corpus should not over-extend this into "Auravant is IDSov-aligned" without further verification — vendor framing on a marketing site is not the same as institutional practice at the data-governance layer.
