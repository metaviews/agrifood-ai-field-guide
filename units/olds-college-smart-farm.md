## Content

Olds College Smart Farm is a 3,600-acre applied research environment in Alberta, Canada. The Smart Farm operates as a commercial-scale applied research venue — "essentially a giant lab that provides the agriculture sector a venue for commercial scale applied research" (Olds College framing). The Smart Farm is one of four AIVA Network validation hubs (alongside EMILI Innovation Farms Manitoba, Area X.O Ottawa, Innovation Farms Ontario).

**Three research areas:**
1. **Smart Agriculture** — evaluating, demonstrating, and validating agriculture technologies, tools, and practices for broadacre, dryland farming in Alberta soil and climate conditions.
2. **Digital Agriculture** — data collection, management, and utilisation.
3. **Autonomous Agriculture Equipment** — automation in agriculture (autonomous equipment, remote sensing).

**Active research areas include:**
- Drone imagery and data tools (with Agriculture Financial Services Corporation — AFSC).
- Weather stations and disease model evaluation.
- Variable Rate Technology (with TELUS Agriculture).
- Emissions monitoring (N₂O, CO₂, H₂O) using LI-COR chambers; relevant to 4R nutrient stewardship.
- Wind trap and soil sensor testing (Spornado, ChrysaLabs).
- Carbon content measurement for carbon credits.
- On-combine NIR (Near Infrared) analyser for real-time grain constituent analysis.
- In-bin drying sensors and algorithms.
- Optical spot-spray technology (deployed via partnerships).
- 5G, Wi-Fi, LoRaWAN, and cellular connectivity trials — addressing the FCC-named rural-connectivity constraint.

**Olds College also runs a Bachelor of Digital Agriculture degree**, signalling applied-research intent at the educational level, not just research-farm level.
