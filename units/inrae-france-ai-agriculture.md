## Content

INRAE, France's national research institute for agriculture, food, and environment, formed 2020 by the merger of INRA (founded 1946) and IRSTEA. The institute positions itself as "the world's number-one institute for research in the fields of agriculture, food, and the environment" — substantive claim worth tracking, but the institute's scale is real (one of the largest agricultural research organisations globally).

**AI-specific work.** INRAE publishes the *Can AI make agriculture more sustainable?* dossier (7-part), which surfaces concrete French and European case studies including:
- **Pl@ntNet** — citizen-science plant-identification platform using AI; major success story in INRAE's framing.
- AI for plant and animal health.
- Data as raw material of agricultural innovation.
- AI as a driver of innovation in renewed agricultural practices.
- La Ferme Digitale partnership (April 2025, 4-year agreement).
- AI governance for agriculture and the agroecological transition.
- Data and local initiatives in Africa.

**April 2025 INRAE / La Ferme Digitale 4-year partnership.** The partnership combines INRAE's scientific expertise with the innovative drive of start-ups. Per La Ferme Digitale co-founder Justine Lipuma: "Through this partnership, we can identify scientific bottlenecks faster, develop collaborative projects and joint events, accelerate the transfer of knowledge into concrete applications, and support the creation of science companies."

**INRAE's national role.** INRAE is the French national research backbone for AI-and-agriculture work. It's structurally similar to USDA-ARS in the US, to AAFC's research branch in Canada, to CSIRO in Australia — but with a distinct *open-data / open-source* positioning through the GAIA partnership and the *Can AI make agriculture more sustainable?* dossier.

## Why it matters for talks

- INRAE's positioning is structurally distinct from US/Canadian agricultural research framing — the *open-data* and *partnership-with-start-ups* approach is genuinely different.
- Pl@ntNet as a concrete INRAE-developed citizen-science AI deployment is a useful case for talks — distinct from the producer-advisory / consumer-facing / robotics deployment patterns.
- The GAIA partnership institutionalises INRAE–industry collaboration. Worth tracking as a model for how national research institutes can work with start-up clusters.
- INRAE's framing of "AI as an ally for the agroecological transition" is distinct from the productivity / yield / efficiency framing dominant in US/EU institutional discourse. Worth understanding.

## Critical context

- The "world's number-one institute" framing is INRAE's own self-claim. Not independent verification.
- INRAE research is publicly funded through the French Ministry of Higher Education, Research and Innovation and Ministry of Agriculture. Public funding context matters for understanding the openness positioning.
- The 7-part dossier is INRAE's structured public communication about AI in agriculture — substantive but also positioned for institutional influence.
- Pl@ntNet is the strongest single concrete INRAE AI deployment but isn't a content unit on its own here; could be a future unit if the field guide expands to include citizen-science AI applications.
