## Content

CLAAS, German agricultural equipment manufacturer (combine harvesters, forage harvesters, tractors, balers), operates the *CLAAS connect* digital platform — Farm connect, Field connect, and Fleet connect modules, plus Telematics data transfer.

**Distinctive interoperability feature.** From CLAAS's product page: with CLAAS connect, a farmer can "view machines from John Deere, Case IH, Steyr and New Holland in your CLAAS connect." This is operationalised mixed-fleet interoperability at scale.

**Deployment model.** CLAAS connect is free at the basic tier for CLAAS customers; subscription tiers for full functionality. Available on desktop and as smartphone app. The platform supports machine management, field task data capture, fleet management, dealer service integration.

**Data standard cooperation.** CLAAS participates in *DataConnect* (joint initiative with John Deere, CNH Industrial, AGCO) and uses ISOBUS standards — the EU equipment-maker approach to interoperability is more open than the US equipment-locked pattern.

**CLAAS's framing.** "Smart farming" — focused on climate change, skills shortages, rising costs, and legal documentation requirements. CLAAS positions connect as the foundation farmers can build on, with farmer case studies from Germany, France, UK (Scottish Borders), and Spain.

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

Anchors the *EU equipment-maker interoperability* pattern. Distinct from US equipment-vendor lock-in (Deere See & Spray) and from the independent platform pattern (xFarm, Agrosmart, Taranis). Useful as the third US/EU market structure alongside Deere and AGCO PTx.

## Why it matters for talks

- The DataConnect cooperation and CLAAS connect's mixed-fleet view (John Deere, Case IH, Steyr, New Holland in CLAAS connect) is a real operational answer to the lock-in question. Worth showing the contrast with Deere's locked-in approach.
- CLAAS's framing of the platform (climate, labour, costs, regulation) is closer to the *critical-civil-society* framing than the typical US vendor framing. The rhetoric-vs-structure alignment is different from xFarm's case.
- The EU equipment-maker approach (cooperation on data standards) is a structural feature of the European market. Worth naming in any talk that covers data interoperability.

## Critical context

- CLAAS connect's *basic* tier is free for CLAAS customers; full functionality is subscription. The "free" framing is vendor-aligned — the data flows to CLAAS regardless of subscription tier.
- The mixed-fleet view in CLAAS connect is real but limited — it shows machines, doesn't necessarily actuate across them. Distinct from full vendor-neutral interoperability.
- The DataConnect initiative involves John Deere, which makes the cooperation narrative more interesting — Deere participates in mixed-fleet data standards while selling vendor-locked actuation (See & Spray).
