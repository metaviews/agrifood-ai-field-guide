## Content

McDonald's × IBM *Automated Order Taker (AOT)* was the most publicised US QSR voice-AI drive-thru pilot of 2021–2024. It deployed to **more than 100 McDonald's restaurants** in the United States. McDonald's announced the end of the IBM partnership in a June 17 2024 memo to franchisees (obtained by CNBC), with the AOT technology scheduled for shut-off no later than July 26 2024.

The discontinuation was attributed by CNBC's sources to **issues interpreting different accents and dialects**, affecting order accuracy. McDonald's public statement left the door open to future voice-AI work (and a Google Cloud partnership was announced at McDonald's investor day in December 2023), but as of mid-2026 no new McDonald's voice-AI drive-thru deployment has been publicly reported.

The IBM partnership followed McDonald's 2021 sale of *McD Labs* (formerly *Apprente*) to IBM, and a separate 2021 sale of *Dynamic Yield* (predictive ordering / menu personalisation) to Mastercard. McDonald's has therefore divested both its in-house AI ordering assets within a few years.

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

The most documented *discontinued* high-profile generative-AI consumer surface in US agrifood consumption. Useful as the *cautionary case* in any talk comparing voice-AI deployments — Wendy's FreshAI and Taco Bell × Omilia both scaled; McDonald's × IBM did not.

## Why it matters for talks

- Documents the failure mode (accent / dialect accuracy) that mainstream vendor narratives underplay.
- Documents the *divestment pattern*: McDonald's bought Apprente (2019) and Dynamic Yield (2019), sold both by end of 2021, and ended the IBM AOT partnership in 2024. The corporate strategy is moving *away* from owning voice-AI.
- The McDonald's × Google Cloud partnership announced at McDonald's investor day in December 2023 has not produced a publicly reported voice-AI deployment as of mid-2026 — important gap to track.

## Critical context the unit does not sanitise

- The accent / dialect accuracy issue is a *digital-equity* problem: AI voice ordering systems that fail on non-standard accents create a two-tier customer experience. McDonald's × IBM pilots were concentrated in specific US regions; the failure was most pronounced on customer dialects under-represented in training data.
- McDonald's divestment of Apprente / Dynamic Yield means the company's voice-AI future is now dependent on third-party vendors (Google Cloud, IBM in adjacent products). Sovereignty over ordering data flows to those vendors.
