## Content

Sollum Technologies, Montréal-based, provides a "sun-as-a-service" AI-powered platform for greenhouse growers. The platform programs LED light fixtures that replicate the full spectrum of the sun's natural rays. AI dynamically adjusts lighting to optimise plant growth in real time.

**Concrete claim (per Montréal International / Investment Monitor):**
- Reduces electricity consumption by up to 40%
- Named in Montréal International's 130+ Quebec agtech businesses integrating AI
- Part of the Greater Montréal agtech cluster

**Why this matters in the Quebec cycle.** Sollum is one of two named greenhouse AI vendors in the Quebec cluster (alongside ChrysaLabs, which does soil sensors). Together with Haven Greens in Ontario, Sollum anchors the *protected-cultivation cell* of the matrix with concrete Canadian deployments. Haven Greens is a *fully integrated* deployment (lettuce from seed to package); Sollum is a *platform-as-a-service* deployment (light fixtures + cloud control).

**Deployment model.** Sollum's "sun-as-a-service" is a *subscription / as-a-service* model — growers pay for the light recipe and the AI control, not the hardware. This is structurally different from Haven Greens' vertical integration and from John Deere See & Spray's equipment-integration model. Worth naming because the deployment model affects who owns the data and how value flows.
