# Canadian Indigenous Data Sovereignty + AI in Agrifood

This unit consolidates the Indigenous Data Sovereignty (IDSov) + AI in Canadian agrifood substantive cell across four layers per the project's ecosystem-mapping pattern: framework, Canadian institutional, operational deployment, critical-analytical voice. **Cross-references `units/indigenous-data-sovereignty.md` (global framework), `units/cornell-atkinson-idsov-cluster.md` (US land-grant academic), and the four Canadian-context dedicated units.**

## Substantive finding

The Canadian agrifood AI ecosystem has substantive **operational deployments from Indigenous-led and Indigenous-partnered organisations** that are *ahead of* the dedicated federal funding instrument for Indigenous-led agrifood AI. The framework side is rich (CARE, IEEE 2890-2025, OCAP, FNDGS, NCIAF); the deployments operationalise the framework's principles but are funded through non-Indigenous-specific instruments (DFO, CanNor, BCSFA, BCSRIF). **This is the framework-deployment funding-instrument gap — G-313.**

The framework-deployment gap is the substantive cycle finding. See `scans/2026-07-canada-indigenous-data-sovereignty-agrifood.md` for full synthesis.

## Framework layer (international + Canadian Indigenous data governance)

**CARE Principles for Indigenous Data Governance** (Carroll, Garba, Figueroa-Rodríguez, et al. 2020, *Data Science Journal* 19:43, doi.org/10.5334/dsj-2020-043). Published 2019 (Gaborone); 1,808 citations. Four principles: Collective benefit, Authority to control, Responsibility, Ethics. Co-leads: Stephanie Russo Carroll (U Arizona) + Maui Hudson (U Waikato, Aotearoa NZ). Translations: Spanish, Vietnamese, Māori, German, Khmer. **Operationalising paper:** Carroll et al. 2021 *Operationalizing the CARE and FAIR Principles for Indigenous data futures* (*Scientific Data* 8, 108).

**IEEE 2890-2025 — Provenance of Indigenous Peoples' Data.** Board approved September 10, 2025; published November 14, 2025. PAR approval June 3, 2020 (5-year development). Working Group Chair: Stephanie Russo Carroll (U Arizona). Standard Committee: SSIT/SC — Social Implications of Technology Standards Committee. Working Group: ID-WG — Indigenous Data Working Group. **First global standard for provenance of Indigenous data; explicitly applies to AI/ML industries** (per Udall Center event description, February 2026).

**OCAP® (Ownership, Control, Access, Possession) — FNIGC, Ottawa.** Established 1998 as OCA; "P" affixed later. Registered trademark of FNIGC. **OCAP predates and informs the CARE Principles** (per GIDA 2024 communiqué).

**First Nations Data Governance Strategy (FNDGS)** — FNIGC + regional partners. Submitted to Indigenous Services Canada 2025 (AFN Resolution 30-2025). Community-driven, Nation-based national strategy. **Plans regional information governance centres (one per region).**

**Inuit Qaujimajatuqangit (IQ)** — Government of Nunavut traditional knowledge framework. National Inuit Strategy on Research (ITK) extends IQ into research governance. **PolArctic Sanikiluaq is the only operational agrifood-adjacent AI deployment that explicitly operationalises IQ.**

**FAIR + CARE complementarity** — international bridge. Articulated by Carroll et al. 2020, Carroll et al. 2021, Taitingfong et al. 2024, GIDA 2024 "CARE Directs Us Home" communiqué (DOI: 10.6084/m9.figshare.29294120.v1), Ruder & Wittman 2025, Gacenga et al. 2024. **The bridge is the substantive synthesis that allows non-Indigenous federal science agencies (NRCAN, AAFC, DFO) to engage IDSov through their existing data infrastructure practices.**

**UNESCO Indigenous Data Sovereignty in AI Report** — UNESCO has published new report and guidelines for Indigenous data sovereignty in artificial intelligence developments. Multilateral-institutional anchor for IDSov + AI at international scale.

## Canadian institutional layer

**FNIGC (First Nations Information Governance Centre, Ottawa).** Founded 1990s. Mandate: First Nations information governance; OCAP®; FNDGS; Regional Information Governance Centres (planned under FNDGS). **Primary Canadian Indigenous data governance institutional anchor for First Nations.**

**NCIAF (National Circle for Indigenous Agriculture and Food).** Launched 13 June 2024 with FCC support. **Only named Indigenous-led agrifood organisation with FCC backing and national scope.** MOU with Canadian Federation of Agriculture (CFA) signed 2025. Senior director: Gabrielle Fallu (Nortera). President and CEO: Kallie Wood.

**Mila Indigenous AI institutional infrastructure.** Three distinct vehicles:
- **Indigenous AI Advisory Circle** (Mila + CIFAR) — Six distinguished Indigenous leaders. Convening function.
- **Indigenous Pathfinders in AI** — Mila summer program. **2nd cohort (2025): 21 participants / 8 AI prototypes presented at Mila on July 15, 2025.** Capacity-building function.
- **Pathways to AI: An Introduction for Indigenous** — Mila + FNTC course. Education function.

**First Nations Technology Council (FNTC, BC-anchored).** Mila + FNTC Pathways to AI partnership.

**AAFC Indigenous Agriculture and Food Systems Initiative** — $8.5M, 2018-2023. **Closed; no successor programme named in AI for All (June 2026).** **G-332.**

**Indigenous Services Canada Pathway Initiative** — 2024-25 Call for Proposals: 87 small-scale projects / $5.6M food security funding. Federal vehicle; no AI-specific carve-out.

**BC Indigenous Food Sovereignty funding** — $8M (BC Ministry of Agriculture, 2024). Only provincial Indigenous food sovereignty funding vehicle surfaced.

**Stats Canada Indigenous agricultural operators:** 5,160 (1.9% of 270,720 total operators); +53.7% growth over the comparison period (per AAFC What We Heard Report). Demographic base for Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployment is real and growing.

## Critical-analytical-voice layer

**Sarah-Louise Ruder (University of Ottawa, SSHRC PDF).** PhD UBC (with Hannah Wittman). **Substantive Canadian agrifood data-governance analytical voice.** Key papers:
- Ruder, S.-L. & Wittman, H. (2025). *Agricultural data governance from the ground up: Exploring data justice with agri-food movements*. **Big Data & Society**. **Cited 22 times.** 40 workshops + 26 conferences, 2020-2023, with farmers + farming organizations + government policy + civil society + academic researchers across Western Canada, US, Latin America.
- Ruder, S.-L. (2025). *Theorizing agricultural data policy and governance*. **Journal of Peasant Studies**. Cited 14 times.
- Duncan, E. et al. (incl. Ruder). (2026). *The political economy of agricultural data governance*. **Journal of Rural Studies**. Cited 6 times.
- **BCACARN Ethical Data Governance Toolkit (2024)** — first documented Canadian agrifood ethical data governance toolkit.

**Hannah Wittman (UBC, Faculty of Land and Food Systems + IRES).** Professor. Substantive papers:
- Wittman, H. et al. (2022). *Land governance for agroecology*. **Elementa**.
- Wittman, H. et al. (2023). *Food sovereignty: An inclusive model for feeding the world and cooling the planet*. **One Earth**.

**Substantive observation:** Ruder-Wittman are the substantive Canadian agrifood data-governance analytical voice. **Distinct from Neethirajan (Dalhousie), Dara (U Guelph), Charlebois (Dalhousie)** — those are AI-deployment critical voices. Ruder-Wittman are the data-governance critical voices.

## Substantive contested claims surfaced

- **C-291:** "Indigenous-led AI agrifood deployment in Canada does not exist." Counter: PolArctic + Salmon Vision + CanNor-funded projects + Inuvik Tech Society.
- **C-292:** "CARE Principles are sufficient for Indigenous AI governance." Counter: GIDA's August 2025 "CARE Directs Us Home" communiqué explicitly cautions against treating CARE as a static compliance checklist.
- **C-293:** "Mila-CSA Indigenous Data Sovereignty Standard exists." Counter: Mila's substantive Indigenous AI work is institutional infrastructure (convening + education + capacity-building), not a CSA joint-committee standard.
- **C-294:** "Indigenous data sovereignty is a Canada-only framework." Counter: CARE Principles are international; IEEE 2890-2025 is international.

## Substantive gaps surfaced

- **G-313:** No federal or provincial programme specifically funds Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployment.
- **G-329:** No Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployment with CARE Principles *explicit* operational framework alignment (deployments do *practise* the principles but do not name them as implementation frameworks).
- **G-330:** No Canadian agrifood-data-governance framework explicitly engaging IDSov and Indigenous Food Sovereignty as integrated principles.
- **G-331:** No Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployment in Quebec (Mila's Indigenous AI infrastructure is Quebec-anchored but deployments are not documented in agrifood specifically).
- **G-332:** AAFC Indigenous Agriculture and Food Systems Initiative ($8.5M, 2018-2023) closed; no successor programme in AI for All (June 2026).
- **G-333:** Indigenous-led agrifood AI readiness assessment for First Nations, Inuit, or Métis agrifood operators not published.
- **G-334:** Mila's Indigenous Pathfinders in AI 8 AI prototypes (2025 cohort) — specific agrifood applications not publicly documented.
- **G-335:** PolArctic operational status (production mariculture vs. pilot-completed) unclear post-2025 publication.
- **G-336:** Salmon Vision's stated expansion to Koeye and KwaKwa rivers — operational status unclear.

## Why this matters for talks

For archetype 05 (critical-lens Indigenous sovereignty):

**Old claim:** "Indigenous data sovereignty frameworks exist (CARE, IEEE 2890, OCAP); operational deployments are sparse."

**New claim:** "Canadian Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployments *do* exist — PolArctic, Salmon Vision, CanNor-funded projects — but **they are funded through non-Indigenous-specific instruments** (DFO, CanNor, BCSFA, BCSRIF). The framework is rich; the dedicated funder does not exist. The deployments are doing what FNDGS / CARE call for, but the funding mechanism is incidental, not designed."

For archetype 02 (data-sovereignty Canadian):

**New claim:** "Salmon Vision and PolArctic are data-sovereignty-by-design deployments — community-stewarded, community-anchored, peer-reviewed. They sit at the same level of data-sovereignty-architecture as JoinData (Netherlands) or NCIA (Australia), but unlike JoinData/NCIA they don't have a federally-supported institutional vehicle behind them."

For archetype 06 (regional-cluster-comparison):

**New claim:** "Canada's Indigenous-led AI cluster is structurally distinct from the EU's cooperative-cluster model and the US's land-grant-extension model. It is *deployment-driven and infrastructure-anchored in Indigenous organisations* (FNIGC, NCIAF, PolArctic, Salmon Vision, Nunavut Economic Developers Association) rather than funder-anchored. This is a fourth operational model worth naming."

For archetype 04 (cooperative-alternative):

**New claim:** "Ruder-Wittman data-justice framing provides the substantive Canadian agrifood governance alternative voice — substantively different from EU JoinData cooperative framing or US NAPDC cooperative framing. CARE + OCAP + FNDGS + Indigenous Food Sovereignty are the framework cornerstones; the deployment-side mechanism (Indigenous-led, community-stewarded AI) is the operational expression."
