# Daedong AI Lab — Three-Core-AI-Domains strategy for Korean agriculture

## Content

**Daedong Group** (Republic of Korea) operates through its AI-robotics software subsidiary **Daedong AI Lab** (CEO Choi Joon-gi; founded 2024; headquartered at the Daedong Seoul Office in Seocho-gu, Seoul). The group's "Kioti" tractor brand is internationally marketed in the United States and other markets.

On **July 17, 2025**, Daedong AI Lab held a media briefing at the Daedong Seoul Office to present the company's development strategy for **three core agricultural AI domains**:

| Domain | What it does | Status (July 2026) |
|---|---|---|
| **Mobility AI** | Vision-based autonomous driving for agricultural machinery; replaces GPS-bound with vision-bound autonomy ("GPS-based autonomy has clear limitations when it comes to recognising field boundaries or responding to obstacles") | Trained on ~500,000 orchard/field images + ~3M driving-video samples; "the largest agricultural dataset in Korea" (Daedong self-statement). L4 tractor scheduled H1 2026. |
| **Operations AI** | LLM-based chatbot "AI Daedongi" expanding to Agentic AI capabilities | In development / public demo available; commercial deployment timeline not in primary sources |
| **Cultivation AI** | Crop-specific AI cultivation tech for predicting crop growth and recommending optimal strategies | In development |

The stated goal is to lead Korea's agricultural sector through a full-scale "AI transformation" — "AI-driven machinery and robots can make independent decisions to carry out fieldwork, while cultivation AI can predict crop growth and recommend optimal strategies."

**Key products and dates:**

- **RT-100 transport robot** — launched **February 2025** — "Korea's first government-certified autonomous transport robot for agricultural applications." Fully electric, zero-emission. (Korean government certification per the Korea Institute of Agricultural Technology Promotion and MAFRA.)
- **Flagship tractor with Level 4 autonomy** — scheduled for release in the **first half of 2026** ("the company's goal is to become a leading agricultural AI specialist by realising what it calls 'AI-powered future farming'").
- **RT-100 AI-Powered Voice Edition** — announced subsequent (Korean-language AI interface).

**Doosan Robotics + Daedong partnership (Oct 2025).** Daedong and Doosan Robotics partnered to design and manufacture an autonomous mobile platform that uses agricultural field data and integrates field operations — combining Doosan's robotic-arm engineering with Daedong's agricultural-machinery heritage.

**Saemangeum Agro-Bio Complex demonstration H2 2025.** Daedong AI Lab planned precision-farming demonstrations at Saemangeum (Seoul-Jeollabuk-do reclaimed land designated for high-tech agriculture) — satellite data collection, drone swarm flights, smart attachments. Government support via the "Agricultural Robot Demonstration Support Project" led by the Korea Institute of Agricultural Technology Promotion and MAFRA's precision-farming policy at Saemangeum.

## Why it matters for talks

Daedong AI Lab is the corpus's anchor for the **"Korean agricultural-machinery vendor pivoting to AI"** pattern — a distinct actor-type from both:

- **NA equipment vendors** (e.g. John Deere's recent Blue River / See & Spray acquisition; `units/dji-agriculture-global-export.md` is China-side aerial counterpart; Kubota Agri Concept 2.0 is Japan-side tractor counterpart).
- **Korean state-anchored cluster** (`units/korea-smart-farm-innovation-valley-rda.md` — the structural counterpoint to Daedong's vendor-led strategy).

Two presentations:

1. **The vendor-state parallel in Korea.** Korea's agrifood AI cluster has *both* a strong state-anchored cluster programme (Sangju et al.) *and* a strong vendor-led AI roadmap (Daedong). The pattern is materially different from Japan (where state plays infrastructure role, vendors play product role) and from India (where state-DPI is the substrate, vendors work above it). Daedong is the canonical "vendor-side AI roadmap" in Korea.

2. **Vision-based vs GPS-based autonomy.** Daedong's Mobility AI explicitly positions vision-based autonomy as *superior* to GPS-based autonomy for agricultural contexts — *"because conventional GPS-based autonomy has clear limitations when it comes to recognising field boundaries or responding to obstacles."* This is a substantive technical claim. Surface in any talk about agricultural autonomy / robotics.

Three secondary presentations:

- **Daedong AI Lab as a corporate-strategy pivot.** Daedong is an agricultural-machinery major (established tractor brand) extending into AI software — a corporate-strategy move that mirrors the NA "industrial conglomerate pivots to data-and-AI" pattern. Surface the corporate-strategy reading.
- **The Saemangeum Agro-Bio Complex as a Korean state-vendor integration context.** Daedong operating the precision-farming demonstration *at a state-reclaimed-land site* is a state-vendor integration pattern. Surface this observation.
- **Doosan Robotics + Daedong.** Cross-sector robotics partnership (October 2025) — Doosan is best-known for industrial / collaborative robot arms; Daedong for agriculture. The partnership is the corpus's first explicit agricultural-industrial-robotics partnership in Korea.
