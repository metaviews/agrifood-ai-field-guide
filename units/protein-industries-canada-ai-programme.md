## Content

**Protein Industries Canada (PIC)** is the plant-protein-focused Global Innovation Cluster. PIC's 2025–26 Corporate Plan (March 10 2025) confirms the AI programme:

- PIC ran an **Artificial Intelligence Program** from **September 2022**.
- PIC was the **delivery vehicle for the Government of Canada's Pan-Canadian Artificial Intelligence Strategy (PCAIS)** in the plant-protein sector.
- **$30M committed** to projects under the AI programme.
- **Programme ended March 2026.** No new calls in 2025–26.
- **7 projects total** by end of programme (5 active + 1 development + 1 completed in 2024–25).

**Three programme streams:**

1. **Projects stream** — individual SMEs incorporating AI as a core business function. Example focus areas: "Use AI to improve processing efficiency, reduce labour costs and improve food safety."
2. **Systems stream** — multi-partner sector competitiveness. Example: "Working within a data trust and pooling information related to the functionality, nutrition and chemical property of ingredients, the creation of a system whereby companies are creating and testing product formulations in a virtual world; Ingredients being analysed for nutritional composition, allergenicity, taste and texture; and Co-innovation of products based on ingredients available from different members of the system."
3. **Community stream** — societal-benefit AI applications. Example: "Design food products that help achieve certain health outcomes or to address health concerns; and Develop chain-of-custody systems to help reduce food waste and improve food security."

**Project selection** by independent Project Selection Committee, with an additional data-feasibility study to ensure companies are prepared to work with AI technology.

**Active and completed PIC AI projects (verified partial):**

- **Data Readiness Improvement Project (DRIP)** — completed. Foundational AI/ML training for agrifood professionals with applied research, business development, and strategic leadership backgrounds.
- **Pea Genomic Selection Platform** — Saskatchewan-led national consortium; PIC $1.4M / total $1.8M; 2-year project announced January 14 2026 at the Saskatchewan Crops Forum. Lead: GIFS Ag Tech Enterprise Inc. Partners: DL Seeds, University of Saskatchewan Crop Development Centre, AAFC. **Inputs × AI × seed × pulse breeding** at national scope.
- Five other active AI projects in 2024–25 (titles not enumerated in the Corporate Plan; PIC's projects page is the source for individual project titles).
- One additional completed project before 2024.

**PIC mandate context.** PIC's broader mission: "Canada is a global leader in plant protein." Mandate extends beyond AI to plant-protein ingredient manufacturing and food processing at large. PIC has a Technology Leadership Program, Capacity Building Program, and AI Program as the three pillars. The AI programme was the smallest of the three by total committed funding ($30M vs. PIC's overall ~$150M+ envelope), but the AI programme was the *first* federally-funded cluster AI programme (PCAIS delivery mandate).

**PIC's broader strategic context (2025–26 Corporate Plan):**
- Total Canadian agriculture productivity has improved 680% in the past 60 years — 2.5× the average productivity growth of all industry sectors in Canada.
- Canadian agriculture and food sector employs more than 2.3 million people, 1 in 9 Canadian jobs, $150 billion (~7%) of Canada's GDP.
- The plant-protein sector is positioned by PIC as a $25 billion opportunity by 2035 supporting 17,000 Canadian jobs.

**The Pea Genomic Selection Platform** is the substantive Prairie × seed × AI anchor. Distinct from the Chile-Canada seed unit and from Brazilian seed-AI units. Inputs × AI × seed × pulse breeding is a *public-private consortium* model: PIC delivers federal funding, GIFS Ag Tech Enterprise Inc. is the lead, DL Seeds is the breeding-industry partner, USask Crop Development Centre is the academic partner, AAFC is the federal research partner. The platform consolidates genetic + phenotypic + environmental + pedigree data into a single database for AI-assisted pea breeding.

## The PCAIS delivery mandate — substantive framing

PIC delivered the Government of Canada's Pan-Canadian Artificial Intelligence Strategy (PCAIS) in the plant-protein sector. This is a *delivery-mandate* relationship: PIC administers the federal AI funding for plant-protein projects, with PCAIS providing the budget. Similar relationships exist for Ocean Supercluster (Pacific + Atlantic + Arctic fisheries AI; OnDeck Fisheries AI is a $1.5M PCAIS-funded project — see parallel scout §11A.2) and other Global Innovation Clusters.

The PCAIS mandate means PIC's AI programme is *not solely a cluster programme* — it is a *cluster-delivered federal strategy*. This is substantively distinct from generic Global Innovation Cluster programming and is a structural feature of the Canadian federal AI architecture.

## The PIC AI programme's closure — substantive question

PIC's Corporate Plan (March 2025) confirms: "There will be no new calls for projects in 2025–26." Programme ends March 2026. **As of July 2026, the PIC AI Programme is closed.** The substantive question is *what comes next* for plant-protein AI:

1. **The AI for All national strategy (June 2026) does not name a PIC successor programme.** The $500M RAII scaling does not include a plant-protein-specific stream.
2. **The Pea Genomic Selection Platform (announced January 14 2026)** is the *last* PIC AI project before closure. It is a 2-year project that will run through January 2028 — beyond the PIC AI programme's closure date.
3. **The data-trust framing in the Systems stream** ("pooling information related to the functionality, nutrition and chemical property of ingredients") is a distinctive PIC AI programme feature. Whether this carries forward into a successor programme is open.
4. **The DRIP training programme** is a workforce AI-readiness example. Whether it carries forward is open.

**Worth tracking in future Canadian cycles** as a substantive post-closure observation.

3. **The data-trust framing in the Systems stream is distinct.** "Working within a data trust and pooling information related to the functionality, nutrition and chemical property of ingredients" is a sector-specific data-governance posture worth naming in talks.
4. **The Pea Genomic Selection Platform** is the substantive Prairie × seed × AI anchor and a public-private consortium model (PIC delivers federal funding, GIFS Ag Tech Enterprise is the lead, DL Seeds is the breeding-industry partner, USask CDC is academic, AAFC is federal research).

## Critical context

- **The PIC AI Programme is closed as of March 2026.** Any future unit or talk should reference this as historical, not active.
- **5 of the 7 PIC AI projects are not enumerated in the Corporate Plan.** PIC's projects page is the source for individual project titles; future cycles should pull from the projects page for completeness.
- **The Pea Genomic Selection Platform project** runs through January 2028, beyond the PIC AI programme's closure date. The project's funding is committed; the question is post-project sustainability.
- **The data-trust framing in the Systems stream** is distinct from generic data-governance discussions but the operational implementation (which companies, which data, which data-trust governance model) is not publicly detailed.
- **PIC's broader mandate extends beyond AI** to plant-protein ingredient manufacturing and food processing at large. PIC's Corporate Plan 2025–26 emphasizes the $25 billion opportunity by 2035, 17,000 jobs. The AI programme is one of three pillars.
