## Content

The **Atlantic Institute for Digital Agriculture (AIDA)** is the academic+convening substrate for digital agriculture in Atlantic Canada. Launched in 2024 within **Dalhousie University Faculty of Agriculture (Truro, NS)**, under the directorship of **Dr. Travis Esau**, Professor, Department of Engineering.

**Five research pillars:**
1. **Precision agriculture** — soil sensors, satellite imagery, AI for resource-efficient farming.
2. **Robotics, automation & artificial intelligence** — autonomous vehicles, drones, AI-powered robots.
3. **Data-driven management** — big data and IoT for farm operations optimization.
4. **Human-computer interface** — farmer education, policy advocacy, user-friendly tools.
5. **Knowledge mobilization & capacity building** — knowledge transfer and outreach.

**Director's framing.** Travis Esau: "Traditional agricultural farming is not sustainable and therefore requires new engineering advancements to remain competitive in both local and global markets. Increased discovery and innovation are necessary to bridge the knowledge gap, enabling the development of new technologies and traceability protocols that enhance farm efficiency and promote the effective use of environmental resources."

### The Canadian Soil Data Portal

**Dr. Brandon Heung** leads the **Canadian Soil Data Portal** (soildataportal.ca). The portal is positioned as nationally-scaling infrastructure — combines national-scale soil mapping with provincial-scale digital soil mapping data, accessible to farmers, agronomists, and researchers. Distinct from the existing CANSIS (Canada Soil Information System) which is data-focused; the new portal is *portal-and-analytics* focused. **G-328** (provincial coverage and accessibility tiers not publicly documented).

### The NB Potato Industry Research Chair — Al-Mallahi's research

**Dr. Ahmad Al-Mallahi** holds the **New Brunswick Potato Industry Research Chair**, an industry-research-chair position specifically funded for AI smart-spraying in potato production. The chair is funded by the NB Department of Agriculture, Aquaculture and Fisheries + industry partners. AI-based smart spraying (real-time weed detection + variable-rate application), contactless nutrient sensing, wireless potato storage quality mapping. **NB is the largest potato-producing province in Canada**; the chair is positioned to address provincial-scale AI deployment.

### Dr. Qamar Zaman's Variable Rate Sprayer patents

**Dr. Qamar Zaman** holds **US/Canadian patents for the Variable Rate Sprayer System** — one of the few Canadian AI-on-sprayer patents held by an academic researcher rather than a vendor. The patents cover vision-based weed detection + variable-rate herbicide application. The Zaman patents predate the CAAIN-funded Fulcrum Works Battery/Electric Cultivating Tractor machine vision autonomous weeding project (which is the Fulcrum vendor side of the same academic-on-research-station / vendor-on-commercial-farm distinction).
