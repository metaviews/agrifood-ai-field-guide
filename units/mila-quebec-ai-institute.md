## Content

Mila (Quebec Artificial Intelligence Institute) is a research institute in Montreal, founded in 2017 by Yoshua Bengio. Approximately 1,100 researchers, ~1,000 students. Anchored at Université de Montréal, with McGill University, HEC Montréal, and Polytechnique Montréal as partner institutions. Quebec provincial funding of $36M (announced 2026).

**DISA — Data-driven Insights for Sustainable Agriculture.** Mila's principal agrifood applied project. Uses satellite imagery and algorithms to:
- Assess existing agricultural practices
- Promote regenerative agriculture
- Sequester carbon
- Reduce soil erosion
- Reduce water pollution

**Other agrifood-adjacent Mila applied projects:**
- **Antenna** — interdisciplinary platform for uploading, classifying, and analyzing in-the-wild images of invertebrates. Advances research and conservation.
- **FLAIR** (First Languages AI Reality) — Indigenous languages, ASR. Adjacent to agrifood through language preservation in Indigenous communities.

**The structural significance.** Mila is the largest academic AI cluster in Canada and one of the largest in the world. Its agrifood work (DISA) is real, deployed, and positioned within the broader applied projects portfolio. The provincial $36M funding is the strongest direct Quebec government commitment to AI in any sector.

## Why it matters for talks

- Mila's DISA project is the strongest Canadian-anchored agrifood AI research-and-deployment work. Worth knowing for any talk about Canadian AI capacity in agrifood.
- The connection between Mila's academic AI strength and the federal *AI for All* strategy is structurally important — Mila is positioned as the Quebec-side implementation partner.
- The Indigenous languages work (FLAIR) is adjacent to the IDSov / Indigenous data sovereignty critical voice in the field guide. Worth surfacing because it shows Canadian AI institutions engaging directly with Indigenous data and language work.

## Critical context

- Mila's agrifood deployment scale (DISA) is not yet at the level of the field's commercial deployments. The project is real and substantive, but operational acreage / partner-farm count is not surfaced in our sources.
- The DISA project's regenerative agriculture framing is substantively different from the productivity / yield framing of most agritech AI. Worth surfacing.
- The Mila–IVADO–Quebec positioning in *AI for All* is recent (June 2026) and the implementation outcomes are forward-looking.
