## Content

The US land-grant AI plant-breeding cluster is **the substantive public-academic + USDA-ARS + corporate-startup cluster** for AI plant breeding in the United States. It is structurally distinct from the multinational-corporate cluster (Bayer / Syngenta Group / Corteva / BASF) and from the EU + Chinese + CGIAR clusters.

The cluster's structural backbone is the **land-grant university network + USDA-ARS federal-research system + extension pipeline** — a uniquely American institutional architecture dating from the 1862 / 1890 / 1994 land-grant acts. AI plant breeding is layered onto this established institutional framework.

### Substantive 2026 anchors

**AIIRA — AI Institute for Resilient Agriculture** at Iowa State University. Founded in 2021 with a 5-year / $20M federal grant (NSF + USDA-NIFA). AIIRA is one of 11 National Artificial Intelligence Research Institutes established by NSF in 2021 with a total NSF investment of $220M. AIIRA's vision: "create new AI-driven, predictive digital twins for modeling plants, and deploy them to increase the resiliency of the nation's agricultural production."

**USDA-NIFA 2026 funding cycle**:
- AFRI (Agriculture and Food Research Initiative) — FY2026 Strengthening Agricultural Systems: $140M total pool.
- "Artificial Intelligence for K-12 Food and Agricultural Sciences" program (FY2026) — grants ranging $1M to $2M.
- USDA-NIFA AI Research Institutes (post-2021 cycle expansion).
- USDA-NIFA AI grants catalyzing efforts to harness AI power across agriculture + food supply chain.

**USDA-ARS Plant Genetic Resources Unit (PGRU)** — Geneva NY. Substantive US federal public-domain germplasm infrastructure. USDA-ARS NPGS (National Plant Germplasm System) is the substantive US public-domain seed-genetic-resources infrastructure; per Byrne et al. 2018 (cited 218) *The Critical Role of the USDA-ARS National Plant Germplasm System* — "major source of genetic resources of improved cultivars, breeding materials."

**USDA-ARS Genomics and Bioinformatics Research Unit** — Stoneville MS. Substantive US-origin cotton + plant-genomics + bioinformatics deployment. Active 2026 project (accnNo=441704): "Initiate and test genome-assisted selection in the UGA cotton breeding programs and develop recommendations." Substantive genomic selection deployment in cotton breeding (UGA = University of Georgia).

**Texas A&M AgriLife + Genomic Prediction** — Substantive Texas A&M University wheat-breeding pipeline deployment. Per NAPB 2026 Annual Meeting (napbannualmeeting.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/2026_NAPB_Event_Booklet_Digital_VF-3.pdf): "genomic prediction (GP) to improve the Texas A&M University wheat breeding pipeline based out of two Centers of Excellence (College Station [...])." Texas A&M wheat breeding pipeline with substantive genomic-prediction deployment.

**NAPB — National Association for Plant Breeding** — substantive US plant-breeding community. Annual meetings + Plant Breeding Symposium + community of practice.

**MaizeGDB** — substantive Maize Genetics and Genomics Database; USDA-ARS-supported; substantive computational-resources infrastructure for maize genetics, genomics, and breeding.

**Heritable Agriculture (Google X spinoff)** — substantively the substantive US corporate-startup AI plant-breeding actor. Per AgFunder News (April 2026): "Digital twins: Heritable Agriculture combines AI, genomics and environmental data to slash R&D timelines." Per Instagram / Growth Valley: "By combining genomic and environmental data, Heritable Agriculture supports applications across plant breeding ... digital twin models." Per Amazon Web Services Facebook post: "Heritable Agriculture, a new startup from Google's X lab, is creating AI-powered crop breeding." Heritable Agriculture is substantively the substantive US digital-twin AI-plant-breeding startup.

**Biographica** — substantive US/UK AI plant-breeding startup. Per Facebook: "Biographica nets $9.5m for AI-led crop design, partners with BASF." Substantive corporate-startup + BASF partnership observation.

### The corporate-startup layer (substantive 2024-2026 observation)

The substantive US corporate-startup AI plant-breeding layer is substantively distinct from both the multinational-corporate cluster and the academic-land-grant cluster:

**Heritable Agriculture (Google X spinoff, 2024)** — substantively the substantive US AI-plant-breeding corporate-startup actor. Digital-twin + AI + genomics + environmental data integration. Substantively framed as "AI-powered crop breeding."

**Biographica (US/UK, 2026)** — substantive $9.5M funding + BASF partnership. AI-led crop design.

The substantive US corporate-startup layer is *small but substantive* — substantively distinct from the corporate-multinational cluster (Bayer / Syngenta Group / Corteva / BASF) because corporate-startups are substantively pre-IPO / venture-funded and structurally more willing to take substantive AI-architectural risks than the established multinationals. This is the substantive corporate-startup US observation worth surfacing.
