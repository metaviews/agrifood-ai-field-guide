## Content

**Nectar Technologies Inc.** (Montréal QC) is the first documented Canadian AI beekeeping deployment. The CAAIN-funded project — *Prescriptive Beekeeping: Leveraging Artificial Intelligence to Automate the Management of a Beekeeping Operation to Maximize Honeybee Colony Survival Rates and Business Profitability* — was completed September 30 2025 with $732,353 CAAIN contribution against $2,276,554 total project value.

**Project partners:**
- **Nectar Technologies Inc.** (Montréal QC) — lead.
- **Intermiel Inc.** (Mirabel, QC) — partner.
- **Busy Bee Farm Ltd.** (Hay Lakes, AB) — partner.

**Description.** "The objective of this project is to develop, deploy, and test a data-driven tool leveraging artificial intelligence that will automate the management of commercial beekeeping operations in a field setting. The tool will automate the prescription and prioritization of tasks and routes according to the outcome on annual honeybee colony survival rates and business profitability."

### The BeeTrack platform

Nectar's BeeTrack traceability platform is the underlying data infrastructure. The CAAIN-funded AI tool is the *prescriptive* layer on top of BeeTrack — automating task and route prescription.

**Deployment scale.** Nectar's customers operate **4,000+ hives** (some operations up to 9,000). The AI tool is deployed at commercial apiarists operating at this scale.

### Three impact steps (per Marc-André Roberge interview)

1. **Supporting the apiarists** whose pollinators are essential to the success of certain types of crop. Help them remain profitable so they can keep providing pollination services.
2. **Steady bee supply for fruit/vegetable producers.** For example, blueberry quantity and quality increase substantially when bees are involved in pollination.
3. **Best practices for hive health.** Recommending best practices that will ensure healthy hives.

### The migration logic

Apiarists move hives across regions. In Quebec: from blueberry farms to strawberry producers. In Alberta: between canola fields. **Commercial beekeeping is migratory** — bees constantly exposed to different climatic conditions. The AI tool prescribes tasks and routes based on this migration logic.

### Roberge's framing: "It's called 'agtech,' not 'tech-ag.'"

"It's called 'agtech' rather than 'tech-ag.' The 'ag' part comes first. You have to take into account the needs of the producers." — Marc-André Roberge.

This is the producer-led (not technology-led) framing for Canadian agtech — structurally distinct from US private-venture agtech which can be technology-led.

### Climate-change framing

"Climate change is threatening the existence of these amazing insects, and that would have a profound impact on our ability to feed a global population nearing eight billion people." — Marc-André Roberge.

The BeeTrack AI tool is positioned as climate-adaptation infrastructure for Canadian agriculture — pollinators are essential for fruit/vegetable production; their decline threatens the agricultural base.

## Critical context

- **The project is completed (Sept 30 2025).** The BeeTrack platform continues but the CAAIN-funded Phase is closed.
- **Nectar's deployment scale (4,000+ hives) is partner-customer scale, not Nectar's own operation.** Nectar is the AI vendor.
- **The "agtech not tech-ag" framing is Roberge's framing.** Whether this is the dominant Canadian agtech posture or one vendor's framing is not surfaced.
- **The honeybee population decline is real** (climate change, colony collapse disorder). The AI tool's impact on colony survival rates is the substantive metric; Nectar's framing is that data-driven decisions reduce mortality.
- **Honeybees are not endemic to Canada.** This is a substantive observation — Canadian commercial beekeeping depends on managed bee populations.
- **Nectar Technologies is Montréal-based, with named QC and AB partners.** Geographic spread is real but limited.
