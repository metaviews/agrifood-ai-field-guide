## Content

**Leanpath** is a Portland, Oregon-based food-waste management technology company founded in the early 2000s, with thousands of commercial-kitchen installations worldwide. The current product line uses AI (computer vision on the food being discarded) on top of an embedded scale to identify and categorise what is being thrown away — capturing waste data without requiring kitchen staff to manually log items.

**Product evolution timeline:**
- Original Leanpath system: scale-based, semi-manual (kitchen staff scanned items before disposal)
- **Bench Scale AI** (recent deployment per Leanpath product page): the AI categorises food automatically as it is placed on the scale
- **Floor Scale AI** (launched October 2025): fully *touchless* AI for high-volume enterprise kitchens — staff throw food as normal and the AI identifies and weighs it without staff intervention (Waste Today Magazine, Leanpath PRNewswire October 22, 2025)
- **Snap AI** (most recent launch, per PRNewswire): mobile food-waste tracker for *off-site events* — addressing the "blind spot" of catering and events outside the fixed kitchen

**Customer / deployment evidence:**
- **Elior North America** — major contract foodservice operator; deployed Leanpath across multiple NA sites per a Leanpath blog announcement framing the partnership around cutting food waste 50% and reducing Elior's carbon footprint 25% by 2030
- **US Foods** — one of the two largest NA foodservice distributors; lists Leanpath as a partner business solution on its healthcare-and-hospitality customer-facing page
- **Compass Group, Aramark, Sodexo** (NA contract feeders) — referenced in Leanpath's own marketing and trade press as part of customer footprint

**Vendor-reported impact metrics (Leanpath, recycled across multiple pages):**
- Average 50% reduction in food waste across Leanpath deployments
- Up to 6% reduction in food purchasing costs
- Deployment in thousands of commercial kitchens worldwide since 2004 (per Waste Today Magazine)

**Why this is the load-bearing NA food-waste AI unit.** Leanpath is the longest-tenured, US-headquartered, NA-deployed AI food-waste vendor, with the strongest North American contract-feeder customer evidence. The existing `canadian-food-waste-ai-landscape.md` unit names Leanpath and ReFED as US-context references but explicitly states that Canadian AI-specific deployment was not surfaced; this unit closes the US-side gap.

## Why it matters for talks

- *Leanpath is the rare vendor that names the impact number.* "50% reduction in food waste" is the same headline number Winnow cites — vendors in this space converge on a similar effect-size claim. For talks, the more interesting question is whether the figure holds across independent verification.
- *Touchless AI (Floor Scale AI) is the operational inflection.* Manual logging was the bottleneck in earlier food-waste tracking. Touchless AI removes the kitchen-staff labour cost that limited adoption. Worth naming as a maturity marker for the food-waste AI category.
- *Customer roster matters.* When US Foods lists Leanpath as a partner solution, that is a different distribution channel than direct end-customer sales. US Foods' ~250,000 restaurant and foodservice operator customer base is a meaningful amplification vector.
- *The Elior 25%-by-2030 goal is a public corporate commitment tied to AI measurement.* Worth flagging because it converts Leanpath from a vendor product story into a publicly-tracked corporate climate commitment.

## Critical context

- All headline figures (50% reduction, 6% cost savings) are vendor-reported and recycled across Leanpath's own marketing pages. Independent verification at scale is not surfaced in our sources (G-055).
- Leanpath was founded in the early 2000s (per Waste Today Magazine; product URLs reference "2004"). The AI-augmented product line (Bench Scale AI, Floor Scale AI) is recent — older installations were scale + manual-scanning, not AI. Worth being precise about which generations of the product are AI.
- The Snap AI launch targets *off-site events* — which is a new application surface (banquets, conferences, catering) and worth tracking separately as the category matures.
- Leanpath's data governance posture is not publicly disclosed — what is captured per site, retention period, aggregation across customers, model-training data reuse are unknowns.
- Customer expansion via US Foods is a marketplace pattern (vendor listed on distributor's solution catalog), not a direct integration. Buyers through US Foods get the product via a different procurement path than direct Leanpath sales.
