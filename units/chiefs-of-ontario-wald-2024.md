# Chiefs of Ontario commissioned paper — First Nations and AI

**Dr. Benjamin Wald (2024). *First Nations and Artificial Intelligence*. Chiefs of Ontario commissioned paper, funded by Ontario Ministry of Health. The most substantive Canadian First Nations AI policy paper identified in primary-source search.**

## What this paper is

The commissioned paper explicitly engages OCAP® and CARE Principles and **identifies OCAP application gaps for AI training data and generative AI outputs**. It also references the AFN submission to the INDU committee on Bill C-27 (October 2023) which criticised AIDA for lacking independent enforcement and for being "overly individualistic, excluding any protection from community harms."

## Why this paper matters

**Substantively important Canadian critical voice on First Nations + AI.** Distinct from:

- **FNIGC standalone statements** — none surfaced.
- **Ruder-Wittman agrifood data-governance papers** — data-justice framing but not First Nations-specific.
- **Tu 2025 Policy Options IRPP piece** — substantive AI+IDSov critique; complementary to Wald 2024.

## Substantive contribution

- **OCAP application gaps for AI training data** — operational question of how OCAP applies to the data used to train AI models.
- **Generative AI output data sovereignty** — operational question of who controls outputs of generative AI trained on Indigenous data.
- **AIDA critique** — Bill C-27's AIDA framework is critiqued as "overly individualistic, excluding any protection from community harms."
