# NRC Indigenous Languages Technology Project

**Operational AI/NLP tooling for Indigenous languages in Canada. The most deployed Indigenous-AI NLP programme in Canada. Operational scope does NOT include agrifood.**

## What this project is

The Indigenous Languages Technology Project is a federal programme led by **Dr. Patrick Littell** at the National Research Council Canada (NRC). **Operational AI/NLP support for Indigenous languages** in Canada:

- Inuktitut
- Plains Cree
- East Cree
- Michif
- Ojibwe
- Gitxsan
- Inuinnaqtun

Substantive applications: speech recognition, machine translation, language documentation, language revitalisation tools.

## Why this project matters

**The most deployed Indigenous-AI NLP programme in Canada.** Substantively important because:

1. It is **operational at scale** — multiple languages, ongoing deployment.
2. It is the **only documented federal programme that explicitly funds Indigenous-language AI infrastructure**.
3. **Agrifood advisory deployment is NOT in scope** — confirmed gap (G-340).

## Why this matters for talks

**For archetype 05 (critical-lens Indigenous sovereignty):** NRC Indigenous Languages Technology Project is the operational federal analogue to a missing Indigenous-led agrifood AI programme. The talk-ready claim: *"The NRC Indigenous Languages Technology Project is the most deployed Indigenous-AI NLP programme in Canada — operational across 7+ languages. There is no equivalent federal programme for Indigenous-led agrifood AI. This is the operational gap that the framework-deployment funding-instrument gap (G-313) names."*

**For archetype 06 (regional-cluster-comparison):** NRC Indigenous Languages Technology Project anchors the Canadian federal-language-AI cluster, distinct from the EU cooperative cluster model and the US land-grant-extension cluster model.

## Substantive gaps surfaced

- **G-340 (new):** No Indigenous-language agrifood LLM deployed. The NRC Indigenous Languages Technology Project provides operational Indigenous-language AI but does not name agrifood advisory as a deployment domain. Confirms G-011 (Indigenous-language agrifood LLM gap) from the regional scan.
- **G-341 (new):** The Maori-language Papa Reo / Te Hiku Media model (Aotearoa NZ) is the closest international analog to a substantive Indigenous-language AI deployment; no Canadian analog.
