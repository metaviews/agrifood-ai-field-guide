## Content

Canadian poultry AI in mid-2026 is anchored by **three CAAIN-funded projects** plus a **US vendor (Targan WingScan) deployed at Quebec hatcheries**. Together they cover **chick sexing, disease mapping, egg fertility/gender pre-incubation** as distinct poultry AI patterns. Spans New Brunswick, Ontario, and Québec.

| Project | Lead | Location | CAAIN $ | Total $ | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Testing and Validation of an AI-Powered Automated Chicken Sorting System Across Canada | Chick Pick Solutions | Moncton, NB (deploys in MB + ON) | $274,522 | $728,365 | Active |
| All-In One Poultry Disease Mapping to Prevent and Control Outbreaks | Farm Health Guardian | Guelph, ON | $227,606 | $690,667 | Active |
| Optimizing Hyper-Eye: Assessment of Fertility and Gender of Pre-Incubated Eggs | MatrixSpec Solutions Inc. | Baie-D'Urfé, QC (partners: Egg Farmers of Ontario + McKinley Hatchery) | $1,326,888 | $3,311,152 | Completed March 2024 |

**Total verified Canadian poultry AI deployment:** $4.73M+ in CAAIN-funded project value across 3 projects, $1.83M CAAIN contribution.

### Chick Pick Solutions (Moncton, NB) — AI chick sexing

Automates sorting day-old chicks by sex. **Two production-grade systems at a commercial layer hatchery in Manitoba + commercial broiler hatchery in Ontario.** Earlier prototypes supported by Mitacs, IRAP, NB Department of Agriculture. Test bench system at Fredericton NB facility for continuous improvement and quality assurance.

Ryan Whitney (Co-Founder): "By the end of the project, Chick Pick Solutions aims to have a validated and scalable solution ready for commercialization for the layer and broiler markets."

### Farm Health Guardian (Guelph, ON) — poultry disease mapping AI

Highly Pathogenic Avian Influenza (HPAI), Newcastle disease, Avian metapneumovirus (aMPV), and Infectious Laryngotracheitis (ILT) — all current poultry-sector concerns in Canada, particularly in BC and Ontario. Farm Health Guardian's AI-driven disease mapping and prediction tools, control zone monitoring, and communications technology address these.

**Mechanism.** FHG transport biosecurity breach alerts + GPS technology monitor poultry vehicles entering restricted zones in real time. Investigates tracking and mapping of migratory bird patterns for early-warning biosecurity. Partner: **Feather Board Command Centre Hamilton ON** — Ontario poultry four-commodity database for eggs, broilers, turkeys, hatching egg farms. Rob Hannam (Founder and CEO).

### MatrixSpec Hyper-Eye (Baie-D'Urfé, QC) — pre-incubation sexing AI

Hyper-Eye hyperspectral imaging AI determines **egg fertility + chick gender pre-incubation**, eliminating culling of male chicks. Scale: from 6,000 eggs/day to 10,000.

**Dr. Michael Ngadi (McGill James McGill Professor, 25 years; born in Nigeria, Government of Canada scholarship 1991).** Started hyperspectral work in 2008 for vegetable quality; pivoted to eggs in 2010; revived 2018 with Egg Farmers of Ontario seed grant; scaled 2022–2024 with CAAIN.

**The MatrixSpec 7-billion-chick culling claim.** *"Around the world the number climbs to roughly seven billion a year [male chicks culled annually]. The process, while necessary, is emotionally demanding of the workers involved, not to mention costly, wasteful, and bad for the environment. If hyperspectral imaging can be used to determine which eggs should be kept and which should be disposed of before they hatch, the impact will be extraordinary. The savings in time and money alone will be significant, as will the social and environmental improvements. We are very excited by the difference we will make when Hyper-Eye is eventually commercialized. This is a made-in-Canada solution to a global issue, and it wouldn't have been possible without CAAIN."*

This is **the most consequential animal-welfare AI claim in the corpus.**

### Targan WingScan (US vendor, deployed at Quebec hatcheries)

A US vendor (Targan) deployed WingScan AI feather-sex identification at Quebec hatcheries. WingScan processes **30M+ chicks/week worldwide** with 97–99% accuracy (early 2026 figure). Hatchery is offering chick sexing by artificial intelligence, with 98% accuracy. Distinct from CAAIN projects but worth knowing for the Canadian context.

Sources: Wattagnet, Canadian Poultry Magazine, Farmtario, TARGAN IPPE 2026.

## Critical context

- **The MatrixSpec 7-billion-chick figure is widely cited industry framing.** US ~300M, Canada "millions" are the more conservative estimates. The 7 billion figure is the substantive claim; the conservative estimate is the gap.
- **Farm Health Guardian's GPS vehicle monitoring requires nationwide truck GPS coverage.** The deployment scale is Ontario-led (Feather Board Command Centre partnership); nationwide coverage not surfaced.
- **Targan WingScan is a US vendor.** Canadian deployment at Quebec hatcheries is vendor-framed. Whether WingScan is the *primary* chick-sexing technology at Canadian hatcheries or one of several is not surfaced.
- **All three CAAIN projects span 2022–2026.** The CAAIN funding model enabled the scale-up from prototype to commercial-ready.
