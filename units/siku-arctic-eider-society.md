# SIKU / Arctic Eider Society

**Inuit-led digital infrastructure in Sanikiluaq, Nunavut. 125,000+ posts, 100+ active projects, Indigenous data stored only in Canada. NISR + FAIR + CARE operationalised explicitly. The substantive operational Canadian Indigenous-led AI infrastructure with agrifood-relevant data architecture.**

## What SIKU is

SIKU.org ("sea ice" in Inuktitut) is the Indigenous Knowledge App developed by the **Arctic Eider Society (AES), Sanikiluaq, Nunavut**. Per the 2024-2025 SIKU Annual Report:

- **125,000+ posts** in 2024-2025.
- **100+ active projects**.
- **Indigenous data stored only in Canada** (private data sovereignty by design; not "open data" by default).
- **User-owned** data.
- **Indigenous Environmental Terminology (IET)** integration.
- **Indigenous Place Names (IPNs)** integration.
- **ICE + WEATHER + Place Names + Indigenous Environmental Terminology** tooling.
- ML/AI tooling for ice + weather monitoring.

**The Arctic Eider Society is the same Inuit-led organisation based in Sanikiluaq that partnered with PolArctic / C-CORE on the Mariculture AI deployment.**

## Framework citation — substantively important

**SIKU is the operational case where NISR + FAIR + CARE are all operationalised explicitly.** Per Nowosad et al. 2025 (*Arctic Science* 11:1-10): *"SIKU is continually working to respect Indigenous data sovereignty by exemplifying the NISR framework (ITK 2018a), as well as the FAIR (findable, accessible, interoperable, reusable) and CARE principles."*

This is **the substantive Canadian operational case where the framework stack (NISR + FAIR + CARE) is implemented at scale**. The SIKU model is replicable for agrifood (in principle) but no agrifood analog has been built.

## Why SIKU matters for the field guide

**Substantive observation:** SIKU is the operational case for *Indigenous-led AI infrastructure with framework governance*. It satisfies all three frameworks simultaneously:

- **NISR (ITK 2018)** — Inuit access, ownership, and control over data and information.
- **FAIR (Wilkinson et al. 2016)** — Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable.
- **CARE (Carroll et al. 2020)** — Collective benefit, Authority to control, Responsibility, Ethics.

**The Canadian agrifood gap is real:** No agrifood analog of SIKU exists. NCIAF does not have an operational digital infrastructure; FNDGS does not have a deployment layer; FNIGC does not have an agrifood-specific AI infrastructure.

**The SIKU model is the template the Canadian agrifood Indigenous data sovereignty cell is missing.**
