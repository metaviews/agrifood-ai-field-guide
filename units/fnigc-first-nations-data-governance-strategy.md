# FNIGC First Nations Data Governance Strategy (FNDGS)

**Community-driven, Nation-based national strategy. Submitted by FNIGC and its regional partners to Indigenous Services Canada in 2025 (AFN Resolution 30-2025). **Plans regional information governance centres (one per region). **

## What FNDGS is

FNDGS is the most authoritative Canadian operational framework for Indigenous data governance at scale. It is **community-driven, Nation-based, and operational** — distinct from OCAP® (the principles) and IEEE 2890 (the industry specification).

**Key institutional detail:** FNIGC submitted the FNDGS in partnership with **regional First Nations organisations** — one information governance centre per region is the planned operational model.

## The institutional chain

- **FNIGC** (First Nations Information Governance Centre, Ottawa) — operational data-governance arm.
- **Assembly of First Nations (AFN)** — political arm; AFN Resolution 30-2025 directs ISC to "direct adequate resources to the First Nations Information Governance Centre (FNIGC) to work with its regional partners."
- **Indigenous Services Canada (ISC)** — federal funder; FNDGS submitted to ISC.
- **Regional First Nations organisations** — operational implementation partners.

## Why FNDGS matters for agrifood

**FNDGS is the substantive Canadian framework for First Nations data governance at scale — and the framework Canadian federal science agencies (NRCAN, AAFC, DFO) are most directly aligned with.** Not CARE, not IEEE 2890 — FNDGS.

For Canadian agrifood AI:
- AAFC and ISC are the federal agrifood data agencies.
- FNDGS is the First Nations data governance framework they are most directly aligned with.
- **For AAFC to operationalise an Indigenous-led agrifood AI programme, FNDGS is the framework reference.**

This is structurally important because:
- FNDGS is community-driven and Nation-based (not vendor-captured).
- FNDGS regional implementation (one centre per region) provides operational pathway for Indigenous-led agrifood AI deployment.
- FNDGS aligns with OCAP® principles (Canadian First Nations) and is consistent with CARE Principles (international Indigenous data governance).

## Funding and operational model

- Federal funding through ISC.
- FNIGC operational coordination.
- Regional implementation through regional First Nations organisations.
- **No AAFC-specific FNDGS implementation for agrifood** — this is the framework-deployment gap.
