## Content

Marfrig Global Foods — the world's largest hamburger producer and one of the largest beef, pork, and poultry processors globally with >120,000 employees across North America, South America, and BRF — launched a pilot project in January 2025 with Brazilian green-fintech **Agrorobótica** to measure and monitor soil carbon on cattle-supplier farms. The pilot uses Agrorobótica's **AGLIBS** AI platform, which applies **LIBS laser technology** (Laser-Induced Breakdown Spectroscopy) — the same analytical technique used by NASA Mars rover missions — to evaluate 22 distinct parameters of soil fertility and carbon without generating chemical waste.

The project context: Marfrig's **Verde+ Program** (launched July 2020) commits to "100% traceable, deforestation-free, inclusive, and more productive" low-carbon livestock farming by 2025 (with the carbon part effectively a continuing program, given the 2025 milestone has passed without public 100% achievement). The pilot is part of Verde+ — specifically, the carbon-credit monetization leg.

**Documented scope (per Marfrig corporate press release, January 7 2025):**
- Pilot on cattle-supplier farms in **Mato Grosso**, Brazil
- Agrorobótica collecting soil samples; LIBS analysis at varying soil depths
- Carbon storage quantification at farm level; conversion pathway to certified carbon credits
- Producer-side benefit: additional revenue via carbon credit sale; soil-quality improvement; biodiversity preservation

**Why pilot, not deployed:** the Jan 2025 announcement explicitly frames this as a *pilot project*. No follow-up press release from Marfrig has surfaced reporting scaled deployment across additional Brazilian states or broader Marfrig supplier network as of mid-2026. The deployment grade for this unit is therefore **S2 / V1 / L2 / T2** (piloting, not deployed at scale), tracking the corpus's existing POV-discipline pattern.

**Significance for the corpus:**
- Marfrig × Agrorobótica is the corpus's first Brazilian beef-processing AI unit with named soil-carbon deployment
- Agrorobótica's LIBS technology is distinctive — most soil-carbon AI in the corpus (e.g. Indigo Ag's agricultural carbon programs in NA; Locus Ag's fermentation-biological carbon) does not use LIBS laser spectroscopy. Agrorobótica is citing the NASA-Mars technique as direct provenance for soil-carbon analysis at scale.
- The pilot positions Brazilian beef processors in the **carbon-credit-monetization AI** cluster — alongside the corpus's `bayer-climate-fieldview.md` (carbon-programs-as-data-infrastructure), `indigo-ag.md` (carbon-credit verification AI), and JBS / Minerva carve-out programs.
- Marfrig does **not** have the public cattle-supply-chain AI controversy that JBS does (Brazilian beef supply chain deforestation traceability has been a major controversy), but Marfrig's own commitments are framed in the same low-carbon-by-2025 terminology. Pilot-scale land measurement with quantified carbon data is *consistent with* a sustainable-supply-chain strategy but does not verify its operational scope.

**Cross-verification status:** the only primary source for this pilot is the Marfrig corporate press release (Jan 7 2025). Independent trade-press coverage not surfaced (no Reuters / BeefCentral / Chain Reaction Research / Greenpeace Unearthed reporting on Marfrig-Agrorobótica specifically). The pilot is real; its scope beyond Mato Grosso is unverified — to be tracked as G-092.

## Critical context

- Pilot only — S2 / V1 / L2 / T2. The Jan 2025 announcement is the only verifiable primary source for the deployment; subsequent scope-confirmation press release not surfaced.
- Marfrig's commitment language ("low-carbon livestock farming that is 100% traceable, deforestation-free, inclusive, and more productive by 2025") is a *promise-of-intent* — the AI in this pilot is one delivery vehicle for parts of this commitment, but the broader 100% achievement claim is separate and not verified.
- Brazilian beef supply chain AI is heavily scrutinised for *deforestation traceability* (not just carbon), and Verdе+'s traceability leg (separate from the carbon leg) is the more widely-cited dimension in critical voices (Chain Reaction Research, Reuters May 2025, Greenpeace Unearthed). Marfrig's separate cattle purchases in Mato Grosso reached 98.2% conformity per Reuters May 2025 — but that's a separate traceability claim, not the AI-based monitoring this unit covers.
- The carbon-credit monetisation leg raises unresolved questions: how is the carbon credit issuance verified? What assurance do third-party carbon registries have? The Marfrig press release does not name a carbon-credit registry partner; the carbon-credit issuance pathway needs verification (G-092 candidate).
- Cattle-supplier farms are *external* to Marfrig's direct operational control. Whether the Marfrig pilot includes farm-level decision-support feedback to farmers (rather than just measurement-for-credit) is not surfaced.
- Worker-displacement implications of soil-sensor-based carbon monitoring at scale are not surfaced. Marfrig-Agrorobótica likely reduces labour requirements for traditional soil-carbon-measurement crews but does not eliminate the need for technical data-handling roles.
