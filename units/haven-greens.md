## Content

Haven Greens, located in King City Ontario, is positioned as Canada's first fully automated AI-powered greenhouse. Founded by Jay Willmot (Dalhousie University commerce / finance undergrad; York University environmental studies master's + law degree). Operations are run by Director of Cultivation Dominick DiMucci and Chief Agricultural Officer Eric Highfield.

**Concrete figures (Farmtario 2026; Globe and Mail May 2026):**
- 5 acres operational, 10-acre expansion underway
- Targets ~2 million kg/year lettuce and leafy greens
- 100 kg/m²/year — outpaced expected model output
- 17 of 20 production lines in operation
- 40+ employees
- Carbon-neutral target 2027
- Closed-loop water filtration: 90% reduction in water consumption
- 85,000-gallon water supply fed by rainwater, snowmelt, pond, well, stormwater

**Deployment model.** Mobile Gully System (MGS) — Finnish technology. Gutters hold ~240 red/green lettuce seeds or ~1,000 arugula/mustard seeds for spring mix. After 2 days in germination chamber, gutters move into precisely climate- and lighting-controlled growing environment. Red/green leafy greens mature over 24-25 days; flavour varieties over 13-14 days. RFID scanning tracks each trough throughout. Pack-hall conveyors measure gross and net weight in real-time; camera-sensing technology in development to gather germination rates and other data points. 20 seconds per gutter harvest.

**The food-sovereignty context.** York University report cited in coverage: 90% of Canada's leafy greens imported from the US; California exports 87% of its lettuce crop to Canada. CFIA temporary import licence conditions (Sept 25 - Dec 17 2025) for US romaine due to Canadian E. coli outbreaks linked to California's Salinas Valley. Haven Greens is positioned explicitly as a food-security / sovereignty / import-replacement play — Willmot's framing in Farmtario: "There's a huge appetite [for local products] and I think it requires not just us, but a number of people to step their game up to do what we need in Canada."

**Substantive external endorsement.** Dr. Rozita Dara (University of Guelph, AI4Food director) quoted in the Farmtario piece: "This is the future." Dara also warns about over-dependence and cyber risk — adjacent to Neethirajan's cybersecurity-as-critical-infrastructure framing.

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

Anchors the *on-farm-production-protected × sensors-and-IoT + robotics-ground* cell with a Canadian-specific deployed example. Distinguishes from:
- **Naïo Technologies** (French; vineyards, vegetable crops; open-field, ground robotics).
- **JD Farm** (China; vertical integration with retail).
- **Alibaba ET Agricultural Brain** (China; cloud-mediated).
- **Greenhouse Canada industry-level claim** ("10-20% yield increases, in some trials up to 30%") is the broader trend Haven Greens sits within.

Also notable: Haven Greens is a *family-farm-scale industrial* deployment. The family owns Kinghaven Farms (horse racing background, then diversified). The story of *why* this deployment happened is partly a tariff-war story — US-Canada trade friction accelerated local demand that domestic supply couldn't meet.

## Why it matters for talks

- 90% of leafy greens imported from US is the structural context — Canadian greenhouse AI is positioned in part as a food-sovereignty / import-replacement play. Worth pairing with the FCC's *AI for All* showcase on Croptimistic.
- The Mobile Gully System (Finnish technology deployed in Canada) is an example of cross-border technology transfer in agritech AI. Distinct from the equipment-maker lock-in pattern.
- Dara's quoted endorsement + her cyber risk warning captures both the technology-as-future framing and the structural risks in the same quote. Worth surfacing.
- The family-farm / tariff-war origin story is a distinctive deployment-driver narrative worth using in talks about who actually adopts AI and why.

## Critical context

- "Outpaced expected model" framing for 100 kg/m²/year is vendor / corporate-reported. No independent verification surfaced.
- The 90% imported / 87% California-exported figures are from a York University report cited in media coverage; primary source worth tracking for next refresh.
- The Haven Greens deployment is *closed-loop* in multiple senses (water, nutrients, carbon) — not just automation. Worth distinguishing from the partial-automation greenhouse pattern in the wider industry.
- The Dara endorsement is honest + cautionary. Worth surfacing both halves in any talk.
- The food-sovereignty framing here is *Canadian food sovereignty* (US import replacement), distinct from Indigenous data sovereignty (CARE / IEEE 2890) and from the global food sovereignty frame (IPES-Food). Different scopes, different framings — worth distinguishing.
