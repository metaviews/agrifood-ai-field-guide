## Content

The *Agriculture Innovation, Validation, and Adoption Network* (AIVA) was launched May 12, 2026, founded by Farm Credit Canada (FCC), EMILI (Manitoba), and the Wabash Heartland Innovation Network (WHIN, US-Indiana). AIVA is a Canadian farmer-centric AI validation network designed to test and validate agritech in real-world conditions before market scale.

**Concrete figures (May 2026):**
- 9 multi-site collaboration projects in 2026 growing season.
- 23 Farmer Alliance Members representing 235,000+ acres in Saskatchewan and Manitoba.
- 4 validation hubs across Canada: EMILI Innovation Farms (Manitoba), Olds College (Alberta/Saskatchewan), Area X.O (Ottawa), Innovation Farms Ontario (Bothwell).
- 33 total projects on EMILI Innovation Farms in 2026.

**AI-relevant projects being validated in 2026:**
- **Geco Strategic Weed Management AI** — AI software predicting weed zones including herbicide-resistant patches.
- **Corteva Agriscience spray drone trials** — fungicide application via drone.
- **Picketa Systems LENS™** — in-field tissue scanning for plant nutrients.
- **Miraterra** — Gabe AI chatbot for field management and soil sampling.
- **AgExpert Field Sustainability Regional Benchmarking software** — soil and crop health insights (FCC's own product being validated).
- **FarmerTitan** — named but not detailed in our sources.

**Distinctive features.**
1. **Standardised testing framework** — AIVA applies consistent evaluation across hubs, not bespoke per-project testing.
2. **Farmer Alliance members** — 23 named farmers representing 235,000+ acres who pilot market-ready technology.
3. **Health Canada engagement** — Area X.O and Corteva met with Health Canada in June 2026 to discuss spray drone regulations. AIVA bridges innovators and federal regulators, not just validation.
4. **AIVA Technology Partners** — recognition given to technologies that demonstrate strong tangible results, providing a Canadian validation credential.

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

Anchors a *new deployment model* not previously populated in the field guide: **farmer-centric validation infrastructure**. Distinct from vendor-led deployment (Deere / Bayer / Loblaw), independent platform (xFarm / Agrosmart), and state-led (China). AIVA is a hybrid — institutional funding (FCC) + farmer-member validation + academic / industry testbed infrastructure.

This is also the field guide's first unit with **multiple actor types at the founding level** — `vendor` (FCC is state-adjacent commercial), `industry-association` (EMILI is industry-led), and the network itself is a *new* structural form. The unit captures this honestly rather than forcing a single actor-type classification.

## Why it matters for talks

- Concrete Canadian contribution to global agritech validation practice. The standardisation framework is what makes AIVA distinct from one-off FCC investments or EMILI projects.
- The named AI projects being validated in 2026 give the talk substrate concrete examples of *what AI is being validated in Canada right now* — better than the deployment-elsewhere framing for Canadian talks.
- The Health Canada engagement surfaces the regulatory conversation as part of the validation process. Most agritech AI deployment discussion treats regulation as downstream; AIVA treats it as part of the validation cycle.
- AIVA is well-positioned to test the contested-claim / verification cycle for the field guide. AI vendor figures circulate; AIVA's standardised testing can produce independent verification.

## Critical context

- AIVA is new (launched May 2026). 2026 is the first full growing season of operation. The 9 multi-site projects are the *first cohort* — outcomes won't be available until late 2026 / early 2027.
- The 235,000 acres figure is concentrated in Saskatchewan and Manitoba (initial regions); expansion to other provinces is announced but not yet executed.
- The validation infrastructure addresses G-020 (independent verification of vendor-reported figures) structurally — AIVA's standardised testing is the kind of independent verification the field guide flagged as missing. Whether it produces verification at scale is a question to track.
- AIVA's founding coalition includes a US-based partner (WHIN), which has implications for the *Canadian sovereignty* frame. Worth naming without flattening — the validation model itself travels well, the Canadian context anchors the deployment.
