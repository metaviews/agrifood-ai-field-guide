## Content

JoinData, founded 2017 in the Netherlands, is the **first agricultural data cooperative** in the world. It is independent, non-profit, and dedicated to Dutch farmers. Mission: "any farmer can pool, control, connect and share data—in a safe, secure and fair way—with agribusiness and innovation partners, and to make sure the data and benefits flow back to the farmer."

### Concrete deployment figures (Development Gateway / USAID case study, Feb 2023)

- **Over 16,000 farmer members**
- **260 parties** (fodder suppliers, accountants, etc.) sharing data via JoinData
- **70 parties** using data for the farmer
- **€50/year farmer membership fee**
- Companies pay fees to JoinData for "data transport" — JoinData reinvests in platform

### Founding coalition

JoinData was founded by a coalition of:
- **FrieslandCampina** (largest Dutch dairy cooperative, multi-billion-euro operation)
- **Agrifirm** (Dutch agricultural cooperative)
- **LTO Nederland** (Dutch farmers' union)
- **EDI-Circle** (partnership of accountants)
- **Rabobank** (Dutch cooperative bank)

The Dutch cooperative cultural tradition is structurally important. FrieslandCampina itself emerged from cooperative dairy traditions going back to the 19th century. JoinData's design choices (non-profit, farmer-controlled, fee-funded) draw on this tradition.

### What JoinData solves

The Development Gateway case study identifies the problems JoinData addresses:

1. **At least 25 digital platforms for arable farmers in the Netherlands alone** (per Wageningen UR 2020 study). Many platforms serve single applications or supply chains, usually from the perspective of food processor or input supplier, not farmer.
2. **Vendor lock-in.** "Data cannot be easily transferred later on to another service provider, making a farmer reliant on that particular platform."
3. **No consolidated view of data authorisations.** Farmers don't know which companies and organisations have received their authorisation to collect data. Authorisations on paper, digital, out-of-date.
4. **Sensitive data exposure risk.** Farmers don't want to share data that can be used against them in contract negotiations.
5. **Privacy and decision-making concern.** "Digitization makes it possible to control parts of the production and move the decision making from farm to algorithms of powerful players."

### What JoinData is *not*

Worth distinguishing:
- **Not a data repository.** JoinData is a *platform for sharing*; it doesn't store farmer data itself.
- **Not a vendor.** Non-profit; farmer-controlled.
- **Not a global cooperative.** Netherlands-only as of source date. "Ultimate ambition is to facilitate secured data sharing on an international level."
- **Not a substitute for Ag Data Transparent certification.** Ag Data Transparent is contract-transparency certification; JoinData is farmer-controlled data sharing infrastructure.

### Critical observation

JoinData is the **most concrete data cooperative example in the field guide** for agrifood. 16,000+ farmer members is a real, substantive deployment. The model — non-profit, farmer-controlled, fee-funded, multi-stakeholder founding coalition — is *replicable in countries with cooperative cultural traditions* (per the case study's framing).

Worth knowing: **farmer-controlled data sharing infrastructure works in a cultural context that supports cooperation.** This is a real, substantive observation worth surfacing in any talk about data cooperatives.

## Why it matters for talks

- 16,000+ farmer members is concrete deployed evidence of farmer-controlled data infrastructure.
- The cooperative cultural fit matters — Netherlands has deep cooperative traditions; not every country has that.
- The Dutch agricultural data infrastructure is the most mature in the world per multiple sources.
- The "first agricultural data cooperative" framing is substantive — the model is replicable, not unique.

## Critical context

- JoinData is Netherlands-only as of source date. International expansion is an ambition, not an achievement.
- The €50/year farmer membership fee is the operational funding model. Worth understanding: vendor cooperatives vs. farmer cooperatives have different funding mechanics.
- The 16,000 farmer members figure is from Feb 2023. Worth verifying — the number is likely higher in 2026.
- JoinData addresses *control* (who sees the data) more than *transparency* (contract disclosure). Distinct from Ag Data Transparent.
- The Dutch context (cooperative cultural tradition, FrieslandCampina scale, Rabobank presence) is structurally important.
