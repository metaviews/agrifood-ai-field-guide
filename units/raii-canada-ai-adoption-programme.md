## Content

The **Regional Artificial Intelligence Initiative (RAII)** is the federal AI SME productization + AI adoption programme delivered by Canada's **seven regional development agencies (RDAs)**. Budget 2024 allocated **$200M over 5 years** across the seven RDAs. The *AI for All* national strategy (June 4 2026) scales RAII to **$500M** ("a $500-million national investment to expand and enhance the Regional Artificial Intelligence Initiative, delivered by Canada's regional development agencies").

**Two RAII streams:**

1. **AI productization and commercialization** — Helps AI start-ups and growing firms scale up and address common challenges on their journey from lab prototypes to real-world applications: securing growth capital, hiring the right people, managing data, navigating regulatory frameworks.
2. **Adoption of AI applications across critical sectors** — Addresses the AI adoption lag among Canadian businesses; targets SME skills gaps, AI integration complexities, and compliance with regulatory and industry standards.

**Priority sectors named** by ISED: agriculture, healthcare, clean technology, manufacturing, and other sectors of regional importance.

**The only publicly disclosed regional allocation is PrairiesCan's $33.8M.** Open Canada Grants portal enumerates recipients across regions but does not disclose by-region totals for non-Prairie RDAs. G-317.

**Named RAII agrifood-adjacent recipients** (verified from Open Canada Grants portal and RDA announcement pages):

- **Food and Beverage Atlantic Association Inc.** (Moncton, NB) — RAII-funded (ACOA, March 2026 announcement).
- Multiple RAII-funded projects in Atlantic Canada (per ACOA March 2026 announcement).
- **Cogent Real-Time Systems Inc.** (Markham, ON) — RAII $1,040,000, April 1 (per Open Canada Grants portal).
- Multiple CanNor projects including:
  - **Prosper NWT** (Northwest Territories) — AI-Driven Entrepreneurship and Business Support Centre, IDEANorth $2.316M / 3 years.
  - **Inuvik Tech Society** (Gwich'in Settlement Region and Inuvialuit Settlement Region, NT) — Digital Literacy, Online Safety, and AI Training Program, IDEANorth $200K / 2 years.
  - **Nunavut Economic Developers Association** (Nunavut) — Empowering Inuit-led Economic Growth Through AI and Innovation, REGI – AI $200K / 3 years.
  - **DeltaVue Inc.** (Whitehorse, Yukon) — AI platform for Arctic sensor, REGI – AI $99,999 / 1 year.

The CanNor February 6 2026 announcement totals **$2.816M across 4 projects** spanning Yukon, Northwest Territories, and Nunavut. The Nunavut Economic Developers Association project is the first documented Indigenous-led AI deployment with federal funding in primary sources — an AI/ML-powered grant and proposal writing tool designed to enable Nunavut communities to independently draft applications aligned with their unique priorities and cultural context.

**The CanNor Inuvik Tech Society project** explicitly notes the recipient region "only recently gained reliable, high-speed connectivity" — confirming the binding constraint documented in the constraint/critical scout §2: rural connectivity is the precondition for any Northern AI deployment.

## The RAII / Universal Broadband Fund tension

RAII is the federal AI SME funding layer that scales ($200M → $500M). The **Universal Broadband Fund (UBF)** is the rural connectivity layer that does *not* renew. Per the constraint/critical scout §2.2:

- The Wire Report (industry source, 27 May 2026) reports: "Ottawa will not renew its multi-billion dollar funding program designed to connect every household to high-speed internet by 2030."
- ISED confirmed (5 June 2026) it will not raise the 50/10 Mbps minimum standard.
- Auditor General of Canada baseline (2021, still the reference): 90.9% national coverage, but only **42.9% on First Nations reserves** and 59.5% in rural/remote.

**For rural AI adoption to work, both RAII (AI funding) and UBF (rural connectivity) must scale together.** RAII's scale-up is good news for agrifood AI in regional Canada; UBF's non-renewal is the binding constraint. The two federal layers are out of phase: AI funding scales, connectivity funding contracts.

This is the **regional-delivery layer of the federal funder/convenor substrate** for Canadian agrifood AI. It anchors:

- The constraint/critical scout's UBF / rural connectivity / Indigenous data sovereignty framing.

## Why it matters for talks

1. **RAII is a concrete federal AI programme with named recipients across all 7 RDA regions.** A talk can name RAII as the federal AI SME funding stream that *delivers regionally*, with named recipients (Food and Beverage Atlantic Association; Cogent Real-Time Systems; CanNor's Prosper NWT / Inuvik Tech Society / Nunavut Economic Developers Association / DeltaVue).
2. **The $200M → $500M scaling in *AI for All* (June 2026) is a substantive federal commitment.** Worth naming as a *scale-up* signal that contrasts with the UBF non-renewal.
3. **The CanNor February 2026 cycle is the first documented Northern Canadian AI funding cycle with named recipients in primary sources.** Worth carrying into talks on Northern / Indigenous-led AI.
4. **The Nunavut Economic Developers Association project is the first documented Indigenous-led AI deployment with federal funding.** Substantively distinct from the existing Indigenous data sovereignty framework work (OCAP + CARE + IEEE 2890) because it is *operational*, not framework-only.
5. **The RAII / UBF tension is the binding constraint dynamic for rural AI adoption.** Talks can use this contrast.

## Critical context

- **RAII funding shares by RDA (other than PrairiesCan's $33.8M) are not publicly disclosed.** G-317. The Open Canada Grants portal enumerates recipients but not by-region totals.
- **No CAAIN project is also a RAII project** in the data surfaced. CAAIN is ISED + AAFC; RAII is ISED-delivered-via-RDAs. The two channels are distinct federal delivery mechanisms.
- **The Nunavut Economic Developers Association project is Inuit-led but not specifically agrifood-AI.** It's an economic-development-AI tool. The "first Indigenous-led AI deployment with federal funding" framing is correct but the agrifood adjacency is structural (community-level economic development in a jurisdiction where the economy is heavily food-and-fisheries-based) rather than direct.
- **The CanNor Inuvik Tech Society project's "only recently gained reliable, high-speed connectivity" language is a primary-source confirmation** of the binding rural-connectivity constraint. Important for any talk that cites rural connectivity as a constraint.
- **The "AI for All" $500M scaling figure is forward-looking.** RAII currently operates under the $200M envelope; the $500M scaling is a *strategy commitment*, not yet a fully-disbursed figure. Worth tracking.
- **Atlantic / Quebec / Ontario / BC / Northern RDAs may have agrifood-relevant RAII projects not surfaced in scouting.** Worth a follow-up read of the RDA-specific announcement pages and Open Canada Grants portal.
