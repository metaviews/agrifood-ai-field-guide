## Content

This unit maps the actor-network around Hervé Pillaud's distinctive French cooperative / commons framing of agritech AI. The ecosystem has three concentric layers.

### Layer 1 — Direct collaborators (co-founders and project leads)

- **Hervé Pillaud** — farmer-livestock producer Vendée 40 years; author *Agronumericus* (France Agricole, 2015); co-founder La Ferme Digitale 2016.
- **Justine Lipuma** — co-founder La Ferme Digitale; CEO Mycophyto (soil-microbiome start-up). On the partnership announcement: "we can identify scientific bottlenecks faster, develop collaborative projects and joint events, accelerate the transfer of knowledge into concrete applications."
- **David Joulin** — co-founder La Ferme Digitale; coordinator of GAIA hackathons. On the second hackathon (February 2025, 90 participants, 8 multidisciplinary teams): "It's fascinating to see people from livestock farming, industry, cooperatives and student backgrounds work together and design concrete projects."

### Layer 2 — Institutional partners

- **INRAE** — France's national research institute for agriculture, food, environment. April 2025 four-year partnership agreement with La Ferme Digitale. INRAE's *Can AI make agriculture more sustainable?* dossier (7-part) includes the Pillaud interview as substantive content.
- **ACTA** — French agricultural technical institutes network. GAIA project partner.
- **OSFARM** — GAIA project partner.
- **BPI France** — French public investment bank. Funds Naïo Technologies.
- **Region Occitanie** — regional public investor. Funds Naïo Technologies.
- **France 2030** — national investment plan. Funds Naïo Technologies.

### Layer 3 — Industry / vendor counterparts within the French ecosystem

- **Naïo Technologies** — French agricultural robotics (Toulouse); four robot models; deployed with French wine brands (Hennessy, Grands Chais de France); publicly funded for structural reasons aligned with Pillaud's funding-gap argument. See `naio-technologies.md`.
- **Mistral AI** — French LLM. No explicit agrifood deployment surfaced, but the European LLM alternative is part of the structural ecosystem Pillaud's framing depends on.
- **World FIRA** — Toulouse-based agricultural robotics event. Hosts the European agricultural robotics conversation.
- **MyEasyFarm** — La Ferme Digitale member.
- **Mycophyto** — Lipuma's start-up; soil microbiome.
- **Atelier Paysan / Fabriek Paysanne** — French / Belgian co-designed farm tooling cooperatives (referenced in regional scan; consistent with cooperative / commons framing).

### Layer 4 — The framework positions that hold the ecosystem together

Three substantive positions from Pillaud that animate the network:

1. **"Unicorns do not exist in agriculture. The French agri-tech industry is not doing well, and funding from private investors remains insufficient."** — The funding-gap framing. Distinctive from IPES-Food's corporate-concentration framing; identifies *financing model* failure rather than *corporate capture*.

2. **"Using open source and developing digital commons are the obvious solution — and something farmers, who invented the mutual model and cooperation, know how to do."** — The cooperative / commons response. Draws on French cooperative / mutual-model tradition (Crédit Agricole, wine cooperatives, agricultural mutual insurance).

3. **"Move from a culture of proof to a culture of probability, while reducing the margin of error to almost zero. Farmers tolerate very little margin of error."** — The epistemological framing. Names a real shift agriculture's adoption of AI requires.

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

Anchors a *fifth deployment-model pattern* not previously populated in the field guide: **cooperative / commons industry-association**. Distinct from:
- Vendor-led deployment (Deere, Bayer, Loblaw, Croptimistic).
- Independent platform (xFarm, Agrosmart, Taranis).
- Equipment-maker interoperability (AGCO, CLAAS).
- Vertical integration (JD Farm, Blue River).
- State-affiliated (Alibaba ET Agricultural Brain, Shengmu, China Action Plan).

The French cooperative / commons pattern is *industry-association-coordinated* with *open-source / digital-commons* design philosophy. The institutional structure is built on the cooperative / mutual-model tradition that is specifically French / continental European.

## Why it matters for talks

- The Pillaud / La Ferme Digitale / GAIA ecosystem gives European-internal talks a *cooperative alternative* to vendor-led deployment. Worth naming alongside US lock-in patterns and Chinese state-affiliated patterns.
- The funding-gap framing is the strongest articulation of *why agritech is structurally different from other AI sectors*. Useful for any talk that needs to explain why agricultural AI doesn't follow general AI venture patterns.
- The cooperative / commons tradition is genuinely French — it draws on actual French institutional history (Crédit Agricole, wine cooperatives, mutual insurance). Not abstract; structural.
- The GAIA project's open-source-core / open-data framing is structurally different from the data-flow-to-vendor pattern documented in Bayer Climate FieldView and Loblaw PC Express units. Worth naming as a contrast.

## Critical context

- The "unicorns do not exist in agriculture" framing is structural critique of the *financing model*, not critique of any specific company. Applies to the whole sector.
- The cooperative / commons framing is internally consistent with the French cooperative tradition but is *not* the dominant European agritech pattern. Most EU agritech follows vendor-led patterns (xFarm, etc.). The French ecosystem has both.
- Mistral AI's lack of explicit agrifood deployment is a real gap. The ecosystem's LLM infrastructure exists but the agrifood-specific application is not yet visible.
- The 4-year INRAE / La Ferme Digitale partnership (April 2025) is recent; outcomes not yet measurable.
- World FIRA is ecosystem-adjacent (Toulouse robotics) but not formally part of La Ferme Digitale.
