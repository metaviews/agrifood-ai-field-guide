## Content

China Shengmu Organic Milk — a major Chinese organic dairy producer with COFCO affiliation — has deployed AI-driven "Digital Identity" infrastructure for individual cows, alongside a "Smart Pasture" platform integrating sensors, satellite remote sensing, and data analytics. The platform is described in Shengmu's own corporate reporting and in the Capitals Coalition pilot application report (2023).

The deployment supports pasture management, animal health monitoring, and traceability for high-end desert-region organic dairy. It operates at industrial scale in Inner Mongolia and adjacent regions.

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

Anchors the *animal production × sensors-and-IoT+ML × input-reduction* cell with a Chinese industrial-scale example. Distinguishes from the JD Digits livestock work (which is pig-focused) and from the European dairy automation pattern (Lely, etc.) which is more capital-mature but smaller-scale.

## Critical context

- Shengmu is COFCO-affiliated (state-affiliated). The deployment is part of China's broader smart-pasture and smart-fisheries agenda under the National Smart Agriculture Action Plan. Useful as a concrete instance of *state-affiliated industrial-scale agrifood AI* — different from both the vendor-led (JD, Alibaba) and the farmer-led (IPES-Food documented alternatives) patterns.
- The Capitals Coalition report (2023) frames Shengmu's deployment through *natural capital accounting* — i.e. AI is being used not only for productivity but for ecosystem-service measurement. This is a different framing from the dominant "AI for yield" framing and worth surfacing.
- AI-driven individual-animal identification (the "Digital Identity" framing) raises livestock traceability and surveillance questions. Not sovereignty-flagged in the strict IDSov sense (Shengmu operates within Chinese state governance, not Indigenous data sovereignty), but adjacent to the *right-to-repair* / *data ownership* conversation in a different register.
