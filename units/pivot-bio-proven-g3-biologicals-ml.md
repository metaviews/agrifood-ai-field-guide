## Content

Pivot Bio is one of the world's leading innovative agtech companies, headquartered in St Louis, Missouri, with roots at UC Berkeley and Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory. The company uses machine-learning discovery methods combined with proprietary gene-editing technology to identify and deploy microbial strains that fix atmospheric nitrogen for major row crops — displacing a portion of synthetic nitrogen fertiliser use.

The framing Pivot Bio uses is distinct from Indigo Ag's biologicals story (per `units/indigo-ag.md`). Where Indigo's microbes were non-gene-edited and operated in a venture-funded boom-bust trajectory, Pivot Bio's microbes are **patented gene-edited strains** with **peer-reviewed validation** and **multi-year university partnership work**. Pivot Bio's posture is closer to a science-backed biotech than to a venture-funded platform play.

### PROVEN G3 — the third-generation product (March 2025 announcement; 2026 commercial)

Per the March 13, 2025 Pivot Bio press release:
- **PROVEN G3 is the third-generation nitrogen solution for corn**, commercially available in 2026 pending state registrations.
- **Three modes of action**: (1) boosting crop growth by mobilising nutrients (iron, manganese); (2) feeding nitrogen directly to plant roots daily via the patented gene-edited microbe; (3) amplifying yield potential via greater plant health and nitrogen-use efficiency.
- **Replaces ~34 pounds of nitrogen per acre** (per Farm Progress 2026, citing Pivot Bio — vendor-reported).
- **Largest farmer demo in Pivot Bio's history**: 300+ locations demonstrating PROVEN G3 performance and consistency at scale.
- **30% price reduction** for the 2026 growing season (Pivot Bio product lineup announcement, per Farm Progress 2026).

The previous-generation product **PROVEN 40** has been used by growers on **millions of acres** (Pivot Bio, vendor-reported; specific acreage not enumerated).

### Cotton — CERT-N (2025 launch)

Pivot Bio also launched **CERT-N** for cotton (first for the cotton market) in late 2025. This is the first microbial nitrogen product Pivot Bio has shipped for cotton specifically.

### Peer-reviewed scientific validation (the structural differentiator)

The peer-reviewed validation is what separates Pivot Bio's posture from the venture-funded biologicals pattern:
- **University of Illinois three-year study** (Agronomy Journal 2024, doi:10.1002/agj2.21729): validates Pivot Bio products as a reliable third source of nitrogen for corn, demonstrating crops absorb more nitrogen — including nitrogen derived from the atmosphere — even in the presence of synthetic fertilisers.
- **Wisconsin and Purdue research** published in *Scientific Reports* (Nature portfolio; 2024, doi:10.1038/s41598-024-78243-3): confirms that Pivot Bio's gene-edited microbes successfully fix nitrogen from the air and deliver it directly to plant roots, **even in the presence of synthetic fertilisers**.
- **Pivot Bio R&D with notable colleges and universities**: ongoing peer-reviewed work.

The Scientific Reports paper is the load-bearing academic anchor for the claim that gene-edited microbial nitrogen is viable in real-world field conditions — including high-synthetic-nitrogen contexts where traditional microbes cease fixing nitrogen.

### Why gene-editing matters (vendor framing)

Pivot Bio's structural pitch, paraphrased: traditional or "native," non-gene-edited microbes stop fixing nitrogen when other synthetic nitrogen sources are present. This limits their effectiveness in real-world field conditions where synthetic fertiliser is the dominant nitrogen source. Gene-edited strains continue fixing during key growth stages, even with synthetic fertiliser present. This is a substantive scientific framing — not a marketing differentiator in isolation.

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

Anchors the **biotech-vendor × ML-discovery × input-reduction** cell of the matrix — substantively different from Indigo Ag's venture-funded biologicals trajectory and from Bayer / Corteva's synthetic-input-vendor platforms. Pivot Bio's posture is:
- **ML-discovery** rather than digital-platform — the AI is in the discovery pipeline (microbial strain identification), not in the farmer-facing operational tool.
- **Gene-edited biological** rather than synthetic chemistry — the product is a living organism, not a chemistry or a digital platform.
- **Peer-reviewed validation** rather than press-cycle hype — the Scientific Reports 2024 and U Illinois 2024 papers provide independent academic confirmation of the underlying technology.

The peer-reviewed posture matters because it provides an unusual degree of independent validation for an agritech vendor — more rigorous than the typical vendor-reported figures cited elsewhere in the field guide (Deere See & Spray 50% reduction, FieldView deployment scale, Taranis 20M acres, xFarm 600K farms — all vendor-reported, none independently verified at scale).

## Critical context

- **The 34-pound nitrogen replacement figure is vendor-reported.** The underlying peer-reviewed work supports the gene-edited-microbe architecture in field conditions; the specific per-acre replacement figure should be cited with the verification gap flagged.
- **"Millions of acres" is the Pivot Bio framing for PROVEN 40 cumulative deployment.** Specific acreage is not disclosed publicly at the same granularity as Deere See & Spray.
- **State registrations are pending for PROVEN G3** as of mid-2026. Commercial availability varies by state.
- **Pivot Bio is US-centric with planned Brazil expansion.** Brazil's regulatory pathway for gene-edited biologicals differs from US; deployment trajectory in Brazil is a future-cycle item.
- **Pivot Bio competes with synthetic nitrogen fertiliser at the price point.** The 30% price cut for 2026 is a strategic move — likely reflects increased competition from other biological-nitrogen entrants and a broader trend toward price-competitive biologicals.
- **The broader US biologicals + ML cluster includes Sound Agriculture, Anuvia, and others.** Sound Agriculture's Source product uses similar ML-discovery-plus-microbial architecture. Pivot Bio is the most-substantive deployment but not the only one.
