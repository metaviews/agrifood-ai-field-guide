## Content

Naïo Technologies, Toulouse-based French agricultural robotics company founded 2012 (per COO Sarlandie, WGCIT 2022) / 2014 (per Naïo's own "10th anniversary" framing in 2022), is the most-cited French agricultural robotics vendor. Four robot models in production, 60,000+ operational hours cumulative, 300+ robots deployed worldwide as of 2022, 80+ employees globally, 40+ certified dealers across 20+ countries.

**Robot line-up:**
- **OZ** — market gardening and specialty crops (soil preparation, seeding, weeding).
- **TED** — autonomous straddler robot for vineyards (inter-row maintenance, under-vine weeding, canopy management). Deployed with major French wine brands including Hennessy and Grands Chais de France.
- **JO** — narrow vineyards, nurseries, berries.
- **ORIO** — versatile tool carrier for vegetable crops and tree nurseries.

**Concrete deployed evidence:**
- Belgian vineyard operator Nicolas Fratantuono reports "2% of vines damaged with JO, compared to over 10% with tractor drivers." Specific operational comparison — robotic weeding outperforms conventional tractor work on vineyard damage metrics.
- TED deployed in French wine industry with named brands (Hennessy, Grands Chais de France).
- US distribution through John Deere dealer network — small-firm-via-large-firm distribution pattern.

**Augmented Autonomy.** Recent Naïo feature enabling fleet operation without in-field supervision while meeting safety and regulatory standards. Distinct from "supervised autonomy" pattern — unsupervised operation is what makes scaling viable.

**Funding.** BPI France (public investment bank), Mirova (asset manager), Region Occitanie, with France 2030 funding visible on Naïo's site.

## What this unit is doing in the taxonomy

Anchors the *robotics-and-autonomy-ground* cell with French / European / specialty-crop focus. Distinct from:
- **Lely / DeLaval** — dairy-only robotics.
- **John Deere See & Spray** — broadacre row-crop equipment-integrated.
- **JD pig farming (China)** — livestock AI.
- **Naïo is the small-farm / specialty-crop / vineyard pattern.** High-value crops + labour-intensive work + high unit cost justifies the capital investment.

Also notable: Naïo distributes through John Deere in the US. This is a *small-firm-via-large-firm* distribution pattern — the small robotics firm gets access to John Deere's dealer network; John Deere gets a robotics product line without building one. Worth tracking structurally.

## Why it matters for talks

- Naïo is the clearest European agricultural robotics example outside the equipment-maker incumbents. Worth naming in any talk about European agritech.
- The high-value-crop + robotics + labour-cost structure is a *deployment pattern* that travels — vineyard robotics in California, vegetable crop robotics in Canada, similar patterns in Australian horticulture.
- The 2% vs 10% vineyard damage comparison is concrete deployed evidence that robotic weeding can outperform conventional methods on specific metrics.
- "Augmented Autonomy" — unsupervised operation — is the scaling lever. Distinct from autonomous-only-in-pilots framing.

## Critical context

- Founded 2012 vs 2014 — sources differ. Naïo's own "10 years" framing in 2022 implies 2012 founding. COO Sarlandie's 2022 WGCIT interview references "launched in France in 2012."
- 300+ robots and 60,000+ hours are 2022 figures. More recent figures not surfaced; should be re-verified annually per freshness model.
- The 2% vs 10% vineyard damage comparison is single-customer / single-vineyard. Independent verification at scale not surfaced.
- France 2030 funding and BPI investment are public funding — agricultural robotics at this scale requires public capital because private capital underfunds the sector (per Pillaud framing, see la-ferme-digitale-gaia.md).
- Naïo's distribution through John Deere in the US is a notable structural pattern: the equipment-maker incumbent and the small-firm robotics vendor coexisting rather than competing. Worth watching whether this holds.
