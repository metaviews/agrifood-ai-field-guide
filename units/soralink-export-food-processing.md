## Content

SoraLINK is an AI predictive maintenance platform for food and beverage production. The vendor's industry page documents deployment across the three largest Quebec-headquartered food processors: Saputo (dairy, largest Canadian), Olymel (pork and poultry, largest Canadian), Agropur (dairy cooperative, largest Canadian).

**SoraLINK's deployment model:**
- AI trained on food and beverage production cycles
- Handles seasonal variability automatically (notable for food processing where cycles are not year-round stable)
- Designed for rotating machinery (compressors, conveyor drives, fan motors, sortation gearboxes)
- Industrial scale, deployed across multiple facilities
- Vendor claim: $250,000 saved in a single intervention

**Why this cell of the matrix is important.** The field guide's processing cell has been largely under-populated. Most units are on-farm (production) or distribution / retail. The processing cell is where a large fraction of value capture happens in the food system, and where the export-oriented food industry's AI is most likely to be deployed.

**The Quebec food processing industry context.** Saputo operates in the US, Australia, Argentina, and the UK. Olymel is the largest pork and poultry processor in Canada with significant US export activity. Agropur is the largest dairy cooperative in Canada. AI deployment at this scale has implications for food system AI globally — not just Canada.

**Why SoraLINK's deployment is a Canadian-substantive unit.** SoraLINK is a Canadian vendor (Quebec-based, per the industry page) serving Canadian food processors. The deployment is *inside Canadian operations*, not imported US technology. Worth distinguishing from US food processing AI (Shelf Engine, Afresh — see `canadian-food-waste-ai-landscape.md`).

## Why it matters for talks

- The $250,000 saved in a single intervention figure is concrete deployed evidence of AI value in food processing.
- The Saputo / Olymel / Agropur deployment is *real* — these are among Canada's largest food companies, with international operations.
- "Handles seasonal variability automatically" is a substantively interesting claim — many AI systems fail in food processing because the production cycle isn't stable year-round (cheese vs. fluid milk, harvest vs. off-season).
- Quebec's export orientation means the AI deployment has implications for international food system operations, not just domestic.

## Critical context

- The $250,000 saved in a single intervention is vendor-reported. Worth verifying.
- The SoraLINK industry page says "and more" after the three named processors — actual deployment scale not enumerated.
- Predictive maintenance is the AI application — *not* product quality, not supply chain, not consumer-facing. Worth understanding the scope of the deployment.
- Saputo, Olymel, Agropur have not publicly confirmed the SoraLINK deployment in their own communications. The vendor's industry page is the source. Worth flagging for talks.
- The 130+ Quebec agtech businesses integrating AI (per Montréal International) suggests SoraLINK is one of many; the field guide records the named deployment.
