## Quote / Position

> "Europe can lead in advanced AI on its own terms. EUROPA will build a frontier European AI model in all 24 EU languages, showing that we can match the best while staying true to our values. This is about strengthening Europe's ability to shape AI's future with openness, trust and strategic autonomy at its core."

## Context

Virkkunen's framing is the load-bearing EU institutional position linking open-source AI to *European sovereign capability*. Three structurally important moves:

1. **Openness + trust + strategic autonomy as a triad.** The framing bundles open-source with values-aligned governance, distinct from US-anchored permissive regime and China-anchored state-aligned release. The openness is *means*, the autonomy is *end*.
2. **24 EU languages as substantive scope.** The EUROPA consortium (Domyn-led) is covering all 24 official EU languages — including the low-resource languages (Maltese, Irish, Latvian, Lithuanian, Estonian, Slovenian, Slovak) that no commercial frontier model has covered at frontier quality. The substantive observation: language coverage is a public-good expression of open-source.
3. **"On its own terms" / "match the best."** Virkkunen's framing claims parity ("match the best") and distinctness ("on its own terms") simultaneously. Mozilla 2026's 3.3% capability gap + 89% East Asia adoption rate suggests parity is plausible for a 400B+ parameter multi-lingual model; "on its own terms" is the sovereign framing.

This entry complements Mozilla's CTO framing (mozilla-krikorian-open-source-turning-point.md) and the Open Source Initiative's definitional framing (osi-maffulli-binary-threshold-open-source-ai.md) — together they triangulate the industrial-software / community-definitional / public-funding axis on open-source AI in agrifood talks.
